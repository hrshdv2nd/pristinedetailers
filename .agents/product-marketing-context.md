## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Car owners who care about their vehicle can't easily find reliable, high-quality detailing that comes to them — they're forced to choose between convenient but generic washes or high-quality but inconvenient workshops.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Fixed studios: High quality but require drop-off/pick-up, often in industrial areas, time-consuming
- Generic mobile washes: Convenient but no paint knowledge, no coatings, risk of scratching
- Dealership packages: Overpriced, cookie-cutter, technicians aren't paint specialists
- DIY: Risks introducing swirl marks, incorrect products, missed areas

**What it costs them (before finding Pristine):**
- Stone chips and swirl marks that devalue the car at trade-in
- Hours wasted on workshop drop-offs or dealing with subpar results
- Mental overhead of managing maintenance on a car they've invested $50K–$200K+ in

**Emotional tension:**
- "I've spent a lot on this car and I don't want to ruin the paint"
- "I don't know who to trust — every detailer claims to be the best"
- "I keep meaning to book a detail but I never get around to it"

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** Fixed-location detailing studios (IDA-certified, high quality) — fall short because customers must drop off the car, inconvenient for professionals, often located in industrial suburbs

**Secondary:** Generic mobile wash services (Squeaky Clean, franchise washers) — fall short because no coating expertise, risk of scratching, not suitable for paint-conscious owners

**Indirect:** Dealership/OEM service packages — fall short because overpriced, non-specialist technicians, no long-term relationship or maintenance care plans

**Status quo competitor:** Doing nothing (the most common) — paint degradation happens slowly so customers don't act until damage is visible
