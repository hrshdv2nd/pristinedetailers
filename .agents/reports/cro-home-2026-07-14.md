## Issue 1 — `ServicesPreview` heading contradicts the visible content

**Element:** `ServicesPreview` → `<h2>` at line 238–241  
**Current copy:** `"Three pillars of obsession."`

**Problem:**  
The heading claims three pillars. The grid renders five cards: Maintenance Detail, Basic Detailing, Ceramic Coating, PPF, and Window Tinting. A visitor who reads the heading and then counts the cards will notice the mismatch — and on a premium page selling paint protection to car enthusiasts who care about detail, this reads as sloppy.

This isn't a visual design issue. The `pd-four-col` grid has `gridTemplateColumns: 'repeat(5, 1fr)'` (line 247) and explicitly loops over 5 services. The copy was simply never updated when the fifth card was added.

The ICP for ceramic coating and PPF is sceptical by nature: "I don't know who to trust with a car like this." Copy that doesn't survive a 5-second count damages that trust before the visitor reaches the flagship section.

**Recommendation:**  
Option A (copy change): Update the heading to `"Five services. One standard."` — accurate, still voice-appropriate, takes 30 seconds.  
Option B (structural change): Reframe around a genuine three-pillar logic (Protection, Maintenance, Enhancement) and group the five services under those pillars. Higher effort but creates a cleaner narrative path toward the flagship ceramic section below.

## Issue 2 — `PPFSection` ends $2,900–$7,900 pricing with no CTA

**Element:** `PPFSection` → pricing list at lines 681–699, end of section at line 742  
**Missing:** No booking button, no quote link, no phone number anywhere in the section

**Problem:**  
PPF is the highest-ticket service on the page: Partial Front at $2,900, Full Front at $4,200, Full Vehicle at $7,900. The section runs for ~100 lines of rendered content — a strong two-column layout with good imagery, a clear self-heal proof card, and three priced packages. Then it ends. Nothing.

A visitor who reads through the PPF pricing and is ready to enquire must now scroll past the entire membership teaser, the gallery, three testimonials, and the FAQ before reaching the next booking link in `CTASection` (line 1107). That is four sections and several hundred pixels of content designed to redirect their attention before they can act.

This is where the highest-value conversion intent peaks and the page has no net to catch it.

**Recommendation:**  
Add a CTA after the pricing list at line 698. Minimum viable fix:

```tsx
<a
  href="https://pristinedetailers.setmore.com/..."
  style={{ /* match existing ghost-dark button style */ }}
>
  Get a PPF quote <Arrow />
</a>
```

Alternatively, for a $4,200+ service, a "Call us" link with a phone number is appropriate — high-ticket buyers often want to talk before booking. Either is better than the current nothing.

## Issue 3 — `FlagshipSection` buries a disqualifying condition mid-paragraph (ESCALATE)

**Element:** `FlagshipSection` → `<p>` at line 358–360  
**Current copy:** `"A nano-ceramic barrier fused to your paint. Hydrophobic, UV-stable, lasting up to 5 years. Applied by certified technicians in a controlled, dust-free environment. Brand new vehicles only."`

**Problem:**  
"Brand new vehicles only." is a hard disqualifier — anyone with a car that isn't brand new cannot book this service as described. It's buried as the final sentence of a four-sentence paragraph, after the pricing card, after the "Most loved" badge, and after visual imagery designed to build purchase intent.

A visitor who owns a two-year-old BMW M3 (squarely the ICP) will read all of this, feel the pull of the ceramic coating section, click "Get a quote," start a Setmore booking — and then be turned away or need to be contacted to clarify. That is a failed conversion and a wasted admin interaction.

There are two separate problems here:
1. **The buried qualifier** damages trust when buyers find out late they're ineligible.
2. **The implied scope is misleading**: Ceramic coating on pre-owned or older vehicles typically requires paint correction first. The page should either: (a) clearly name what "brand new vehicles only" means for everyone else, or (b) link to the Revitalise Package as the path for older vehicles.

**Recommendation:**  
Surface the condition immediately and pair it with a redirect:

> "For brand-new vehicles. Existing paint? Start with our [Revitalise Package →] to restore your clear coat first."

This preserves the conversion for both segments — new car owners book ceramic, pre-owned car owners get routed to the next service down rather than bouncing.

## Quick Win — Hero subhead rewrite (flagged June 16, still not shipped)

**Element:** `HeroSection` → `<p>` at lines 90–92

**Before:**
> "Obsessive-grade detailing - from monthly maintenance details to elite paint protection services."

**After:**
> "Mobile detailing at your driveway, office car park, or garage — ceramic coating, PPF, and paint correction by certified Melbourne technicians."

**Why this converts better:**
- "Mobile" appears in the first word — confirms message match for Google traffic landing on "mobile car detailing Melbourne"
- Names three high-value services by their actual names, not "elite paint protection services"
- "Certified Melbourne technicians" directly addresses the #1 objection to mobile services (quality vs. fixed studio) and adds local signal
- Removes "obsessive-grade" self-congratulation with no proof attached

This was raised June 16. It has not shipped. It is the highest-ROI change on the page — 15 minutes of implementation, visible on every hero impression.

## Bigger Bet — Test car-make trust badges directly under hero CTAs

**Hypothesis:**  
The ICP's primary anxiety when landing cold is: "Will they know how to handle my car?" The current hero resolves this zero times above the fold. Social proof (reviews, testimonials, car names) lives at the bottom of an 11-section page.

Moving a car-make badge row directly below the hero CTA buttons — immediately after the "Book a Detail" / "Explore services" pair at lines 93–127 — should reduce the trust gap at the moment of decision and increase CTA click-through for the ICP (owners of European/luxury vehicles, the $30K+ car segment).

**Control (current):**  
Hero ends with two CTA buttons. Visitor must scroll to `TestimonialsSection` to see car makes mentioned.

**Variant:**  
Add directly below the CTA buttons:

```
Also trusted with:  [Porsche]  [Ferrari]  [Range Rover]  [Tesla]  [BMW]  [McLaren]
```

Six monochrome car wordmarks or names in pill chips — same visual language as the suburb pills in `MelbourneSection`. No new content required; these makes are already referenced in testimonials and the business context.

**What to measure:**
- Primary: Click-through on "Book a Detail" CTA (hero, tracked separately from the `CTASection` CTA)
- Secondary: Scroll depth past hero (does trust at fold reduce drop-off?)
- Segment: Filter results to sessions where device is mobile — this ICP skews mobile from local search

**Why this is a bet, not a certainty:**  
Car-make social proof may read as name-dropping rather than earned trust if the logos are unrecognised by the visitor. The variant should only run if the business can honestly name ≥3 of these makes as repeat customers — which the testimonials confirm. Test for 4–6 weeks, ≥500 sessions per variant minimum.
