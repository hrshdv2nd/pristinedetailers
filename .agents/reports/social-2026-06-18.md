Melbourne's winter looks harmless for cars. It isn't.

Here's what's actually happening to your vehicle's paint between now and August: the Eastern Freeway, the Westgate, and the Monash are at their worst for stone chips when wet — road debris follows traffic spray directly into your front bumper and bonnet at 100km/h. Meanwhile, the plane trees lining St Kilda Road and Toorak Road are depositing sap onto every car parked in the inner east for more than a few hours. Add Port Phillip Bay salt air for anyone in Brighton, Bayside, or Sandringham, and you have three separate contamination sources running simultaneously on your vehicle from June through August.
