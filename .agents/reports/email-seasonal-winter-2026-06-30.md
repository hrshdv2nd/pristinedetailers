Subject: Winter is doing things to your car you can't see yet

Hi [First name],

Winter in Melbourne is quiet and mostly harmless-looking. The roads are wet, the air is cold, and your car sits in the driveway looking fine.

It's not fine.

Here's what's actually happening right now:

**The Bayside roads.** If you live or commute near the coast — Brighton, St Kilda, Hampton, Sandringham, Frankston — the council uses a brine pre-treatment on roads before cold snaps. That salt spray gets kicked up by every car ahead of you and lands on your lower panels, your wheel arches, your sills. It sits in crevices. Salt and paint chemistry don't get along.

**Condensation.** Every morning your car drops in temperature overnight, then warms up as the day comes in. That cycle causes moisture to form under rubber seals, in paint micro-scratches, and on ceramic-coated surfaces that haven't been maintained. On an unprotected car, that moisture doesn't bead off — it sits.

**Your interior.** Wet jackets over seats. Fogged windows wiped with hands. Damp boots on floor mats. Six weeks of this and you've got the start of mould in the carpet backing and door card crevices — the kind that smells in November when you turn the heater on for the first time in spring.

None of this is dramatic. None of it looks bad yet. That's the problem.
