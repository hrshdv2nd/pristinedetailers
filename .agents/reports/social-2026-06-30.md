## Platform: LinkedIn

**Caption:**

A two-stage paint correction isn't a luxury service for show cars. On a daily-driver like the 2022 Audi A5 we worked on in South Yarra last week, it was damage control.

Three years of drive-through washing had induced heavy swirl marking across every horizontal panel. The rotary brushes at most automated washes drag fine grit across the clear coat on every pass — imperceptible individually, compounding into visible degradation over a few years. Add bird drop acid etching on the bonnet and the net result is a $90K car that looks a decade old under direct light. The owner had assumed it was unavoidable wear.

It's not. A two-stage machine polish — cutting compound followed by a finishing refinement — removed approximately 90% of the visible defects. A 5-year ceramic coating was then applied in the building's car park. The owner didn't rearrange a single workday. The finish came out measurably better than factory delivery condition.

For business owners or professional services firms with company vehicles in Melbourne's inner suburbs: this outcome is repeatable. Drive-through washing is the single most controllable source of paint damage on a business fleet. One paint correction and a ceramic coating costs a fraction of the depreciation difference at a 3-year lease return. If that's a conversation worth having, DM or connect below.
