# Growth Report — Referral Program
**Date:** 2026-05-06  
**Focus:** Week 1 — Referral Program  
**Author:** Morgan, Growth & Referrals Manager

---

## Situation Assessment

Pristine Detailers has no documented referral mechanic. This is a significant gap because every completed job is a walking billboard — a freshly ceramic-coated BMW in Toorak or a spotless Porsche in Brighton is inherently shareable. The question isn't *whether* customers talk about it. It's whether we capture that conversation and turn it into a booking.

**Why referrals work especially well here:**
- The result is visible (the car is the ad)
- The ICP socialises in tight, high-income peer groups (car clubs, golf clubs, office buildings)
- Trust is the primary purchase barrier — a mate's recommendation removes it instantly
- CAC from referrals will be a fraction of paid ads; referred customers also churn less

**Program type:** B2C double-sided customer referral (not affiliate — this is word-of-mouth, not content creator)

---

## Recommended Referral Offer

### The Mechanic: "Mate's Rate Wash"

> **Refer a mate. They book any Pristine service. You both get a free maintenance wash.**

**Why this works:**

| Element | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Double-sided reward | Both parties win — higher share rate and conversion |
| Maintenance wash as currency | Aspirationally valuable (~$70–90 value), operationally low-cost, and it's exactly what members get — a taste of the subscription |
| No minimum spend required | Removes friction for the referred friend to book |
| Tied to membership conversion | After their free wash, the upsell to membership is one sentence: "This is what members get every month" |

**Reward triggers:**
- Referrer reward unlocks when the referred friend **completes** their first booking (not just signs up — prevents fake referrals)
- Referred friend's free wash is applied automatically to their first booking
- Reward is a credit, not cash — keeps it in the Pristine ecosystem

**Variation for members referring to membership:**  
If a referrer is already a member and their referred friend **joins as a member**, upgrade the reward:

> **Member referral bonus: Both get a free maintenance wash + referrer gets $50 credit on their next invoice.**

This makes membership referrals feel elite — members recruiting members.

## The Growth Loop

```
Great Job Completed
        ↓
Car looks immaculate — customer is delighted
        ↓
Natural conversation: "Where'd you get that done?"
        ↓
Post-service SMS arrives with referral link (timing: 2–4 hrs post-job)
        ↓
Customer shares link — friend books any service
        ↓
Friend experiences service quality
        ↓
At end of friend's first booking: "This is what members get monthly."
        ↓
Friend becomes a member
        ↓
Member refers their network (car clubs, colleagues, neighbours)
        ↓
Brand ambassador: tagged in their Instagram post, mentioned in their car club chat
        ↓
Loop repeats at scale
```

**The key insight:** The trigger isn't a marketing email — it's the moment someone sees the car and asks a question. Our job is to arm the customer with a frictionless way to share *before* that moment happens.

---

## Message 1: Post-Service SMS

**Timing:** Send 2–4 hours after job completion (car is fresh, customer is still riding the high)  
**Channel:** SMS (primary) / push notification if app exists  
**Character target:** Under 160 characters for SMS; below is a slightly longer version for email delivery

---

**SMS version (160 chars):**

> Your [Model] is looking sharp. Know anyone who'd want the same? Share your link — you both get a free wash when they book. [LINK]

---

**Email version (same moment, slightly expanded):**

**Subject:** Your car's looking good — here's something for the mate who asks

> Hey [First Name],
>
> Hope you're happy with how the [Model] came up today.
>
> If someone notices and asks where you got it done, send them this link. They'll get a free maintenance wash on their first booking — and so will you.
>
> [Referral link button: "Share Your Link"]
>
> No forms, no fine print. They book, you both get a wash. That's it.
>
> — The Pristine team

---

**Notes on this message:**
- Opens with the car, not the offer — stays in the customer's world
- "Someone notices" frames it as inevitable, not aspirational
- The offer is explained in one sentence
- No call to action to become a member — that happens on the referred friend's side after their service

## Message 2: Existing Member Email

**Timing:** Monthly — include in member newsletter or send as a standalone in weeks where no other member comms are scheduled  
**Channel:** Email  
**Audience:** Active members on a monthly plan

---

**Subject:** You already get the best deal. Here's how to share it.

> Hey [First Name],
>
> You're on a membership, which means your car gets looked after on a schedule — not when you remember to book.
>
> We don't run paid ads. We grow through members like you telling their network. So here's the deal:
>
> **Refer someone to Pristine. They book any service. You both get a free maintenance wash.**
>
> If they join as a member, you get the wash *plus* $50 off your next invoice.
>
> Your referral link: [LINK]
>
> Most of our members drive in the same suburbs, park in the same buildings, and know people with the same cars. One conversation is worth more than a billboard.
>
> — Morgan, Pristine Detailers

---

**Notes on this message:**
- Opens by validating the member's smart choice (they already belong)
- "We don't run paid ads" is a trust signal — positions referral as authentic, not desperate
- The tiered reward is clearly stated: standard vs. member referral
- The last paragraph is a subtle social proof + permission to talk about Pristine — "one conversation is worth more than a billboard" gives them a frame to share without feeling like a salesperson

## Implementation Checklist

### Phase 1 — Minimum viable (launch within 2 weeks)
- [ ] Set up referral tracking: use a simple tool (ReferralHero, Tolt, or even a Typeform + manual tracking to start)
- [ ] Generate unique referral links per customer (tied to their email/booking record)
- [ ] Add referral SMS to the post-service workflow (trigger: booking status = completed)
- [ ] Add "free maintenance wash" as a redeemable credit in the booking system
- [ ] Brief technicians: they should verbally mention the referral at handover ("we've got a refer-a-mate thing — check your phone")

### Phase 2 — Member referral tier (launch with next member newsletter)
- [ ] Segment members vs. non-members in CRM/email tool
- [ ] Create member-specific referral links with higher reward tier
- [ ] Add referral section to the membership confirmation email
- [ ] Track: referred friend → member conversion rate (this is the key metric)

### Phase 3 — Ambassador layer (60–90 days out)
- [ ] Identify top referrers (anyone who has referred 3+ bookings)
- [ ] Invite them into an informal "Pristine Crew" — early access, name recognition, exclusive offers
- [ ] Create a leaderboard or simple thank-you recognition (a handwritten note + complimentary add-on goes a long way with this demographic)

## Morgan's Note

The most important thing we can do right now is make the ask at the right moment. A customer who just watched their Porsche go from oxidised to ceramic-coated is not going to say no to sharing a link. We just need to be there — in their phone, with a frictionless link, within hours of the job finishing.

The referral program doesn't need to be complex to work. It needs to be *present* at the exact moment the customer feels the most pride. That's the job.

Everything else — tiers, ambassador programs, leaderboards — layers on top once the basic loop is working.
