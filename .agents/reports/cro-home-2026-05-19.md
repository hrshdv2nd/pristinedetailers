# CRO Audit — Homepage
**Date:** 2026-05-19  
**Auditor:** Riley (CRO Analyst)  
**File:** `components/pages/home.tsx`  
**Cycle:** Week 1 of 4

---

## Context

Page serves cold traffic from local search ("mobile car detailing Melbourne", "ceramic coating Melbourne") and direct. Primary goal: book a detail via Setmore. Secondary goal: capture membership intent. Visitors skew mobile, time-poor, premium-vehicle owners.

---

## Issue 1 — Hero sub-copy buries the #1 differentiator

**Element:** `HeroSection` > `<p>` (line 90–91)  
**Problem:** The hero headline — *"Where your car meets its best self."* — is aspirational but says nothing unique. Any detailing company in Australia could run it. The decisive UVP (we come to you, mobile) doesn't appear anywhere above the fold. It's first mentioned in `MelbourneSection` at line 527–528, which is the fifth section down the page. A visitor who lands from "mobile car detailing Melbourne" has to scroll past three full sections before seeing the word "mobile."

The sub-copy at line 90–91 reads: *"Obsessive-grade detailing - from monthly maintenance details to elite paint protection services."* This is a breadth statement, not a positioning statement. It also uses an inconsistent dash (hyphen, not em dash) and reads like a category description, not a reason to stay on the page.

**Recommendation:** Rewrite the sub-copy to lead with the mobile model. Optionally test replacing the h1 headline entirely (see Bigger Bet below). The sub-copy change is low-risk and can ship this week.

---

## Issue 2 — Review count conflict destroys trust at the exact moment it should build it

**Element:** `HeroSection` star badge (line 69) vs `TestimonialsSection` headline (line 940)  
**Problem:** The hero badge reads `★ 4.9 / 30 reviews`. The testimonials section reads `4.9 stars across 39 reviews`. That's a 9-review discrepancy — visible to anyone who scrolls two minutes into the page. A visitor evaluating a premium $750–$7,900 service will notice. It reads as either outdated copy or, worse, fabricated numbers. Neither reading is acceptable for a brand positioning on expertise and trust.

**Recommendation:** Reconcile to one figure immediately. If Google shows 39, update the hero badge to `★ 4.9 / 39 reviews` (line 69). Treat this as a single source of truth and review quarterly. Consider linking the badge to the `/about/reviews` page.

---

## Issue 3 — "Three pillars" heading renders four service cards

**Element:** `ServicesPreview` > `<h2>` (lines 229–231)  
**Problem:** The section headline is *"Three pillars of obsession."* The component maps over four service objects (lines 217–221): Maintenance Detail, Vehicle Detailing, Ceramic Coating, Paint Protection Film. Four cards render. The factual error is visible at a glance and makes the page look unproofed — exactly the wrong signal for a brand selling precision craftsmanship to Porsche owners.

**Recommendation:** Either (a) change the headline to reflect four services, or (b) promote Ceramic Coating as the one flagship and dedicate the services grid to three supporting services. Option (b) has better narrative logic since `FlagshipSection` already exists to highlight Ceramic below.

Suggested copy: *"Every car. Every need."* or *"Four services. One standard."*

---

## Quick Win — Hero sub-copy

**Before (line 90–91):**
> Obsessive-grade detailing - from monthly maintenance details to elite paint protection services.

**After:**
> We come to you. Certified technicians, studio-grade results — at your driveway, office, or garage across Melbourne.

This lands the mobile differentiator in the hero, uses concrete language the ICP cares about (certified, studio-grade), and anchors the geographic context (Melbourne) in the same sentence. Change is six words longer but removes the vague spectrum framing in favour of a direct value statement.

---

## Bigger Bet — A/B test the hero headline

**Hypothesis:** The current headline (*"Where your car meets its best self."*) prioritises brand personality over clarity. For cold organic traffic landing from local search intent, a direct UVP headline will convert better — even if it scores lower in a "which do you prefer?" survey.

**Control (line 82–89):**
> Where your car<br>meets its best<br>self.

**Variant:**
> Professional detailing.<br>We come to<br>you.

**Secondary variant (more specific):**
> Your car detailed<br>at your door.<br>Melbourne-wide.

**What to measure:** Setmore booking link clicks (primary CTA) within the session. Use a 50/50 split. Run for 3–4 weeks minimum given current traffic volume.

**Rationale:** The current headline has strong aesthetic fit with the design system but relies on visitors already knowing what Pristine Detailers does. Most won't. The variant sacrifices one line of brand poetry for a clear, searchable, scannable statement that a time-poor professional processes in under two seconds.

- **Line 997 vs 845:** FAQ says membership includes *"10% off all our other services"* but `MembershipTeaser` says *"15% off coatings."* These are different figures and different scope. Pick one and use it everywhere.
- **Line 890:** Gallery "View gallery" link points to `href="#"` — dead link. Route to `/gallery`.
- **Line 131:** Hero stat reads `5yr` with label `Ceramic Coatings` — this is ambiguous. Does it mean the warranty is 5 years, or the coating lasts 5 years? The `FlagshipSection` copy says "lasting up to 5 years." Change the stat label to `Ceramic warranty` or `up to 5yr protection` to be explicit.
