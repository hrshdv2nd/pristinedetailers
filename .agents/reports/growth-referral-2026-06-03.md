## The Growth Loop

```
Great detailing job → car looks incredible → someone notices in Toorak traffic, 
a Brighton car park, or a Mornington Peninsula weekend → they ask → referral → 
new customer → member → they refer others
```

The key insight: Pristine Detailers has a **visible product**. A freshly ceramic-coated BMW in South Yarra is a moving billboard. The referral program's job is to make it frictionless to capture that moment when someone asks "where did you get that done?"

---

## 1. Current Referral Mechanic Assessment

**Status: None identified.**

Based on the current business context, there is no formalised referral program in place. This is a significant gap given:

- **High NPS potential** — mobile premium services with certified technicians generate strong word-of-mouth naturally
- **Existing membership base** — recurring members are the highest-intent advocates; they've already committed financially
- **Social visibility** — ceramic coating and PPF results are physically obvious on the road
- **Affluent, tight-knit demographics** — Toorak, Brighton, and Hawthorn customers move in similar social and professional circles; a recommendation from a peer carries significant weight

**What currently happens after a job?**  
Likely a generic thank-you message (if anything). The post-service window — when the customer is most emotionally invested in how their car looks — is going unused as a referral trigger.

**Estimated referral rate today:** <2% (industry baseline for service businesses with no program)  
**Target referral rate with program:** 8–15% (achievable for premium service with double-sided offer)

## 2. Referral Program Recommendation

### The Offer: "Bring Someone Into the Club"

**Double-sided, membership-anchored reward:**

> **Referrer gets:** 1 month of membership free (credited to their next billing cycle)  
> **Referred friend gets:** First month of membership at 50% off

**Why this structure:**

1. **Ties referrals to membership growth** — the most valuable outcome for LTV and retention
2. **Both sides win immediately** — no "earn points toward a reward" ambiguity
3. **Feels premium, not cheap** — a free month of membership is tangible and recurring, not a discount code
4. **Creates urgency without pressure** — the referred friend gets a real incentive to try the membership, not just book a one-off
5. **Self-reinforcing loop** — new members who joined via referral refer at 2–3x the rate of organic customers

### Naming the Program

Avoid "referral program" language in customer-facing copy — it sounds transactional.

**Recommended framing:** *"The Pristine Circle"* or simply *"Bring a Mate"*

Keep it informal and premium. Not a loyalty scheme — an invitation.

### Referral Mechanic (How It Works)

1. After every completed service, customer receives a unique referral link via SMS/email
2. Link goes to a dedicated landing page: `pristinedetailers.com.au/refer`
3. Referred friend books and joins membership → referrer credited automatically
4. Referrer notified: *"Your free month is applied. [Name]'s car is in good hands."*

**Tracking tool recommendation:** Mention Me (enterprise referral, supports service businesses) or Dub.co for link tracking + a simple CRM tag for manual credit if budget is tight at launch.

### Fraud Prevention (Simple Rules)
- Referral must convert to a paid membership booking (not just a one-time service)
- Self-referral blocked by email verification
- One credit per unique referred customer

## 3. Message 1 — Post-Service SMS + Email

*Trigger: 24 hours after service completion. The car still looks immaculate. The customer has already shown it to someone.*

---

### SMS Version (≤160 characters)

```
Your car looks the part. When someone asks — share this:
pristinedetailers.com.au/refer/[code]
They get 50% off their first month. You get one month free. – Pristine
```

*(Personalise [code] with customer name or unique token. Keep it short — no explanation needed at this moment.)*

---

### Email Version

**Subject line (A/B test these):**
- Option A: `Your car looks great. Here's what to do when someone asks.`
- Option B: `Someone's going to ask about your car this week.`
- Option C: `Your referral link — one month free when a mate joins`

---

**Email body:**

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Your [vehicle make] is done, and it shows.
>
> When someone notices — in the car park, at work, on the weekend — and asks where you got it done, here's your link:
>
> **[Unique referral link]**
>
> They get their first month of Pristine membership at 50% off.  
> You get one month free, credited to your next billing cycle.
>
> No forms. No voucher codes. They click your link, they book — you're sorted.
>
> The link's yours to keep. Share it once or a dozen times.
>
> — The Pristine Team

---

**Notes on this message:**
- Opens with the car, not the offer — matches brand voice (expert, not salesy)
- "Someone's going to notice" is a confidence statement, not a pitch
- Mechanics explained in 3 lines — no walls of text
- "The link's yours to keep" removes scarcity anxiety and encourages multiple shares
- No emoji, no exclamation marks — consistent with premium tone

## 4. Message 2 — Existing Member Referral Nudge

*Trigger: Monthly send to the active membership base. Best sent mid-month (not at billing time). Framed as an invitation, not a campaign.*

---

### SMS Version

```
Quick one — if you know someone with a car that deserves better care, 
your referral link is: [link]
They get half off their first month. You get one free. Cheers, Pristine
```

---

### Email Version

**Subject lines (A/B test):**
- Option A: `Do you know someone who'd be a good fit?`
- Option B: `A favour for your network (and a free month for you)`
- Option C: `Most of our best members came from someone like you`

---

**Email body:**

> Hi [First Name],
>
> We don't run ads. Most of our members found us because someone they trust told them about us.
>
> If you know someone in Melbourne who cares about their car — and hasn't found a detailer who matches — here's your link:
>
> **[Unique referral link]**
>
> When they join as a member:  
> → They get their first month at 50% off  
> → You get one month credited, no strings
>
> The kind of person who'd appreciate this: owns something they're proud of, hates wasting weekends driving to a workshop, wants the same standard every time.
>
> If that sounds like someone you know — send it through.
>
> — The Pristine Team
>
> *P.S. Your link works for multiple referrals. Each one earns you another free month.*

---

**Notes on this message:**
- Opens with social proof ("we don't run ads") — builds trust and flatters the member as a valued source
- ICP description in the P.S. section helps members self-qualify who to refer — reduces low-quality referrals
- "Someone you trust told them" positions the member as a trusted authority, not a salesperson
- P.S. clarifies the link is reusable — this is the #1 question members have and answering it preemptively increases share rate
- Tone: peer-to-peer, not company-to-customer

## The Loop in Full

```
Service complete 
  → SMS/email trigger fires (24hrs) 
    → customer shares link 
      → friend books + joins membership 
        → referrer gets free month 
          → new member delighted → refers their own network 
            → Pristine grows without paid acquisition
```

The compounding effect: if 10% of members refer one person per quarter, and those referred members also refer at 10%, the membership base grows geometrically without a single paid impression.
