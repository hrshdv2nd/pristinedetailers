## Strategy Overview

These four emails cover the complete post-acquisition customer lifecycle for Pristine Detailers. Each email has one job. Together, they move a one-time customer toward loyalty, advocacy, and membership.

**The loop:**  
Great service → Review → Referral → Re-engage → Membership → Brand ambassador

**Audience:** Melbourne professionals, 28–55, driving $30K+ vehicles. Time-poor, quality-focused, suspicious of salesy language. Speak like a trusted expert, not a marketer.

## Email 1 — Post-Booking Confirmation

```
Sequence:    Post-booking (transactional)
Trigger:     Booking confirmed
Send timing: Immediately on confirmation
Goal:        Set expectations, reduce anxiety, prevent no-shows
Exit:        Service completed
```

**Subject:** Your detail is booked — here's what to expect  
**Preview:** [Tech name] will be with you on [date]. A couple of things to have ready.

---

**Body:**

Hi [First name],

Your booking is confirmed. Here are the details:

**Service:** [Service name]  
**Date:** [Date]  
**Time:** [Time window]  
**Location:** [Address]  
**Estimated duration:** [X hours]

One of our certified technicians will come to you — no need to go anywhere or leave your car somewhere.

**To make the most of your appointment:**

- Park where there's room to work around both sides of the car
- A covered or shaded spot is ideal for paint correction and ceramic work, but not required — we'll work with what's there
- We bring everything: water, power, products, and equipment

For ceramic coatings and paint correction, we'll need uninterrupted access for the full duration. You're welcome to leave us to it.

Your technician will send a message when they're 20 minutes out.

If anything changes before then — reply here or call us on [phone].

[Tech name]  
Pristine Detailers

## Email 2 — Post-Service Follow-Up

```
Sequence:    Post-service lifecycle
Trigger:     Service marked complete in booking system
Send timing: 20–24 hours after completion
Goal:        Aftercare guidance + Google review ask + soft referral nudge
Exit:        Review left OR referral link clicked
```

**Subject:** How's [the BMW / your car] looking?  
**Preview:** A few things to know in the next 48 hours — and one small favour.

---

**Body:**

Hi [First name],

[Tech name] here. Thanks for having me out yesterday — [the car] is looking exactly how it should.

**A few things to keep it that way:**

*[Conditional block — ceramic coating:]*  
For the next 7 days, try to keep the car dry where you can. No car washes, no prolonged rain exposure. The ceramic coating is curing — give it that window and it'll bond properly. After that, you're protected for years.

*[Conditional block — paint correction / full detail:]*  
Avoid automated car washes — the brushes and chemicals are what causes the damage we just corrected. A hand wash every 3–4 weeks keeps the paint doing what it should.

*[Conditional block — interior detail:]*  
Leather conditioning takes 24–48 hours to fully absorb. Try to avoid heavy use of the seats today if you can. After that, you're good.

*[Conditional block — maintenance wash:]*  
You're all set. If the car has a ceramic coating, the hydrophobic effect will be working at full strength again — notice the water beading next time it rains.

---

**One favour:**

If the result was what you were after, a quick Google review means a lot. It helps other Melbourne car owners find us and know what to expect.

Takes about 90 seconds: [Leave a review →]

---

**And if you know someone whose car needs this:**

We don't advertise heavily — most of our bookings come from recommendations. If you've got a friend, colleague, or neighbour with a car they care about, tell them to mention your name when they book. We'll look after them properly.

Talk soon,  
[Tech name]  
Pristine Detailers  
[phone number]

---

**CTA (primary):** [Leave a Google review →] → Google Business profile  
**CTA (secondary):** Referral mention (no link needed — "mention your name" is intentionally low-friction)  
**Length:** ~220 words  
**Notes:** Segment the aftercare block by service type — this is the most important personalisation point. The referral ask is buried intentionally: review first, referral second. Don't flip the order.

## Email 3 — 30-Day Dormant Win-Back

```
Sequence:    Re-engagement
Trigger:     No booking activity for 30 days after last service
Send timing: Day 30 of inactivity
Goal:        Re-engage, book next service, or convert to maintenance member
Exit:        Booking made OR unsubscribe
```

**Subject:** It's been a month — Melbourne hasn't been kind to your paint  
**Preview:** Bird droppings can etch clear coat in under 24 hours. Here's where things stand.

---

**Body:**

Now is a better time than three months from now.

Book this week and we'll include a complimentary paint inspection — we'll tell you exactly where things stand, no pressure, no upsell unless it's warranted.

[Book a service →]

[First name] at Pristine Detailers

**CTA:** [Book a service →] → /booking

## Growth Loop Notes

The loop this sequence is designed to close:

1. **Service → Email 1** sets quality expectations upfront (fewer disappointed customers, better reviews)
2. **Service → Email 2** converts satisfied customers into public advocates (Google reviews) and low-friction referrers
3. **Inactivity → Email 3** re-activates customers before they mentally churn — framed as expert advice, not a promo
4. **2nd booking → Email 4** catches customers at peak trust and converts them to predictable recurring revenue (membership)

The referral nudge in Email 2 is the bridge to next month's focus (Week A: Referral Program). Every customer who refers is a potential entry point for the full membership funnel.
