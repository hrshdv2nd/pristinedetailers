**Seasonal context:** Mid-June in Melbourne. Bird dropping etching is the most acute current pain point. H4 ("Bird Drops Bead. Don't Etch.") is seasonally apt — prioritise testing it in position 2 across June–July.

**Ad schedule:** Weight towards weekday evenings (6–9pm AEDT) and Saturday mornings — when car owners are home, notice their vehicle, and have mental bandwidth to book. Reduce bids during business hours Monday–Friday. In winter, Saturday morning intent typically drops slightly vs. summer; monitor and adjust if Saturday spend isn't converting.

**Device:** Mobile-first — confirm `/booking` loads under 2 seconds on 4G. Premium buyers in Toorak and Brighton are often booking from their phone in the driveway, not at a desktop.

## Melbourne Context Notes

**Why bird drops are the right seasonal hook:** Acidic bird droppings (pH 3.5–4.5) etch clear coat within hours on a cold Melbourne winter morning if a car sits parked under a tree. Inner-east plane trees and Bayside palms/she-oaks are roosting corridors. A ceramic coating's hydrophobic surface means droppings bead and are less likely to dwell — a technically accurate claim that resonates with any Toorak resident who has lost clear coat to a myna bird.

**The mobile angle is the structural differentiator:** Every fixed-studio competitor in Melbourne requires the customer to drive to Cremorne, Moorabbin, or Dandenong. For a Toorak or Brighton car owner, that's 30–45 minutes each way plus two trips (drop-off and collection). Our model removes both trips. H2 ("We Come to Your Driveway") should always be in rotation — it's the sentence competitors cannot match.

**8-year framing:** The ceramics we apply range from 3-year entry-level to 8-year premium-grade. Advertising "8 years" sets the ceiling of the range, which attracts buyers willing to invest more. This is intentional — the conversation at booking naturally moves toward the right product tier for the vehicle and use case.
