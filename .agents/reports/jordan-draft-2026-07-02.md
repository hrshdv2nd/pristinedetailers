## Article Body (Markdown)

In Melbourne, winter road grime accumulates 3–4× faster than summer because wet roads kick up mineral deposits that bond to clear coat within 48 hours.

That's not an estimate. It's what happens when cold, wet bitumen combines with Melbourne's mix of construction dust, tyre rubber, brake particles, and salt air from Port Phillip Bay. By the time the grime is visible on your paint, it's already started the bonding process.

Most Melbourne car owners assume winter is low-risk — no sun, so UV isn't a problem. That's right about UV, and wrong about everything else.

## What Wet Roads Do to Your Clear Coat

Melbourne's winter roads carry a film of water mixed with mineral runoff, construction dust, and brake pad deposits. At freeway speeds — or even 60 km/h on a suburban arterial — that contaminated water hits your lower panels under pressure.

Clear coat at cold temperatures is less flexible than in summer. Mineral particles don't just sit on the surface; they work into the clear coat texture and begin oxidising. Within 48 hours, they've started forming a chemical bond. After two weeks without a wash, removing them properly requires an iron fallout decontamination treatment, not a standard shampoo.

If your car has a ceramic coating, the coating handles most of this. If it doesn't, the clear coat bears the load alone. At 50–100 microns thick, it doesn't have unlimited capacity.

## Melbourne Hazards by Area

**Bayside and Brighton:** Salt air from Port Phillip Bay combines with winter condensation on paint. Vehicles parked near the bay overnight accumulate salt deposits that accelerate oxidation. Monthly decontamination washes aren't optional here — they're maintenance.

**Inner East (Toorak, Hawthorn, South Yarra):** Dense bird populations mean frequent droppings. In cold weather these sit wet for hours. Bird dropping acid reaches pH 3.5 — low enough to etch clear coat in under a day.

**CBD and Southbank:** Underground car parks generate fine concrete dust that settles on paint across an eight-hour day of parking. Over a Melbourne winter, this abrasive buildup accumulates on bonnets and rooflines.

## Practical Winter Maintenance Schedule

**Every 2–3 weeks:** Wash with a pH-neutral shampoo. Avoid drive-through auto-washes — rotating brushes introduce swirl marks that show clearly when spring sun returns.

**Monthly:** Chemical decontamination to remove bonded iron fallout, particularly around wheel arches and lower panels. Bayside residents should add a clay bar pass every two months for salt removal.

**Immediately:** Remove bird droppings the moment you spot them. Cold, wet conditions mean the acid stays active. Don't wait for the next scheduled wash.

**Now:** If your paint is unprotected, a polymer sealant provides 3–6 months of chemical resistance going into the worst winter months. If you've been considering ceramic coating, winter is an underrated time to get it done — you go into spring already protected rather than booking in October when demand peaks.

## FAQ

**Should I get ceramic coating in winter?**

Yes. We work in sheltered setups regardless of weather, and the 7-day cure period is the same year-round. The practical benefit: you go into spring — pollen, tree sap, UV return — already protected.

**Does rain damage car paint?**

Rain itself doesn't. What damages paint is the mineral fallout, road acid, and contamination that rain carries and leaves behind on the surface. A ceramic-coated car sheds that contaminated water. An unprotected car holds it until it bonds.

**How often should I wash my car in Melbourne winter?**

Every 2–4 weeks for most inner suburbs. Every 2 weeks if you're near the bay or regularly park under trees. If you're seeing a grey film after a week, that grime is already beginning to bond — every extra day makes it harder to remove cleanly.
