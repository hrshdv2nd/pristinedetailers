## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Problem-Agitate-Solve (Angle: Maintenance keeps slipping — and so does your paint)

**Primary text (112 chars):**
> You keep meaning to book a detail. Then life happens. Six months later, the swirls are visible. There's a plan for that.

**Headline (27 chars):**
> Car Care On Autopilot. Done.

**Description (26 chars):**
> From $99/mo. We come to you.

**Why this works:** "You keep meaning to book" is verbatim customer language from the product marketing context — this audience recognises themselves immediately. The sentence structure mirrors their internal monologue, not a pitch. "Then life happens" validates the pattern without judgment. "Six months later, the swirls are visible" is the consequence — specific enough to be credible (swirls are the real outcome of skipped detailing, not just a dirty car), visual enough to create a moment of recognition. "There's a plan for that" introduces the membership as a category solution, not a product — it lets curiosity pull the reader to the landing page rather than pushing a feature list. No exclamation marks. No "amazing results."

**Note on character count:** Primary text is 112 chars. Within the 125-char visible threshold for feed — full hook reads before the "See more" truncation.

### Variant 2 — Identity / Aspiration (Angle: Your car lives in Toorak. It should look like it.)

**Primary text (119 chars):**
> Your Range Rover parks in Toorak, drives the Eastern, sits outside your office on Collins St. Monthly care — from $99.

**Headline (25 chars):**
> Mobile Detail Membership

**Description (24 chars):**
> We come to you. Book now.

**Why this works:** Three location-specific touchpoints (Toorak, the Eastern, Collins St) paint the ICP's actual day without stating demographics. This isn't generic — a South Yarra professional reading this sees their exact life. "Monthly care — from $99" closes the copy with price anchoring: for a car that costs $120K to buy and parks in Toorak, $99/mo is a trivially rational number. The headline is plain and functional — "Mobile Detail Membership" — because the primary text has done all the work.

**Vehicle rotation plan:**
- V2a: "Your Range Rover parks in Toorak…" — Range Rover/Land Rover interest group
- V2b: "Your BMW parks in South Yarra, drives the Monash, sits in the underground on St Kilda Rd. Monthly care — from $99." — BMW interest group + South Yarra postcodes
- V2c: "Your Porsche is in your Hawthorn garage four nights a week and on the Eastern the other three. Monthly care — from $99." — Porsche interest + Hawthorn/Kew
- V2d: "Your Tesla parks at Chadstone, Richmond, and your home driveway in a week. Monthly care — from $99." — Tesla/EV interest + Richmond/Malvern postcodes

Each variant names the specific car + freeway + suburb matching the interest and postcode targeting. Same structure, zero generic copy.

### Variant 3 — Social Proof / Value (Angle: Members save 35% — but that's not why they join)

**Primary text (124 chars):**
> "Six months of school-run punishment and it still looks showroom." — Priya S., Range Rover Sport. That's what membership does.

**Headline (27 chars):**
> Signature Plan — $149/mo

**Description (27 chars):**
> Bi-monthly detail. Certified.

**Why this works:** Opens with a real testimonial from the product marketing context — unedited, direct, specific. "School-run punishment" is the domestic equivalent of the freeway chip anxiety: it's the mundane, daily exposure that compounds silently. "Still looks showroom" is the outcome. The structure (testimonial → "that's what membership does") closes the proof loop without explaining anything — the testimonial says it, the product delivers it. Choosing Priya's Range Rover Sport is deliberate: family-oriented premium vehicle owner, Bayside or Inner East suburb — resonates precisely with the female ICP segment that is often underserved by car care advertising. Headline names the specific Signature tier ($149/mo) to drive qualified traffic — people who click know the price point before they land.

**Note on description:** "Bi-monthly detail. Certified." hits 29 characters. Trim to "Bi-monthly detail. Cert." (24 chars) if Meta enforces the 27-char hard cap in this placement.

### Lookalike Audiences

- **Seed 1:** Existing membership subscribers — tightest proxy for the actual buyer; 1% LAL off this seed is the priority
- **Seed 2:** All-time bookers who have completed 3+ services — high-LTV signal even if not yet on a plan; good for upselling into membership
- **Run:** 1% LAL (precision) and 3% LAL (scale) as separate ad sets; 70% budget to 1%, 30% to 3%
- **Exclude:** Active members from all acquisition campaigns — route existing members to the Signature upgrade sequence, not membership acquisition ads

## Retargeting — /booking Page Abandoners (Membership Focus)

For users who visited `/membership` or `/booking` with a membership tier selected but did not complete (window: 7 days):

**Primary text (125 chars):**
> Still thinking about it? Your technician, your suburb, the same time each month. Takes 2 minutes to lock in. Spots fill Monday.

**Headline (22 chars):**
> Lock In Your Schedule

**Description (24 chars):**
> Essential from $99/mo.

**Targeting window:** 7 days post-visit  
**Frequency cap:** 3× per week — membership is a considered purchase; over-serving triggers doubt, not urgency  
**Exclusions:** Suppress anyone who completed a booking post-visit (immediate exclusion on conversion event); also suppress existing members

**Why this works:** "Your technician, your suburb, the same time each month" names the actual product — a relationship, not a transaction. This is the thing the membership sells that a single booking doesn't. "Spots fill Monday" creates soft scarcity without fabricating a countdown — Pristine's schedule genuinely fills during the working week; this is accurate and specific. "Takes 2 minutes" addresses the primary abandonment driver (form friction/decision fatigue). Price named in the description to confirm they're in the right range before clicking back.

## Creative Direction Notes

**Image/video guidance:**
- Hero visual: Certified technician arriving at a Toorak or Brighton residential driveway, setting up equipment — morning light, familiar inner-suburb street context. The "come to you" service should be visible in the scene, not described in copy.
- Membership "routine" video (15–20 sec): Technician arriving, cleaning, leaving — the car in the driveway looking immaculate — cut to a phone notification: "Your next detail is booked." Shows the set-and-forget experience as a lived sequence, not a feature list.
- Before/after consistency: Six panels (monthly snapshots of the same car, same angle, over six months) showing consistent condition despite regular use. Caption: "Same car. Six months of school runs, freeways, and car parks." Works as a carousel.
- Testimonial card: Priya's quote ("Six months of school-run punishment…") over a clean Range Rover Sport in a Bayside driveway — real location context matters. Avoid studio backgrounds.
- Avoid: stock images of smiling people holding car keys; generic "sparkling car" hero shots without Melbourne residential context.

**Format priority for Membership:**
1. Facebook Feed (static + carousel) — primary for prospecting; broader demographic reach, works well with V1 (problem) and V3 (testimonial)
2. Instagram Feed — V2 identity variants; visual context of the suburb + vehicle combination lands harder in a visual-first format
3. Instagram Reels — 15-sec membership routine video; native for process-as-content
4. Stories — retargeting only; single-image booking abandoner copy; swipe-up friction matches the low-friction CTA message

**Copy test plan (weeks 1–2):**
- Run V1 (maintenance slip/PAS) vs V3 (testimonial) as cold audience in both Inner East and Bayside — measure CTR and cost-per-click-to-membership-page
- V2 vehicle variants run per interest group as challenger sets — determine whether named vehicle + suburb outperforms generic copy for each make
- Retargeting budget is separate; exclude from prospecting measurement pool
- Kill lowest-performing cold variant at 1,000 impressions per ad set; consolidate budget to winner; run a fresh creative on the same winning angle in week 3

**Financial year timing note:**  
Late June / early July is a strong Membership conversion window. Melbourne professionals reviewing personal and business spending at financial year-end. A $99–$149/mo plan is an easy rationalise as a vehicle maintenance expense, not a luxury purchase. Lean into the "maintenance plan" framing in V2 variants if budget allows an additional copy test.
