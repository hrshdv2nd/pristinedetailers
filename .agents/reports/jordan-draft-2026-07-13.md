## Victoria's Legal VLT Limits

VLT — Visible Light Transmission — measures how much light passes through your window film. Higher VLT means a lighter tint. Here's what's legal in Victoria:

| Window | Legal Minimum VLT |
|---|---|
| Front windscreen (tint strip only) | 75% VLT; strip must not extend more than 10% down the glass |
| Front side windows (driver + passenger) | 35% VLT minimum |
| Rear side windows | No minimum — any darkness permitted |
| Rear windscreen | No minimum — any darkness permitted |

The front side window rule catches most drivers out. A 35% tint still blocks significant heat and glare — the restriction exists to protect the driver's sightlines at night, not to limit aesthetics. Rear windows and the rear windscreen have no restriction, so you can go as dark as the car's style calls for.

If you're unsure what your factory glass already sits at, we run a VLT meter reading before we start — it takes 30 seconds and confirms your baseline.

## How Mobile Window Tinting Works

1. **Booking** — Reserve your slot online. We confirm the job location, your vehicle, and your preferred tint level.
2. **Pre-clean** — On arrival, we clean every glass surface with isopropyl solution. Residue, old adhesive, or silicone from a previous tint will ruin adhesion — this step isn't optional.
3. **Film cutting** — Film is cut to your exact window dimensions using a precision plotter or hand-cut where curvature demands it. Curved rear windscreens require heat-shrinking the film to shape.
4. **Application** — Film goes on wet, squeegeed smooth, trimmed to final dimensions, and tucked cleanly at corners and door seals.
5. **Cure period** — Standard cure is 3–5 days depending on temperature and humidity. Don't wind down your windows during this time. Initial haze and minor bubbles are normal — they clear as the film bonds to the glass.
6. **Walkthrough** — We walk you through the finished result before we pack up.

A standard four-door sedan takes 2–3 hours end to end. SUVs and wagons with large wrap-around rear glass run 3–4 hours.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does window tinting take?**
A four-door sedan takes 2–3 hours. SUVs and wagons with larger rear glass run 3–4 hours. We'll give you a time estimate at booking based on your specific vehicle.

**Can you tint in an underground car park?**
Yes. We work in underground car parks across Melbourne regularly — Crown Casino, Westfield carparks, apartment towers in South Yarra and Richmond. We use battery-powered tools and portable LED lighting. The main requirement is that the car isn't completely hemmed in on both sides. Let us know your building at booking and we'll confirm access suitability in advance.

**Does window tinting void my car warranty?**
No. Window tinting is a cosmetic modification and cannot void your vehicle warranty under Australian Consumer Law. Manufacturers are not entitled to reject warranty claims on mechanical or drivetrain grounds because of a window film application.

**What VLT is legal in Victoria for front windows?**
Front side windows — driver and passenger — require a minimum of 35% VLT. Rear side windows and the rear windscreen have no legal minimum in Victoria, so there's no restriction on how dark you go. The front windscreen permits a tint strip across the top, provided it covers no more than 10% of the glass area.
