# Meta Ads — Paint Correction
**Date:** 2026-06-14 (Sunday prep — launch Thursday 2026-06-18)
**Week:** 24 — Rotation: Paint Correction (weeks 4 & 8)
**Platforms:** Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed
**Campaign objective:** Lead generation / landing page conversions
**Landing page:** `/booking` (service pre-selected: Paint Correction)
**Target location:** Melbourne, VIC — inner east, Bayside, Mornington Peninsula postcodes

---

## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Problem-Agitate-Solve (Angle: Melbourne's environment is actively damaging your paint)

**Primary text (108 chars):**
> Toorak car parks. St Kilda Rd sap. Monash grit at 100km/h. Your paint is absorbing all of it. We correct it.

**Headline (26 chars):**
> Paint Correction Melbourne

**Description (23 chars):**
> Mobile. We come to you.

**Why this works:** Opens cold with three specific Melbourne damage sources — no preamble, no claim, just the reality the reader already knows. The ICP driving down Toorak Rd every week recognises the plane tree sap problem. "Your paint is absorbing all of it" shifts from observation to a mild threat. The CTA is framed as a resolution, not a pitch. Plane trees on St Kilda Rd, the underground car park at Toorak Village, Monash/M1 grit — all are real, recognisable to the right buyer.

---

### Variant 2 — Before/After (Angle: Paint correction protects resale value)

**Primary text (123 chars):**
> Swirl marks aren't just aesthetic. They cut resale value. One machine polish session removes years of damage — at your driveway.

**Headline (23 chars):**
> Restore. Sell for More.

**Description (22 chars):**
> Book at your driveway.

**Why this works:** Reframes paint correction as a financial decision, not just cosmetic. The Toorak/Bayside buyer cares about resale — these are people trading up to the next BMW or Land Cruiser in 2–3 years. "One session" and "at your driveway" remove the two standard objections (effort and inconvenience) in a single clause. The headline "Restore. Sell for More." is punchy and direct — three words, two ideas, no filler.

---

### Variant 3 — Identity (Angle: A premium car with swirled paint is a contradiction)

**Primary text (120 chars):**
> You bought a Porsche. It shouldn't look like it lived in a car park for three years. Mobile paint correction, Melbourne.

**Headline (25 chars):**
> Your Car Deserves Better.

**Description (27 chars):**
> We bring the studio to you.

**Why this works:** Leads with identity — the reader bought a specific car because of how it makes them feel. Swirl marks break that. The copy doesn't explain the problem; it names the dissonance the reader already feels. "Mobile paint correction, Melbourne" closes with the practical resolution without over-selling. The vehicle reference (Porsche) is interchangeable — in creative, test with BMW, Audi, Mercedes, and Tesla as separate variants for interest-targeted ad sets. Don't use a generic "luxury car" — name the car the audience drives.

**Note on Mornington Peninsula:** Peninsula car owners do the Nepean Hwy run regularly — stone chips and UV exposure on convertibles and weekend cars are primary pain points. Don't lump with inner-east targeting. Run as a separate ad set with V3 creative (identity angle) and swap "car park" reference to "Nepean Hwy gravel" in a tailored version.

### Interests & Behaviours
- Luxury vehicle brands: BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi, Porsche, Tesla, Toyota Land Cruiser (target as separate interest groups — don't combine, measure separately)
- Car care, auto detailing, car valeting, automotive accessories
- Engaged shoppers — home services, on-demand services
- Behavioural proxy for high income: frequent domestic travel, premium retail engagement

### Lookalike Audiences
- **Seed:** Upload existing booking-page converters + any customer email list (aim for 500+ for a quality seed)
- **Run:** 1% LAL (quality) and 3% LAL (scale) as separate ad sets with split budget — 60/40 in favour of 1%
- **Exclude:** Anyone who has already converted (suppress current service customers from acquisition campaigns)
- **Stack with interests:** Overlap 1% LAL with the luxury vehicle interest group for the highest-precision cold audience

## Retargeting — /booking Page Abandoners

For users who visited `/booking` but did not complete a booking (window: 7 days):

**Primary text (98 chars):**
> You looked at paint correction. Good call. We're still taking bookings this week — we come to you.

**Headline (22 chars):**
> Still Taking Bookings.

**Description (27 chars):**
> It takes 2 minutes to book.

**Targeting window:** 7 days post-visit (hot, high-intent)
**Frequency cap:** 3× per week maximum — these are warm leads, not cold audience
**Angle:** Low-friction reactivation. "You looked at paint correction. Good call." validates their interest without pressure. "We're still taking bookings" creates mild urgency without countdown tactics. "It takes 2 minutes to book" directly addresses the friction that stopped them the first time — they may have been interrupted mid-booking flow.

## Creative Direction Notes

**Image/video guidance:**
- Hero visual: a certified technician arriving at a leafy Toorak or Brighton driveway with a European or Japanese prestige vehicle in the foreground — context signals premium, not generic
- Machine polish in action is a high-performing visual: the dual-action polisher, the clear coat transforming in frame — process credibility
- Before/after swipe carousel performs strongly on Instagram Feed for paint correction: half dull/swirled, half corrected gloss — the contrast is the hook
- Avoid studio or white-background shots — mobile, outdoor, residential context converts better for this audience
- For V3 (identity angle): shoot with a recognisable Melbourne vehicle and a recognisable Melbourne driveway — leafy street, heritage facade, recognisable suburb feel

**Format priority:** Instagram Feed > Facebook Feed > Instagram Reels (test in that order for paint correction; Reels for process video if assets available)

**Vehicle-specific creative test:**
Run V3 ("You bought a Porsche...") as three separate versions — Porsche, BMW, and Tesla — each targeted to the corresponding vehicle interest group. Test whether naming the car lifts CTR vs. a generic "premium car" reference. This is the highest-value creative iteration to run in the first two weeks.

**Copy test plan (weeks 1–2):**
- Run V1 (environment/PAS) and V2 (resale/BAB) head-to-head — both are rational; V1 agitates, V2 converts
- V3 (identity) as challenger with vehicle-specific targeting
- Kill the lowest CTR at 1,000+ impressions per variant
- Scale the winner with fresh visual creative on the same copy angle
