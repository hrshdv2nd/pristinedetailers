## Article Body

You've just signed the paperwork on a new BMW or Mercedes. You're sitting across from the finance manager, who slides a laminated sheet across the desk. "Paint protection package," it says. $1,200. Sometimes $1,800. Sometimes more.

Most people say yes. It feels like insurance on a big purchase, and the timing — right when you're already in spending mode — makes it psychologically easy to add on.

Here's what's usually in that package. And it's not what you think.

![Gleaming paint protection on a sports car — this is what professional-grade protection actually looks like](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1503376780353-7e6692767b70?w=1200&q=80)

## What Dealerships Are Actually Selling

The majority of dealership "paint protection" packages fall into two categories: a spray sealant or a low-grade polymer coating applied quickly in the prep bay before delivery.

Neither is ceramic coating. Neither is PPF. Neither is the type of protection you'd get from a certified detailer spending 6–8 hours on your car.

A spray sealant — which is what many of these packages consist of — lasts around three to six months. Some use a thin polymer coating that performs better than wax but nowhere near a proper professional ceramic coating, which chemically bonds to your clear coat and lasts three to eight years.

The difference in materials? Often a $30–$50 product applied in under two hours. The difference in price to you? $1,200 to $2,000.

That margin is funding the finance department's quarterly bonuses.

## Why Timing Is the Bigger Problem

Even if the dealership's product were top-grade ceramic coating — which it usually isn't — there's a fundamental timing issue.

New cars come from the factory with transport film residue, lot fallout, and handling marks. By the time a car sits on a Melbourne dealership lot for weeks (or months), the paint has micro-marring from automatic wash machines, bird droppings, dust contamination, and repeated handling. A proper ceramic coating needs to go onto clean, decontaminated, corrected paint to bond properly.

Dealerships do not polish your car before applying their coating package. They detail it. There's a difference. A detail gets the surface clean. A pre-coating paint correction removes the defects already in the clear coat so the ceramic bonds to a smooth, sealed surface.

If a ceramic coating goes onto contaminated or marred paint, it seals those defects in. Your brand-new car ends up with $1,500 worth of swirl marks trapped permanently under a coating.

![Close-up of professional paint protection application — surface preparation is everything](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1604754742629-3e5728249d73?w=1200&q=80)

## What Professional Protection Actually Involves

A legitimate ceramic coating job from a trained detailer involves several steps the dealership skips entirely.

**Surface decontamination** — chemical treatment to strip bonded fallout, tar, and industrial deposits from the paint before any polishing begins. Iron contamination from Melbourne's rail corridors and traffic is more common than most people realise.

**Paint correction** — machine polishing to remove wash scratches, delivery marks, and any clear coat defects. Even brand-new cars need this. Most new vehicles arrive with soft European clear coat that marks easily in transport and lot prep.

**Coating application in controlled conditions** — ceramic coatings cure through chemical bonding with the clear coat. Temperature, humidity, and surface preparation all affect adhesion quality. A prep bay with a roller door open to the elements isn't controlled conditions.

**Curing time** — most professional ceramic coatings need 12–24 hours before water contact. You don't drive home the same afternoon you picked up the car.

This process takes the better part of a day. The dealership is doing it in under two hours alongside twenty other handover vehicles.

Let's be direct about the numbers.

| Product quality | Consumer-grade sealant or thin polymer | Professional-grade (Gtechniq, IGL, CarPro) |
| Surface prep | Basic wash | Decontamination + paint correction |
| Protection duration | 3–18 months | 3–8 years |
| Application time | 1–2 hours | 6–8 hours |
| Warranty | Usually none | Yes, with certified detailers |

In many cases, you're paying more at the dealership for a product that lasts a fraction of the time.

## What to Do Instead

If you've already paid for the dealership package, it's not a disaster. The sealant will wear off in the first year. At that point, book the car in for proper decontamination, a single-stage correction, and a professional ceramic coating applied to clean, prepared paint.

If you haven't taken delivery yet, decline the package. Tell the finance manager you have a detailer. Then book your car in within the first fortnight of ownership — before Melbourne's UV, bird droppings, and freeway stone chips start working on that fresh clear coat.

New car paint is in its best condition the day you drive it off the lot. The ceramic coating window is open for a short time before contamination and light marring start accumulating. Don't let it close while you're waiting for the right moment.

And don't pay dealership prices for dealership-grade protection.
