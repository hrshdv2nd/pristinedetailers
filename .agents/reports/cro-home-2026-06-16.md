## Audit Summary

The homepage is visually strong. The design does the hard work of establishing premium positioning. The conversion leaks aren't aesthetic — they're copy precision and trust signal consistency. Three issues are actively losing bookings right now.

---

## Issue 1 — Hero H1 buries the #1 differentiator

**Element:** `HeroSection` → `<h1>` (lines 82–89)  
**Current copy:** `"Where your car meets its best self."`

**Problem:**  
The most visited section of the page tells a new visitor nothing about what Pristine Detailers *does* or *how*. The word "mobile" — the brand's single biggest point of difference against fixed-location competitors — does not appear anywhere in the hero fold. It first appears in `MelbourneSection` at line 527, five full sections below the fold.

A visitor arriving from a Google search for "mobile car detailing Melbourne" sees a brand statement, not a confirmation they're in the right place. Cold traffic needs value confirmation before brand personality. Right now the hero inverts that order.

The subhead (lines 90–92) reads: `"Obsessive-grade detailing - from monthly maintenance details to elite paint protection services."` — still no mobile mention, and "obsessive-grade" is the kind of self-description the brand voice doc explicitly warns against.

**Recommendation:**  
Rewrite the hero subhead (lines 90–92) to lead with the mobile model and the service range. Keep the H1 headline for brand — it's fine — but make the subhead do the functional confirmation work. See Quick Win below.

---

## Issue 2 — Review count contradiction between hero and testimonials

**Elements:**  
- `HeroSection` eyebrow pill, line 69: `"★ 4.9 / 30 reviews"`  
- `TestimonialsSection` header, line 940: `"4.9 stars across 39 reviews."`

**Problem:**  
The same page displays two different review counts — 30 and 39 — in the same session, 8 sections apart. A skeptical buyer (and Pristine Detailers' ICP absolutely is skeptical — they're spending $750–$7,900) will notice this and discount every other trust claim on the page.

This isn't a minor inconsistency. Social proof only works when it's unambiguous. Two different numbers create a "which is real?" question the page cannot answer.

**Recommendation:**  
Pick the correct current count and update both instances to match. If 39 is accurate, update line 69 to `"★ 4.9 / 39 reviews"`. Set a process to sync these whenever a review milestone is reached — they should be a single source of truth, ideally pulled from the same constant.

## Issue 3 — Membership pricing contradicts itself in three places on the same page

**Elements:**  
- `MembershipTeaser` eyebrow, line 793: `"Membership · $80/mo"`  
- `MembershipTeaser` feature list, line 845: `"15% off services s"` ← trailing `s` typo, visible to all users  
- `FAQSection` answer, line 997: `"$79/month gets you... 10% off all our other services"`

**Problem:**  
Three separate errors on one page around membership pricing:

1. **Price conflict:** `$80/mo` in the teaser vs. `$79/month` in the FAQ. One of these is wrong.  
2. **Discount conflict:** `15% off coatings` in the teaser vs. `10% off all services` in the FAQ. Different rates and different scope — coatings only vs. all services.  
3. **Copy typo:** `"15% off services s"` is a broken string left in the feature list bullet.

Together, these tell a buyer that Pristine Detailers either doesn't know what their membership costs, or is hiding the real terms. Either kills the conversion to `/membership`. The membership CTA at line 809–825 is asking visitors to click through on the basis of pricing information that contradicts itself.

**Recommendation:**  
Audit the actual membership price and discount rate against whatever is live on `/membership`. Update all homepage instances to match. Fix the `"services s"` typo immediately (line 845) — it takes 30 seconds and is currently visible to every visitor.

## Quick Win — Hero subhead rewrite

**Location:** `HeroSection`, `<p>` at lines 90–92

**Before:**
> "Obsessive-grade detailing - from monthly maintenance details to elite paint protection services."

**After:**
> "Mobile car detailing that comes to your driveway. Ceramic coating, PPF, and paint correction — applied by certified technicians across Melbourne."

**Why this converts better:**  
- Surfaces "mobile" immediately — confirms message match for search traffic  
- Names the three core high-value services (not vague "elite protection")  
- "Certified technicians" addresses the #1 objection to mobile services (quality vs. fixed studio)  
- "across Melbourne" adds local signal without needing to list suburbs  
- Removes "obsessive-grade" self-congratulation in favour of specifics

---

## Bigger Bet — A/B test hero H1 specificity vs. brand statement

**Hypothesis:**  
Cold traffic (Google organic, local search) converts at a higher rate when the H1 communicates the *delivery model* rather than brand voice. The current headline `"Where your car meets its best self."` is excellent brand personality but may be costing clicks from visitors who haven't committed enough attention to read past it.

**Control (current):**  
> "Where your car meets its best self."

**Variant:**  
> "We detail your car at your home. Same results as a studio — without the trip."

**What to measure:**  
- Primary: Click-through on hero "Book a Detail" CTA  
- Secondary: Scroll depth past hero (does specificity reduce bounce?)  
- Guard rail: Time on page (don't accept higher bookings at cost of brand perception)

**Why this is a bet, not a certainty:**  
The design and imagery on this page do heavy brand-positioning work. The headline may be doing emotional priming that produces high-intent bookings even if fewer people click immediately. Don't assume specificity wins — test it over 4–6 weeks with ≥500 sessions per variant before concluding.

## Bonus — Dead link in gallery section

**Element:** `GallerySection`, line 890: `href="#"`

Not a CRO issue in the traditional sense, but every user who clicks "View gallery" after seeing strong before/after imagery hits a no-op (scrolls to top of page). That's lost purchase intent from a high-signal action. Link this to `/gallery` or remove the link until that page exists.
