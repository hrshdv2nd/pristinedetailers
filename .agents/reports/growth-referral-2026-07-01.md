## Current State Assessment

**Existing referral mechanic:** None. Referrals happen organically but there is no structured program, no tracking, no incentive, and no ask.

**Why this is a significant gap:**
Pristine Detailers' ICP — professionals in Toorak, Brighton, Hawthorn — talk to each other. A Porsche GT3 owner at a Sunday Cars & Coffee knows six other people with cars worth detailing. The problem isn't that customers don't recommend us. It's that we never *ask*, never make it easy, and never reward it. That means referrals are random instead of systematic.

**LTV context (why we can afford to invest here):**
- Signature member LTV (2 yr): ~$3,576
- Ceramic + Signature member LTV (2 yr): ~$4,326+
- Even a $150–$200 reward per referred member pays back 15–20× in member revenue alone
- Referred customers typically have 16–25% higher LTV and churn 18–37% less — matching our ICP profile perfectly

## Recommended Referral Offer

### The "Pristine Circle" Referral Program

**Core mechanic (double-sided, membership-anchored):**

> **"Refer a mate. You both get a free Maintenance Detail."**
>
> When a friend you refer joins any Pristine membership (Essential or Signature), you each receive one complimentary Maintenance Detail — valued at $99.

**Why this structure works for Pristine:**

1. **The reward IS the product** — giving a free Maintenance Detail is self-reinforcing. The referred friend experiences the quality, then they refer someone else. One loop completes another.
2. **Anchored to membership, not one-offs** — this drives recurring revenue, not transactional bookings. The referral program is a membership acquisition engine.
3. **Easy to explain in person** — a technician can say it in one sentence while packing up their gear. No codes, no apps, no friction.
4. **Premium-appropriate** — it's not a Kmart voucher. It's a real service experience that reinforces the brand.

**Referral mechanic flow:**
```
Post-service moment → Customer gets referral ask (SMS/email)
→ Customer shares name + number of a friend
→ Friend contacts Pristine, mentions referrer
→ Friend books and joins membership
→ Both receive complimentary Maintenance Detail at next visit
→ Both now have an active relationship with Pristine
→ Both are candidates to refer again
```

**Scale-up tier (activate at 100+ members):**

Once the basic mechanic is running, add a tier for "Pristine Ambassadors" — customers who have referred 3+ members:
- Named in a private "Pristine Circle" group
- Priority scheduling window (first access to new slots)
- Annual complimentary Revitalise Package ($300 value)

This is the brand ambassador tier — the end of the loop.

## Referral Tracking (Simple Start)

No software required to launch. Start with:
- Customer gives their name as the referral source when the friend calls/books
- Log referrals in a shared spreadsheet (Referrer | Referred Friend | Date Joined | Reward Issued)
- Issue free detail at next scheduled visit

At 20+ active referrals per month, migrate to a tool like **Mention Me** (best for service businesses, handles attribution + reward delivery) or use a simple referral link via **Dub.co**.

## Referral Ask Message 1: Post-Service SMS

**Send:** 24 hours after service completion (when the car is still looking its best and the customer is still feeling good about the experience).

**Channel:** SMS

**Tone:** Direct, warm, not salesy. One ask, one action.

---

**SMS (under 160 characters — keep it clean):**

```
Your car's looking sharp, [First Name]. Know someone who'd want the same?
Refer a mate to Pristine — you both get a free Maintenance Detail when
they join a membership. Just reply with their name and number and we'll
take it from there.
```

*(If SMS platform allows longer messages or links, append:)*
```
Or share this link: [referral link]
```

**Why this works:**
- Opens with a compliment that's earned (the car *does* look great — we just did it)
- "Refer a mate" — Melbourne vernacular, not corporate
- The offer is clear in one sentence
- The action is dead simple: reply with a name and number (no link required)
- Sent at peak goodwill — 24h post-service, before the "new normal" sets in

## Referral Ask Message 2: Existing Member Email

**Send:** To all current members in a standalone campaign. Re-send quarterly to non-referrers.

**Channel:** Email

**Tone:** Acknowledges the existing relationship. Frames referral as doing a friend a favour, not selling for us.

---

**Subject line:** Your mates' cars need this too

**Preview text:** One recommendation. Two free details.

---

**Email body:**

---

Hi [First Name],

Six months ago (or two years, or last Tuesday — however long you've been a member), you trusted us with a car you care about.

That means something to us. And it tells us something about you: you're the kind of person whose friends trust your recommendations on things that matter.

Here's a simple way to put that to work.

**If you refer a friend who joins a Pristine membership, you both get a complimentary Maintenance Detail — on us.**

No voucher codes. No sign-up forms. Just send us your mate's name and number (reply to this email, or text us on [phone number]) and we'll reach out, mention you sent them, and take it from there.

If they join, we book you both in for your free detail at the same time — or separately, whatever works.

The cars we look after in [Toorak / Brighton / Hawthorn / wherever their suburb is] tend to cluster. Someone on your street, in your building, at your car club — chances are they've been meaning to sort proper paint care but haven't found someone they trust yet.

Now they have. You can tell them.

Reply with a name and number and we'll handle the rest.

— The Pristine team

*Know more than one? Refer as many as you like. Each successful referral earns you a free Maintenance Detail.*

---

**Why this works:**
- Subject line speaks to *their* social identity (they're someone whose taste their friends trust)
- "Refer a mate" framing positions the customer as a helpful friend, not a sales agent
- Suburb-specific line ("the cars we look after in Toorak...") — personalise by member suburb for maximum resonance
- Zero friction: reply to email or text the number — no portal, no login
- The postscript opens the door to serial referrers without making it feel transactional

## The Referral Loop — How a Detail Becomes a Brand Ambassador

```
1. GREAT JOB       → Car looks better than the day it was bought
2. TRIGGER         → 24h SMS: "Your car's looking sharp. Know someone?"
3. REFER           → Customer texts a name. We follow up.
4. FRIEND JOINS    → Friend experiences the service, joins membership
5. REWARD          → Both get a free Maintenance Detail
6. FRIEND REFERS   → Friend texts a name (they now have the same goodwill moment)
7. MEMBER × 3      → Serial referrer earns Pristine Ambassador status
8. AMBASSADOR      → Annual Revitalise, priority booking, social proof asset
```

Every Signature member who becomes a brand ambassador is worth $5,000+ in direct revenue and potentially $15,000+ in referred revenue over three years. The cost: one free Revitalise per year ($300).
