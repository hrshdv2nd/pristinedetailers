## Targeting

### Melbourne Postcodes (Priority Radius)
Target these postcode clusters — highest ICP density:

| Suburb | Postcode |
|--------|----------|
| Toorak | 3141 |
| South Yarra | 3142 |
| Brighton | 3186 |
| Hawthorn | 3122 |
| Richmond | 3121 |
| St Kilda / Elwood | 3183 |
| Elsternwick | 3185 |
| Prahran / Windsor | 3184 |
| Mornington Peninsula | 3931–3943 |

> Set a 10km radius around each postcode cluster, not the whole Melbourne metro. Tighter targeting = lower CPM and more relevant reach.

### Interest Targeting

**Tier 1 — Direct vehicle intent:**
- BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi, Porsche, Tesla (brand pages/interests)
- Car detailing, auto care, automotive maintenance

**Tier 2 — Lifestyle signals:**
- Luxury goods (frequent purchasers)
- Property ownership / home ownership
- High-end dining, travel (Melbourne premium lifestyle indicators)

**Tier 3 — Behavioral:**
- Engaged shoppers (Meta behaviour)
- Luxury automotive buyers (recent auto finance or purchase signals)

### Lookalike Audience Strategy

| Audience | Source | Size | Priority |
|----------|--------|------|----------|
| LAL 1% | Past `/booking` converters (pixel) | Narrowest, highest intent | Primary |
| LAL 2% | All website visitors (90-day) | Moderate expansion | Secondary |
| LAL 3–5% | Email subscriber list upload | Broad discovery | Test only |

> Build the 1% LAL first. If booking volume is low (< 200 seed events), widen to 2–3% until the pixel has enough signal.

## Retargeting — `/booking` Page Visitors (Non-Converters)

**Audience window:** 14 days  
**Frequency cap:** 3x per week  
**Exclude:** Anyone who completed a booking (pixel event: `Purchase` or `Lead`)

| Element | Copy | Chars |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Primary text** | Still thinking about it? Your first month is the hardest part — after that, your car stays protected. Pick your plan. | 117 |
| **Headline** | Your Car's Still Waiting | 24 |
| **Description** | Plans start from one visit. | 27 |

> **Retargeting note:** Don't discount. Don't panic. The objection at `/booking` is friction, not price. Remind them the first step is small — one visit, cancel anytime. Urgency should feel like a gentle nudge, not a closing pitch.

## Creative Testing Hierarchy (This Week)

Run all 3 variants simultaneously. First checkpoint at 1,000 impressions per variant:

1. **Concept/angle** — Which of the 3 angles drives the lowest CPC? (Time-poor / Hazards / Savings)
2. **Melbourne specificity** — Track CTR on suburb name mentions (Toorak vs. Brighton vs. Peninsula)
3. **Pause** any variant with CTR < 0.8% after 2,000 impressions
4. **Scale** winner by 20% budget increase; do not combine audiences until week 2
