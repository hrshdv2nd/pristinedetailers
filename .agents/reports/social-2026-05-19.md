**Caption:**

Paint correction is the step most car owners skip — and it's the one that determines whether a ceramic coating actually works.

A ceramic coating bonds chemically to your clear coat. If that clear coat is full of swirl marks, oxidation, and micro-scratches — which it usually is after 2–3 years on Melbourne roads — you're sealing in the damage, not protecting over it. The coating looks decent for six months. Then the imperfections show through and performance drops off fast.

The correct sequence is: decontaminate, correct, then coat. We ran this process on a fleet manager's executive vehicles last month — three BMWs used for client pickups in South Yarra. Paint correction alone took four hours per car. The ceramic coating then had a clean, uniform surface to bond with properly.
