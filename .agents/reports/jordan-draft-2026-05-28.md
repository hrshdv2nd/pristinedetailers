## Full Article Body

## Why Bird Droppings Are More Destructive Than You Think

Most Melbourne drivers see a bird dropping and think: nuisance. The reality is more serious. Bird excrement is uric acid with a pH between 3.5 and 4.5 — aggressive enough to begin softening your car's clear coat within minutes of contact.

The damage isn't just cosmetic. When the clear coat is compromised, UV radiation and environmental contaminants get direct access to your base coat. What starts as a white smear on the bonnet can become a permanent etch mark — a dull patch that no amount of polishing will fully restore.

In Melbourne, this problem is compounded. The city's warm summers push ambient temperatures above 30°C regularly. Heat accelerates the chemical reaction. The same dropping that might give you 20 minutes on a cool morning can start etching in under 5 minutes on a hot summer day.

## The 10-Minute Rule

The 10-minute window isn't arbitrary. It's the approximate threshold before uric acid begins penetrating the clear coat on most modern factory finishes.

Here's what the timeline looks like:

**0–10 minutes:** The dropping sits on top of the clear coat. Removal at this stage is clean and leaves no trace.

**10–30 minutes:** The acid begins to soften and penetrate the clear coat surface. You may notice a slight haze after cleaning. Often correctable with a light machine polish.

**30 minutes–2 hours:** Etching is underway. The clear coat surface becomes visibly textured where the dropping sat. This will need professional paint correction — typically a single or two-stage polish.

**2+ hours:** Deep etching. The clear coat may be compromised through to the base coat in concentrated areas. At this stage, professional correction may not fully restore the finish.

**Several days (dried):** The uric acid has crystallised and the damage is locked in. Depending on severity, you're looking at paint correction, spot blending, or in worst cases, a panel respray.

## What to Do If You Spot One

Speed matters more than perfect technique. Here's the right sequence:

1. **Don't wipe it dry.** A dry cloth against a hardening dropping will drag it across the paint and cause micro-scratches.

2. **Soften it first.** Spray water directly onto the dropping, or use a dedicated quick detailer spray. Let it sit for 20–30 seconds.

3. **Blot, don't wipe.** Use a clean microfibre cloth folded into quarters. Press and lift — don't drag. Turn the cloth to a clean section with each blot.

4. **Rinse the area.** Follow up with a clean water rinse to remove any remaining residue.

5. **Inspect under light.** Once dry, view the area at an angle in direct sunlight or under a garage light. A faint watermark or texture means it's been etched. Note the location and have it assessed.

## Melbourne's Worst Offenders

Not all suburbs carry the same risk. If you park under trees in Toorak, Brighton, or anywhere along the Port Phillip foreshore, you're dealing with high volumes of fruit-eating birds — mynas, sulphur-crested cockatoos, and wattlebirds. Their droppings are particularly acidic because of their diet.

Office car parks under established gum trees in Richmond and Hawthorn are also notorious. The cars that come in for paint correction most often have spent time in these locations.

The Mornington Peninsula adds a coastal factor: salt air, strong UV, and heavy bird activity from the coastline combine to make exterior paint care a higher priority than most owners realise.

## Why Ceramic Coatings Change the Equation

A ceramic coating doesn't make your car immune to bird dropping damage — that's a common misconception worth clearing up. What it does is slow the clock significantly.

The ceramic layer adds a sacrificial barrier between the uric acid and your clear coat. The dropping still needs to be removed, but your window to act extends considerably. The coating can absorb minor acid exposure that would otherwise reach the clear coat directly.

Removal is also easier. A hydrophobic surface means the dropping doesn't bond as strongly, and a light rinse often loosens it enough for safe removal without abrasive wiping.

If you've already had paint correction done, applying a ceramic coating immediately after is the most logical next step — you're protecting a restored surface rather than compounding ongoing damage.

## The Bottom Line

Bird droppings are one of the most common causes of preventable paint damage in Melbourne. The fix is almost always faster than the problem — if you catch it in time.

Keep a small spray bottle of quick detailer and a folded microfibre in your car. That's the entire kit you need. Use it within 10 minutes and the damage simply doesn't happen.

If you've already got etching you're not sure about, book a paint inspection. The sooner it's assessed, the more options you have.
