## Email 3 (Winter Edition) — July/August Win-Back

```
Sequence:    Re-engagement — winter version
Trigger:     No booking activity for 30 days after last service
Send timing: Day 30 of inactivity, July–August sends only
Goal:        Re-book before end-of-winter paint damage compounds
Exit:        Booking made OR unsubscribe
```

**Subject:** Melbourne winter is doing things to your paint right now  
**Preview:** It's not visible yet. That's the problem.

---

**Body:**

Hi [First name],

It's been about a month since we last looked after [your car / the BMW / the Range Rover].

July in Melbourne is the month most people forget about their paint — because the car looks fine, and the weather doesn't scream "UV damage." But winter does something slower and more expensive.

Here's what's working on your paint right now:

**Wet leaves and debris on panels.** Tannins from decomposing leaves are mildly acidic. Left in contact with clear coat for days (not weeks), they leave staining that hand washing won't remove.

**Road grit.** CityLink, EastLink, and the Monash chew up road surfaces in winter. The fine debris your car throws up behind trucks is abrasive — it's micro-scratching every panel every day.

**Bay salt.** If you're in Brighton, Beaumaris, Mentone, or anywhere on the Peninsula, salt air accelerates surface oxidation on unprotected paint, seals, and trim. It's not dramatic. It just accumulates.

**Bird season.** Kookaburras and mynas are active all winter. Droppings from these birds have a uric acid content that can etch clear coat in under 24 hours in cold weather when the paint is contracting.

---

If your car has a ceramic coating: it's doing its job. But even ceramic-coated cars need a maintenance wash every 6–8 weeks to flush contaminants and keep the hydrophobic layer at full strength. Think of it as a winter service interval, not optional.

If the paint is unprotected: winter is actually the best time to get ahead of it — UV is lower, so paint correction results last longer before the summer sun tests them.

Book this week and we'll include a complimentary paint health check — a 10-minute assessment of where your paint currently sits, no obligation.

[Book a service →]

[First name]  
Pristine Detailers

---

**CTA:** [Book a service →] → /booking  
**Secondary offer:** Free paint health check (framed as information, not a sales tool)  
**Length:** ~280 words  
**Notes:**  
- Send: July 1 – August 31 only. Swap to spring block (September–November) in September.  
- The leaf tannin detail and bird acid specifics are accurate — don't soften them; the precision is the credibility.  
- "Brighton, Beaumaris, Mentone, Peninsula" — geo-personalise if possible, otherwise leave the list; it signals local expertise.  
- Do not use the word "winter" more than twice — it reads marketing.

## Email 4 — Membership Nudge: A/B Variants

The June report flagged the subject line as the primary test opportunity. Here are three variants plus one body copy alternative. Run one test at a time.

```
Sequence:    Lifecycle upgrade
Trigger:     Customer completes 2nd booking (no active membership)
Send timing: 48 hours after 2nd service completion
Goal:        Membership conversion
```

### Subject Line Tests

| Variant | Subject Line | Hypothesis |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **A (Control)** | You've booked with us twice. The maths is now in your favour. | Logical/value frame — appeals to the ROI-aware professional |
| **B** | The two-booking point is where most members join us. | Social proof frame — normalises the decision, removes friction |
| **C** | [First name], your [car] qualifies for member pricing. | Personal/exclusivity frame — "your car" + "qualifies" implies earned access |

**Recommended test order:** Run B vs A first (expect B to win on CTR due to social normalisation). Then test C vs winner (C may win on opens but lose on conversion if it feels too promotional).

**Preview text pairings:**

- Variant A: "Most members save $200–$400 in the first year. The scheduling priority is the part they talk about."  
- Variant B: "Most people wait until the third or fourth booking. Here's why the second is actually the better time."  
- Variant C: "Priority scheduling, member pricing, monthly wash included. Here's what that means for your car."

---

### Body Copy Variant — Scheduling-Led Angle

The control body leads with savings ($200–$400). This variant leads with scheduling priority, which the June report noted "surprises people the most." Test this body against the control body when variant A or B subject wins.

[First name]

**CTA:** [See membership options →] → /membership  
**Secondary CTA:** Reply (human, no funnel pressure)

## Email 5 — NEW: Post-Membership Onboarding

This is the missing email in the current sequence. Once a customer joins a membership, nothing happens to make them feel like an insider, explain what to expect, or accelerate their path to becoming a referrer. This email fixes that.

```
Sequence:    Membership onboarding
Trigger:     First membership payment processed (Essential or Signature)
Send timing: Immediately on payment confirmation
Goal:        Set expectations, confirm value, plant referral seed, make them feel insider
Exit:        First membership service scheduled
```

**Subject:** Welcome to the membership — here's how it works from here  
**Preview:** Priority booking, what's included each month, and one thing most new members don't know.

---

**Body:**

Hi [First name],

Your Pristine Detailers [Essential / Signature] membership is active.

Here's what to expect from here:

**Your first step:** Book your [monthly wash / bi-monthly detail] — you can do it now or whenever suits you. As a member, you're accessing the priority calendar, so you won't be competing for slots with public availability.

[Book your first member service →]

---

**What's included every month:**

*[Essential members:]*  
Your monthly wash and seal is included in your membership — no extra charge. You'll also get member pricing on any additional services you book (paint correction, PPF, ceramic upgrades).

*[Signature members:]*  
Your bi-monthly full detail is included, along with ceramic maintenance each service. For any add-ons or upgrades, your member rate applies automatically.

---

**What most new members don't realise:**

Membership isn't just about the price. The technician who looks after your car gets to know it — the panel quirks, the previous correction work, what the paint responds to. After two or three services, the work gets better because the context accumulates.

This is the part a one-off service can't give you.

---

**And one more thing:**

If you have a friend, colleague, or neighbour with a car they care about — we'd love to look after them. Mention it to us when you book and we'll make sure they get the same level of service you experienced.

There's no formal referral program, just: people who know Pristine tend to tell people who should know Pristine.

Talk soon,  
[First name at Pristine Detailers]  
[phone number]

---

**CTA (primary):** [Book your first member service →] → /booking (priority calendar view)  
**CTA (secondary):** Referral nudge (no link — conversational, framed as community not incentive)  
**Length:** ~290 words  
**Notes:**  
- Personalise membership tier (Essential vs Signature) — the inclusions are different.  
- The "technician knows your car" paragraph is the emotional hook — it frames membership as a relationship, not a subscription. This is what separates Pristine from any transactional service.  
- The referral seed is planted here deliberately — before the 2nd service, before the post-service follow-up referral ask. This is ambient, not a CTA.  
- Consider including the technician's first name if your booking system assigns a primary tech at signup.

## Growth Loop Notes

The addition of Email 5 closes the loop the previous report described but didn't fully wire:

```
Service → Email 2 (review + referral nudge)
         ↓
     [2nd booking]
         ↓
Email 4 (membership nudge) → Member joins
         ↓
Email 5 (membership onboarding) → Referral seed planted
         ↓
     Referral friend books → enters Email 1
         ↓
     [Loop restarts]
```

The referral in Email 5 is intentionally not a formal program ask — it's a "tell people who should know us" framing. This aligns with Week A's referral program work (growth-referral-2026-07-01.md). The formal referral mechanic (offer, link, tracking) lives in Week A; the ambient referral culture is built here, in the post-service and post-membership emails.
