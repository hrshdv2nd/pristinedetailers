**Caption:**

A client handed over their black Mercedes-Benz last week believing the paint was finished. Three years of Melbourne sun, a few too many automatic car washes, and the kind of slow neglect that happens when a car is a tool rather than a trophy.

Under a detail inspection lamp, the panel showed dense swirl marks across every surface — micro-scratches from abrasive brushes and dry wiping that scatter light and kill the depth in dark paint. The coating hadn't failed. The clear coat beneath it had taken years of unaddressed damage.

Four hours of machine correction later — cut compound, finishing polish, dual-action polisher — the clarity came back. No respray. No new panels. Just systematic removal of damaged clear coat, revealing the gloss underneath.
