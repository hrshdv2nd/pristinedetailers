## What Is Legal in Victoria?

Window tint is regulated by VicRoads through the Australian Design Rules. Every car on Victorian roads must comply — and getting this wrong isn't just a fine, it's a roadworthy issue that can invalidate your registration.

| Window | Minimum VLT (Visible Light Transmission) | Notes |
|--------|------------------------------------------|-------|
| Windscreen (above AS line) | No tint film permitted | Factory-tinted glass only |
| Front side windows | 35% VLT or higher | Must allow 35% of light through |
| Rear side windows | No restriction | Must have dual side mirrors if below 35% |
| Rear window | No restriction | Must have dual side mirrors if below 35% |

VLT is the percentage of visible light that passes through the glass. A lower number means a darker tint. Factory glass on most vehicles sits around 70–75% VLT. Adding a 35% film to 70% factory glass results in a combined VLT of approximately 24% — which falls below the legal front-window limit. Our technicians calculate the combined VLT before every installation, not after.

The practical rule for front windows: a film rated at 50–70% VLT applied over standard factory glass keeps you legal while delivering real heat and glare reduction. For rear windows, you have considerably more flexibility.

## How Mobile Tinting Works

You don't need to drop anything off or arrange alternate transport. Here's what happens when Pristine comes to you:

1. **Booking** — choose your vehicle and confirm which windows you want tinted; we verify your location has suitable access (undercover preferred, though covered outdoor spaces work too)
2. **Arrival and prep** — the technician cleans each window with a contamination-free wash solution to remove dust, oils, and any residue that would trap beneath the film
3. **Film cutting** — film is cut to precise window dimensions using a computer plotter or hardcopy template — no scoring on the glass, no guesswork
4. **Application** — film is applied wet to the interior glass surface, pressed flat with a precision squeegee, and trimmed cleanly to the window edge
5. **Initial cure** — film is touch-dry within 24 hours; full cure takes 3–5 days depending on temperature and humidity

## Carbon Film vs Ceramic Film: Which Should You Choose?

If heat rejection matters more than cost, specify ceramic. Ceramic film blocks up to 60% of infrared radiation while maintaining full transparency and leaving RF signals untouched — relevant on any Tesla, BMW, or Mercedes where glass-mounted antennas handle mobile data, GPS, and Apple CarPlay. It also holds its colour longer; dyed films can purple or fade within a few years in Melbourne's UV.

If you're tinting a commuter car and want visible glare reduction without a large outlay, a quality carbon film on the rear windows with a lighter film on the fronts is the most practical combination.

## FAQ

**How long does window tinting take?**
A full car takes 2–4 hours. Front two windows only takes under an hour.

**Can you tint in an underground car park?**
Yes. We've worked in basement car parks across South Yarra, Richmond, Toorak, and the Melbourne CBD. We need reasonable overhead lighting and enough space to open doors fully. Covered outdoor spaces work equally well.

**Does window tinting void my car's warranty?**
No. Professionally applied window film sits on the interior glass surface and does not affect manufacturer warranties on any other component. The film is also removable without damaging the glass if needed.

**What VLT is legal in Victoria for front windows?**
35% is the minimum legal VLT for front side windows under Victorian road rules. Your technician calculates the combined VLT — factory glass plus film — before committing to a specification.

**Can I drive immediately after tinting?**
Yes. The film is stable from the moment installation is complete. Avoid winding the tinted windows down for the first 3–5 days while the adhesive finishes curing.
