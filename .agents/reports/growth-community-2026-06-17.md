**Why this community:**

Our ICP (prestige car owners aged 28–55) lurks and posts here, especially the performance and European car crowd. High-intent audience: people asking about coatings are already in buying mode. - Post original educational content every 2–3 weeks  

**Draft Post — r/CarsAustralia:**

> **Title:** Melbourne-specific paint damage: what's actually destroying your clear coat (and it's not what most people think)
>
> I've been detailing cars in Melbourne for years and the same four things come up on the paint inspection every single time. Sharing this because I see a lot of people focus on wash technique when the real damage is happening between washes.
>
> **1. Bird droppings — and the time between drop and wipe**  
> Bird dropping etching is the #1 paint killer in inner suburbs. The uric acid in bird droppings starts bonding to your clear coat within 20–30 minutes in direct sun. In Toorak, South Yarra, and St Kilda — where you've got mature plane trees and lots of on-street parking — cars get hit daily. The damage isn't the dropping itself, it's leaving it. A clean microfibre and a spray detailer in your car costs $20 and saves a $2,000 paint correction later.
>
> **2. Freeway stone chips — specifically the Eastern and Monash corridors**  
> Melbourne's freeway network throws more chips than most people realise. The Monash and Eastern have sections with exposed aggregate near merge lanes. Most chips on bonnets and front bumpers come from 80–110km/h freeway driving. Once the chip breaks through to primer, it starts rusting within 3–6 months depending on how much you park near the coast (looking at you, Bayside and Mornington Peninsula).
>
> **3. Automated car washes — the swirl factory**  
> The rotating brush washes at servo stations are the primary cause of swirl marks I see on dark-coloured cars. One wash through a brush machine can create more micro-scratches than a year of careful hand washing. Touchless is fine. Anything with brushes or cloth strips — avoid.
>
> **4. UV near Port Phillip Bay**  
> Bayside suburbs cop more reflective UV from the water than most people account for. White and silver cars hide it, but dark metallics (BMW Space Grey, Porsche Jet Black Metallic, Mercedes Obsidian Black) oxidise faster if they're parked outside regularly in Elwood, Brighton, Hampton.
>
> Happy to answer any paint protection or correction questions — this stuff is all I think about.

### Community 3: BMW Car Club of Victoria / Porsche Club of Victoria (Club Forums & Facebook)

**Why this community:**  
Marque clubs are the highest-trust communities our ICP belongs to. A recommendation from a fellow BMW or Porsche Club member carries more weight than any Google review. Members are protective of their cars, knowledgeable about products, and willing to pay premium for quality — they've already proven that by buying a $100K+ car. Both the BMW Car Club of Victoria and Porsche Club of Victoria have active Facebook groups, newsletters, and event programs.

**Presence strategy:**  
- Approach club committee about becoming a "Preferred Supplier" — these clubs typically have a supplier partner list  
- Attend a club run or track day as a guest/exhibitor  
- Offer club members an exclusive rate (5–10% discount, or complimentary decontamination wash with any coating)  
- Post technical content in the club Facebook groups — not on every thread, but when someone asks about paint care

**Draft Post — BMW Car Club of Victoria Facebook Group:**

> **Post text:**
>
> Question for the group: how many of you have had a paint depth reading done on your car before buying/applying a ceramic coating?
>
> I ask because paint thickness matters a lot for coating adhesion and longevity — and on European cars in particular, factory respray panels (which are common on pre-owned cars) behave very differently to OEM paint. Respray panels often have non-standard clear coat thickness, and some products don't bond as well to them.
>
> If your car has had any panel work done (even by a prestige repairer), it's worth knowing before you commit to a coating. A depth gauge reading takes 2 minutes per panel and tells you exactly what you're working with.
>
> Posting this because I see people disappointed with coating results that aren't the coating's fault — it's the prep and paint assessment that made the difference.
>
> If anyone wants a reading done at the next club night, happy to bring the gauge along.

## Partnership Recommendation: Prestige European Car Dealerships

**The opportunity:**  
When someone buys a new BMW, Mercedes, Porsche, or Audi from a Melbourne dealership, they are handed the keys and told about "dealership protection packages" — typically an overpriced, generic sealant applied by detailers the dealer contracts at low margin. Buyers are sceptical of these packages but do want real protection for their new car.

**The pitch to dealerships:**  
Pristine becomes the dealership's "preferred detailing partner" for customers who decline the in-house package. Position it as: *"For customers who want professional-grade ceramic coating or PPF, we refer them to Pristine Detailers — certified technicians, they come to you."*

**Why dealerships say yes:**  
- They don't make much on the in-house package anyway  
- They can offer it as a premium alternative without managing it  
- It's a genuine value-add they can use in the sales conversation  
- No exclusivity required from them — just a referral agreement  

**Target dealerships (Melbourne):**  
- **Zagame BMW** (Richmond) — large volume, performance car focus  
- **Trivett Porsche Melbourne** (Port Melbourne)  
- **Mercedes-Benz of Brighton** (Bayside — exact ICP geography)  
- **Audi Centre Melbourne** (South Yarra)  
- **Autobahn Motors** (multiple locations, European specialists)  

**Referral structure to propose:**  
- Flat $150–$200 referral fee per converted customer (ceramic or PPF job)  
- OR: Co-branded collateral left in the dealership handover kit ("Your new car deserves professional paint protection — here's an offer from our preferred partner")  
- Offer: new car buyers get a complimentary paint decontamination wash (valued at $120) with any coating booking — dealership presents this as a bonus  

**Why this compounds:**  
New car buyer → Pristine ceramic coating → joins membership (ongoing maintenance) → car looks great at next Cars & Coffee meet → recommends Pristine to club members → new customers → repeat.
