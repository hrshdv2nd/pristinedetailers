**Caption:**

A misconception we see constantly: ceramic coating and Paint Protection Film do the same job. They don't — and confusing them is a costly mistake on a vehicle worth serious money.

Ceramic coating is a nano-ceramic compound that chemically bonds to your clear coat. It creates a hydrophobic, UV-stable surface barrier that repels water, bird acid, tree sap, and contamination. Outstanding at what it does. And it does nothing to stop a stone chip. A rock at freeway speed will punch straight through it.

Paint Protection Film is a self-healing polyurethane layer applied to the panels most exposed to road debris — bonnet, front bumper, mirrors, door edges. It absorbs the physical impact before it reaches your paint. For any vehicle driven regularly on Melbourne's freeways, PPF on the front end isn't a luxury. It's basic asset management on a car that may be worth $80,000 to $250,000.

The correct setup for most prestige vehicles: PPF on high-impact zones first, ceramic coating layered over the full car on top.
