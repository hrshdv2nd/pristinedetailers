# Email Sequence: Membership Retention
**Focus:** Re-engage members who haven't booked in 45+ days  
**Date written:** 2026-06-02  
**Melbourne season:** Early Winter (June)  
**Skill used:** churn-prevention  

---

## Context

These three emails target members on a Pristine Detailers monthly subscription who haven't made a booking in 45+ days. The membership is running, they're being charged, but they're not using it. Goal is to re-engage before they quietly cancel.

Winter framing is deliberate: Melbourne road grit, rain, salt air near the bay, and condensation build-up are real, concrete reasons to book — not manufactured urgency.

Trigger this sequence automatically based on days-since-last-booking. Suppress if a booking is made at any point.

---

## Email 1 — Day 45: Soft Check-In

**Subject:** Haven't seen your car in a while  
**Preview text:** Quick check-in from the Pristine team  
**Send time:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 8:30am  
**Tone:** Warm, zero pressure, human

---

Hey [First Name],

It's been about six weeks since your last detail. I noticed you haven't had anything booked in, so just wanted to check in.

No pitch here. Just: Melbourne's been doing its thing lately. Rain, road grit, that low winter sun that makes every water mark and streak show up on your paint. It adds up quietly, especially on a coated car.

Your membership's all there and ready when you are. If you want to lock something in, a maintenance wash is usually 60–90 minutes and we come to you — home, office, wherever suits.

[Book a maintenance wash →]

Talk soon,  
Tom  
Pristine Detailers  

---

**Notes:**
- Personalise [First Name] from CRM
- "Tom" = sender name from account manager or team lead — keep consistent across sequence
- CTA links to /booking pre-filtered to maintenance wash service

---

## Email 2 — Day 52: Value Reminder

**Subject:** What your membership includes that you haven't used this month  
**Preview text:** It's sitting there — here's what's covered  
**Send time:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 8:30am  
**Tone:** Informative, slight nudge, still no pressure

---

Hey [First Name],

You're at about eight weeks since your last detail. Before I follow up again, I just want to make sure you know what's covered in your membership right now — because a fair bit of it goes unused when bookings drop off.

**What's included and ready to use:**
- Monthly maintenance wash (already paid for, doesn't roll over)
- Priority booking — no waitlist, slots held for members first
- Member rate on ceramic top-up treatments and interior work

The reason I'm flagging this now: June isn't the harshest month in Melbourne, but wet roads carry grit that sits on the coating surface. On a ceramic-coated car, a maintenance wash every 4–6 weeks is what keeps the hydrophobic layer working properly. Leave it too long and the coating still works, but it has to work harder — and the next detail takes more time to bring it back up.

Not a disaster if you miss a month. But two missed months starts to show.

If a Saturday morning slot is easier, I can usually find you something in the next fortnight — it's quieter in winter.

[Check available times →]

Tom  
Pristine Detailers  

---

**Notes:**
- Bullet list should pull from their specific membership tier if data allows (e.g. "Premium Membership" includes X, Y, Z)
- "Saturday morning" is a real offer — make sure booking page surfaces weekend availability
- Still no discount — this email is about value education, not incentive

---

## Email 3 — Day 60: Save Offer

**Subject:** A free interior detail if you book before the 15th  
**Preview text:** No catch — here's the offer  
**Send time:** Tuesday, 8:30am  
**Tone:** Direct, honest, personal — like a conversation not a campaign

---

Hey [First Name],

Two months since your last visit. I'm going to be straight with you.

Your membership is still running, and I want to make sure it's actually worth your while. If things have changed — you've moved, sold the car, whatever — just reply and we'll sort it out. No awkward process.

But if it's just been one of those months where it slipped off the radar, here's what I'd like to do:

**Book any service before [DATE: 15th of current month] and I'll add a full interior detail at no charge.**

That's leather wipe-down, full vacuum, inside windows, odour treatment. About 45 minutes of work. It makes a real difference this time of year — winter means more wet gear going in and out of the car, and interiors get musty faster than people expect.

This isn't a campaign we're running. It's me trying to get you back in front of the team so you remember why you signed up.

[Claim the free interior add-on →]  
*(Offer valid until [DATE]. One per active membership. Book by the 15th, service can be scheduled within 30 days.)*

If you'd prefer to pause your membership rather than cancel, we can do that too. Just reply here or call me on [PHONE].

Tom  
Pristine Detailers  
[phone number]  

---

**Notes:**
- [DATE] = 15th of current month — populate dynamically at send time
- Free interior detail is the save offer — it's tangible, winter-relevant, and has real perceived value ($80–120 standalone)
- "Reply to this email" must route to a real inbox, not a no-reply address
- If member replies saying they want to pause/cancel, route to human immediately — do not automate the response
- Track: open rate, click rate, and whether a booking is made within 7 days of send. Bookings = save. No action after Email 3 = review membership status.

---

## Seasonal Note (Winter Frame)

June in Melbourne: persistent rain, road spray, morning condensation, sea salt near Bayside/Brighton. These are legitimate reasons to book a maintenance wash — not manufactured. The emails use real winter-specific concerns (grit on coating, musty interiors from wet gear) rather than generic urgency. This keeps the brand voice honest and the emails credible.
