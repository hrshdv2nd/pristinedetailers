## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Problem-Agitate-Solve (Angle: Melbourne's environment is eating your clear coat)

**Primary text (125 chars):**
> Your Porsche sits in the Toorak sun. Bird droppings etch in 24 hours. UV fades clear coat in months. Ceramic stops all three.

**Headline (26 chars):**
> 8-Year Ceramic. Your Home.

**Description (25 chars):**
> Mobile. $750+. Certified.

**Why this works:** Three environmental threats named in three sentences — the sentence structure mimics the way damage actually accumulates: sun exposure (daily), bird droppings (weekly, catastrophic when left), UV degradation (monthly, invisible until it isn't). Each threat is Melbourne-specific: Toorak street parking, eucalyptus canopy bird traffic, and Port Phillip Bay UV intensity. "Ceramic stops all three" closes as a technical claim without explaining the mechanism — the reader already knows what ceramic does (or they'll click to find out). Headline addresses the two barriers in five words: the warranty length (8 years) and the logistical hook (your home). No drop-off mentioned; "Your Home" implies it.

**Note on character count:** Primary text is exactly 125 chars — full hook reads before "See more" truncation on all placements. Do not edit without rechecking count.

### Variant 2 — Investment Argument (Angle: You've spent $100K on this car. A $30 wash isn't protecting it.)

**Primary text (125 chars):**
> A $100K car doesn't need a $30 wash protecting it. Ceramic coating bonds to your clear coat. 8-year warranty. We come to you.

**Headline (25 chars):**
> Ceramic Coating Melbourne

**Description (24 chars):**
> Protect your clear coat.

**Why this works:** The $100K vs $30 contrast is not an attack on the owner — it's a reframe. Most people with a $100K+ car aren't consciously choosing inadequate protection; they haven't been told a better alternative exists that actually comes to them. The "chemical bond to your clear coat" line is the mechanism that earns credibility without a laboratory explanation. "8-year warranty" is the proof point. "We come to you" is the logistics resolution — four words that remove the only remaining objection after the investment argument lands.

Match vehicle to ad set's interest targeting. Same investment argument, specific vehicle credibility.

**Headline (24 chars):**
> Your Coating Consult Waits

**Description (25 chars):**
> We come to you. Book now.

**Targeting window:** 7 days post-visit
**Frequency cap:** 3× per week — ceramic is a high-consideration purchase; over-serving creates doubt, not urgency
**Exclusions:** Suppress anyone who completed a booking post-visit (immediate exclusion on conversion event); suppress active ceramic coating customers

**Why this works:** Investment framing rather than urgency or discount. The buyer who got to `/booking` for a $750+ service has cleared their own financial objection — they're stuck on something else (timing, trust, logistics). "You were 2 minutes from booking" acknowledges that they were close without being pushy. "Pick back up below" is a practical CTA that treats them as a capable adult who knows what they want. Do not add a discount or limited-time offer — it devalues the premium positioning and is unnecessary for a buyer this far down the funnel.

## Creative Direction Notes

**Image/video guidance:**
- Hero visual: Water beading off a dark-coloured Porsche, BMW or Range Rover bonnet in sheets — close-up, morning light, residential Toorak or Brighton driveway context. The hydrophobic effect is visual proof that needs no caption. Let the image work.
- Technician sequence (15–20 sec video): Pristine technician arriving at a residential driveway, unpacking equipment, applying ceramic coating in stages — end on the completed car with a water bead demo. Native to Instagram Reels. No voiceover needed if captions carry the story. Avoid studio lighting — the residential context is part of the sell.
- Before/after carousel: Panel comparison of a BMW M4 or Porsche Cayman — pre-correction dull/oxidised vs. post-ceramic deep gloss. Six panels including a water-bead shot. Caption: "Same car. 12 months of Melbourne roads. Before correction + ceramic vs. after." Honest sequencing — correction first, then ceramic.
- Testimonial card: Marcus T. quote ("The finish on my GT3 is better than factory. Water rolls off in sheets.") over a clean GT3 in a Hawthorn or Toorak garage — the residential garage context reinforces the mobile story. Use real garage environment, not a studio backdrop.
- Avoid: Generic "sparkly car" studio shots; stock images of smiling mechanics; before/after comparisons that look doctored; any claim about "best in Melbourne" or "industry-leading."

**Format priority for Ceramic Coating:**
1. Instagram Feed (static) — V1 and V3 as primary prospecting; car enthusiast audience skews toward Instagram; visual proof (water beading, finish quality) lands hardest in a visual-first format
2. Facebook Feed (static + carousel) — V2 investment argument; slightly older demographic (35–55) who respond to the financial logic; wider reach for Bayside and Mornington Peninsula
3. Instagram Reels — 15–20 sec technician process video; native storytelling for the "how does this work" curiosity angle
4. Stories — retargeting only; single-image booking abandoner copy; swipe-up friction matches the low-friction CTA

**Copy test plan (weeks 1–2):**

- Retargeting budget is separate; exclude from prospecting measurement pool

- Paint Correction → Ceramic sequential audience (Seed 3 LAL) runs V3 only — they already know correction was done, they need the outcome proof to book ceramic next

## Context Notes

- **Melbourne-specific hooks used:** "Toorak sun" in V1 (accurate: Toorak has high street tree cover but direct sun on driveways and street parking); "bird droppings etch in 24 hours" (Australian bird population density in inner-suburban canopy streets is genuinely higher than comparable European/North American suburbs; this is a real local concern); "Port Phillip Bay UV" implied via bayside targeting; "Collins St" and "Mornington Peninsula" in V2 variants; Marcus T. testimonial tied to Porsche GT3 audience
- **Tone check:** Confident, technical, not salesy — "chemical bond", "clear coat", "hydrophobic" used accurately; investment argument avoids "luxury" framing; testimonial used verbatim without embellishment; no exclamation marks
- **Ceramic vs PPF distinction:** This campaign is ceramic coating only. PPF (Paint Protection Film) campaigns run weeks 2/6. Do not mix messaging — the two products address different damage types (ceramic handles UV/chemical/bird; PPF handles stone chips/physical impact). Buyers researching both should be directed to a combined consultation, not to a single-product landing page.
- **Seasonal note:** Winter is not peak ceramic coating booking season in Melbourne, but it is a smart time to book — installers have more availability, and the coating has 24–48 hours to cure in a covered garage or car park (which Melbourne's grey July skies actually support). Lean into the "book now, faster install slots" angle on the landing page if capacity allows.
