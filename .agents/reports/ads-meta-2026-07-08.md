## Context: Why Paint Correction in July

Melbourne's peak swirl season is April–July. Wet roads kick up grit, bird droppings accumulate on paint left standing in suburban driveways, and automated car washes — the worst offenders — get used more often in winter because nobody wants to hand-wash in the cold. By July, a car that looked fine in March is showing micro-scratches in direct sunlight. For paint-aware owners who've been meaning to book a correction since autumn, late July is the psychological inflection point: spring is visible on the calendar, and they don't want to go into it — or into a ceramic coating booking — with degraded paint.

Seasonal frame for all three variants: **post-winter reset, before spring / before ceramic**.

## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Problem/Seasonality (Angle: Winter left its mark — now is the reset window)

**Primary text (118 chars):**
> Winter leaves its mark. Grit, bird drops, wet roads. Seeing swirls in sunlight? This is your reset window.

**Headline (25 chars):**
> Paint Correction. Mobile.

**Description (26 chars):**
> We come to you, Melbourne.

**Why this works:** Opens with a statement the ICP can confirm without thinking — winter, grit, bird drops are lived Melbourne experiences, not claims about a product. "Seeing swirls in sunlight" is the specific moment of recognition: pain-aware owners have stood beside their car on a bright day and noticed it. "This is your reset window" reframes July from an awkward mid-year moment into an opportunity. The word "reset" positions correction as a deliberate act, not a reaction to damage — which matches how this audience thinks about maintenance. No product pitch until the landing page.

**Seasonal resonance:** The Mornington Peninsula angle is strong here. Owners who drive the Peninsula Highway regularly accumulate gravel impact by July. Run a variant for Peninsula postcodes (3931–3945) with "Mornington Peninsula roads are hard on your paint" as the opening line instead.

### Variant 3 — Education / Intent Bridge (Angle: Coating without correction is a mistake — here's why)

**Primary text (122 chars):**
> Ceramic coating locks in what's underneath it. Swirls now? Sealed in forever. Correct first. Then coat. Mobile, certified.

**Headline (26 chars):**
> Correct It Before You Coat

**Description (23 chars):**
> From $300. Book Online.

**Why this works:** This variant does not sell paint correction — it sells the logic of the decision sequence. The opening statement is a technical fact, not a sales claim: ceramic coatings bond to the paint surface as it exists at time of application. For a buyer who has already decided they want a ceramic coating ($750+), this ad does two things simultaneously: (1) validates their research by confirming they need correction first, and (2) positions Pristine as the expert who gets the sequence right. The shift from "Swirls now?" to "Sealed in forever" creates a consequence frame without exaggeration. "Correct first. Then coat." is a directive, not a pitch — it reads as expert advice. This variant should be served to retargeting audiences who visited `/services/ceramic-coating` and have not yet booked correction.

**Targeting note:** Prioritise this variant for users who have browsed the ceramic coating page but have not yet booked. It bridges the intent gap and drives them to the correct first step without losing the ceramic booking downstream.

## Retargeting — /booking Page Abandoners (Paint Correction Focus)

For users who visited `/services/paint-correction` or `/booking` with paint correction selected but did not complete (window: 7 days):

**Primary text (92 chars):**
> You were one step away from paint that turns heads. Your spot's still open — we come to you.

**Headline (26 chars):**
> Still Thinking? We're Here

**Description (23 chars):**
> Certified. No drop-off.

**Targeting window:** 7 days post-visit
**Frequency cap:** 3× per week — do not over-serve; for this ICP, excessive retargeting signals desperation and undermines the premium positioning
**Exclusions:** Suppress on confirmed booking event; suppress existing paint correction customers booked in last 90 days

**Why this works:** "One step away from paint that turns heads" is outcome-first — not "complete your booking" but the result they almost got. "Your spot's still open" introduces soft urgency without fabricating scarcity: Pristine's schedule does fill during the working week, so this is accurate and specific. "We come to you" answers the most common hesitation before abandonment — logistics. "No drop-off" in the description is the objection-handler: the most common reason buyers delay on detailing services is the workshop inconvenience. No discount. The friction for this audience isn't price; it's trust that a mobile service matches a studio result.

## Creative Direction Notes

**Image/video guidance:**
- **Hero visual:** Certified technician with a dual-action polisher on a dark-coloured Porsche or BMW in a Toorak or Hawthorn residential driveway — morning light hitting the panel at an angle that reveals the machine polishing action. Melbourne residential context visible in background (period architecture, established garden). The swirl removal should be visible as a process, not just a before/after.
- **Before/after split panel:** One half under harsh LED fluorescence (to show swirl marks clearly — the way they appear in a car park or direct sunlight), one half after correction in the same light. No heavy editing. Caption: "Same car. Same light. Paint correction, no drop-off." This format performs well on Facebook Feed for the education-aware buyer.
- **Process video (15 sec):** Technician arriving at a residential driveway, setting up, machine polishing, revealing the result in direct sunlight — water beading as confirmation. Ends with text overlay: "Mobile paint correction. Toorak to Bayside." No voice-over needed; the visual process does the work. This format suits Instagram Reels.
- **Testimonial card:** Marcus T.'s quote ("The finish on my GT3 is better than factory. Water rolls off in sheets.") over a clean Porsche 911 GT3 in a Melbourne residential setting. Real location context matters — avoid studio backgrounds.
- **Avoid:** Stock images of generic sedans; "sparkling clean car" heroes without location context; before/after images where the difference is achieved through heavy photo editing rather than an honest depiction of the result.

**Format priority for Paint Correction:**
1. Facebook Feed (static before/after) — primary for prospecting; before/after format communicates the result mechanically without needing copy to explain it
2. Instagram Feed — V2 variants (testimonial cards with prestige car + Melbourne suburb context); visual context of the vehicle and suburb combination lands harder in visual-first format
3. Instagram Reels — 15-sec process video; native for how-it-works content; strong for Variant 3 (education/ceramic intent bridge audience)
4. Stories — retargeting only; booking abandoner copy; swipe-up friction matches the low-friction CTA message

**Copy test plan (weeks 1–2):**
- Run V1 (winter reset/seasonal) vs V2a (GT3 testimonial) as cold audience in Inner East and Bayside — measure CTR and cost-per-click-to-paint-correction-page
- V3 (ceramic intent bridge) runs as a separate audience: exclusively to ceramic coating page visitors who haven't booked correction
- V2 vehicle variants run per interest group as challenger sets — determine whether named vehicle + suburb outperforms the seasonal angle for each make
- Kill lowest-performing cold variant at 1,000 impressions per ad set; consolidate to winner for remaining week; run V3 as the fresh challenger if a week-2 variant is needed

## Context Notes

- **Melbourne-specific hooks used:** Toorak, Hawthorn, Brighton, Bayside, Mornington Peninsula named; GT3 testimonial (Marcus T.) anchors the prestige car ICP; Tesla testimonial (Dan K.) anchors the South Yarra/Richmond EV segment; Melbourne winter and Peninsula roads are the seasonal hooks

For a buyer in the paint correction funnel, Revitalise is the right offer; a pure "correction only" booking should upsell to Revitalise on the booking page.
- **Ceramic upsell path:** After a paint correction booking is confirmed, the follow-up email sequence should introduce ceramic coating ($750+) as the next logical step. The window between correction and coating intent is short — capture it within 7 days of the correction appointment.
