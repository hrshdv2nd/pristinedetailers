**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for trusting us with the [car model/year — e.g. "3 Series"]. The team just finished and everything's looking sharp.

A quick heads-up on what's happening to your paint right now:

The ceramic coating is still curing. That process takes about 7 days to fully harden, and during that window there are a few things worth knowing:

**Don't wash the car for the next 7 days.** Even a rinse. The coating bonds to the clear coat during cure — water sitting on the surface during this phase can leave spots that are hard to remove later.

**If it rains, don't panic.** Melbourne in June does what it does. A light shower won't ruin the cure, but try not to let heavy rain sit on the paint without drying it off if you can. A quick microfibre wipe down is fine.

**Avoid bird droppings and tree sap sitting more than a few hours.** This applies forever, but it matters more right now. If something lands on the paint, lift it gently — don't rub.

After day 7, you'll start to notice the coating working: water sheeting off in sheets, dust not sticking the way it used to, and cleaning taking a fraction of the time.

Any questions before then — just reply to this email. We're easy to reach.

## Email 2 of 4 — Day 7

```
Send:    7 days after service completion
Subject: Your coating is fully cured. Here's how to keep it that way.
Preview: Winter in Melbourne is harder on paint than people think — here's what to watch.
```

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Seven days in — your coating has finished curing and it's now doing exactly what it was designed to do.

This is a good moment to talk about how to maintain it, because the mistakes people make in the first few months are the ones that matter most.

**Washing:**
Never use an automatic car wash with spinning brushes. Ever. Those brushes are the fastest way to put swirl marks through a coating that cost [price]-worth of professional prep. If you need a quick clean, a touchless wash is okay. Better: a two-bucket hand wash with a quality wash mitt.

**What to use:**
A pH-neutral car shampoo. Standard dish soap strips the coating's hydrophobic layer over time. Most people don't know this until the water stops beading.

**Melbourne-specific things to watch in winter:**
- Bird droppings. Magpies and wattlebirds are particularly active around the bayside suburbs right now, and their droppings are acidic enough to etch unprotected paint within hours. With your coating, you have a buffer — but don't leave them sitting.
- Road grime from the Monash and Eastern. Winter rain picks up oil and rubber off the road surface. A rinse after a highway run is worth it.
- Condensation under covered parking. Moisture trapped between the car and a cover causes more problems than outdoor parking. If you use a cover, make sure it breathes.

Your coating is doing its job. These habits just make sure it keeps doing it for the full 3–5 years.

If you ever want us to come back for a maintenance wash — we check the coating, top up any thin spots, and leave the car looking like day one — that's what we're here for.

## Sequence Notes

- **Personalise the car:** If CRM captures vehicle make/model, use it in Email 1 and Email 4. "Your 3 Series" lands differently than "your car."
- **Winter timing is an asset:** June–August in Melbourne is legitimately the hardest season on paint. Use it. The bird droppings, road grime, and condensation points are real — not manufactured urgency.
- **Sender name:** Send from a person (Tom, or the technician who did the job if trackable). "Pristine Detailers" as sender gets less opens than a name.
- **Exit condition:** Anyone who joins the membership should exit this sequence immediately and move to the Member Onboarding track. Don't pitch membership to someone who already has it.
- **Subject line A/B test candidates:**
  - Email 3: "Three weeks in — how's the car looking?" vs. "What your coating needs now (and what it doesn't)"
  - Email 4: "One small favour — and something in return" vs. "If you liked how your car turned out…"
