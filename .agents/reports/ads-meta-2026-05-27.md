# Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) — Paint Protection Film
**Date:** 2026-05-27 (Week 22 — PPF Focus)
**Platform:** Meta — Feed, Stories, Reels
**Prepared by:** Taylor, Paid Media Manager

---

## Campaign Context

**Service:** Paint Protection Film (PPF)
**Objective:** Conversion — drive `/booking` page submissions
**Audience:** Melbourne prestige car owners, Toorak / Bayside / Brighton / Hawthorn / Mornington Peninsula
**Key angles:** Freeway stone chip damage, resale value protection, mobile installation convenience
**Brand voice:** Expert, specific, Melbourne-native — not salesy

---

## Ad Variants

---

**Angle:** The Monash Freeway (and Eastern, Western Ring Road) systematically damage paint. This isn't bad luck — it's physics. PPF is the fix.

**Primary Text** (101 chars — feed-safe ✓)
> The Monash eats paint. One gravel chip and you're looking at a respray. PPF stops it. We come to you.

**Headline** (21 chars ✓)
> The Monash Eats Paint

**Description** (24 chars ✓)
> PPF. Mobile. Book Today.

**Visual brief:** Split image or short video — left: close-up of a stone chip on a dark-coloured bonnet (Porsche or BMW hood); right: same bonnet post-PPF installation, flawless. Text overlay: "One gravel chip. One respray bill." Shot in Melbourne — the Monash or a Toorak driveway in the background.

**CTA button:** Book Now → `pristinedetailers.com.au/booking`

> Your 911 is worth more with protected paint. PPF locks in resale value — and we wrap it at your driveway in Toorak.

**Headline** (23 chars ✓)
> Protect Your Investment

**Description** (24 chars ✓)

**Visual brief:** Aspirational lifestyle shot — a Porsche 911 or Tesla Model S in a Toorak driveway, early morning light. Clean, minimal. A technician applying PPF film in the background — branded van visible. No text overlay needed; let the image breathe.

**CTA button:** Learn More → `pristinedetailers.com.au/services` (or `/booking` for direct conversion test)

## Targeting

### Primary Audience — Cold Traffic

**Locations:**
- Melbourne metro, with bid concentration in:
  - Toorak 3142
  - South Yarra 3141
  - Brighton 3186
  - Hawthorn 3122
  - Kew 3101
  - Malvern 3144
  - St Kilda 3182
  - Richmond 3121
  - Mornington Peninsula (3930–3960 range)

**Age / Gender:** 28–58, all genders (skew M 60/40 based on car enthusiast audiences)

**Interests:**
- Luxury/prestige vehicles: BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi, Porsche, Tesla, Lexus, Land Rover
- Automotive: car detailing, car care, car modifications
- Lifestyle: luxury goods, property investment, golf (Toorak/Brighton lifestyle signal)

**Behaviours:**
- High household income (top 25% AU)
- Frequent travellers / business class (proxy for discretionary spend)
- Engaged shoppers — online high-value purchases

**Exclude:** People who have already submitted the `/booking` form (existing converters)

---

### Secondary Audience — Lookalike

- **1–2% Lookalike** based on existing customer email list (upload to Meta Custom Audiences)
- Restrict to Melbourne metro only
- Layer with: Luxury auto interest to tighten quality
- Serve Variants 1 and 3 (social proof and convenience angles perform well with lookalikes)

---

### Retargeting Audience — `/booking` Page Visitors (No Conversion)

**Audience definition:** Visited `pristinedetailers.com.au/booking` in last 14 days AND did not fire the conversion event

**Message:** Objection-handling. They were close — remove friction, remind them of the mobile convenience.

**Primary Text** (96 chars ✓)
> You were one step away. We'll still come to you — no workshop, no hassle. Book your PPF consult.

**Headline** (24 chars ✓)
> Still Interested in PPF?

**Description** (25 chars ✓)
> We Come to You. Book Now.

**Visual:** Same creative as Variant 3 (convenience angle) — reinforce the "no drop-off" message that may have been the hesitation point

**Frequency cap:** 3 impressions per person per week (retargeting fatigue sets in fast — don't overdo it)
**Window:** 14-day retargeting; exclude anyone who visited >14 days ago (intent has cooled)

## Creative Testing Plan

| Priority | Test | Hypothesis |
|----------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Variant 1 (threat) vs. Variant 2 (investment) | Do Melbourne car owners respond more to loss aversion or value gain? |
| 2 | Static image vs. Reel (Variant 3) | Video outperforms static for mobile-service demos |
| 3 | "Book Now" vs. "Get a Quote" CTA | Lower-commitment CTA may improve top-of-funnel volume |

**Test duration:** 7 days minimum, 500+ impressions per variant before calling a winner.

---

## Budget Recommendation

- **Daily budget:** $30–50 AUD cold traffic, $10–15 AUD retargeting
- **Bid strategy:** Cost per result (conversion) — let Meta optimise once pixel has 20+ events
- **Campaign structure:**
  - Campaign: PPF — Conversions
    - Ad Set 1: Cold — Interest + Lookalike (Variants 1, 2, 3)
    - Ad Set 2: Retargeting — /booking visitors (Retargeting ad)
