**The first 24 hours matter.**

The ceramic coating (or PPF adhesive, if that's what you went with) needs time to fully bond. In that window:

- Keep it out of direct sun where possible

Water will behave differently. In the rain, it beads and runs. Bird droppings and road dust won't stick the same way. That's the hydrophobic layer doing exactly what it's supposed to.

## Email 2 of 4 — Day 7: First-Week Care Tips

```
Send:    Day 7 post-service
Subject: One week in — a few habits worth building now
Preview: These are the things that separate a coating lasting 2 years from one lasting 7.
```

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Seven days in — your coating is fully cured. You're probably already noticing the difference in how the car sheds water.

Here's what to do (and avoid) to get the full lifespan out of it.

**Wash routine that won't degrade your coating:**

- **pH-neutral shampoo only.** Anything with wax additives or high alkalinity starts breaking down the hydrophobic layer over time. Ask us if you're unsure about your current product.
- **Two-bucket method** — one for shampoo, one for rinse. Keeps the grit you've picked up off your panels.
- **Microfibre cloths only.** No chamois, no dish towels, nothing abrasive.
- **Avoid automatic car washes with brushes.** The friction introduces swirl marks — it just takes longer to see on a coated surface.

**Melbourne-specific:**

If you're commuting on the Monash or the Eastern, you're picking up more road grime and brake dust than you'd think. A rinse every couple of weeks — even just a hose-down — removes abrasive particles before they bond to the surface.

Bird droppings are still your main enemy. Not because they'll penetrate the coating immediately, but because they're mildly acidic and, left sitting in direct sun for a few hours, they can etch into the clear coat above the coating layer. Wipe them off as soon as you spot them.

**Honest word on maintenance:**

A ceramic coating isn't maintenance-free. It's low-maintenance. The people who get 5–7 years out of theirs are the ones who wash it correctly and have it topped up annually.

We do maintenance washes specifically for coated cars — the right products, right technique. Worth considering once you've settled into a routine.

Any questions — reply here. Happy to answer.
