## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — New Financial Year / Fresh Start (Angle: You've been meaning to. Now you have a reason.)

**Primary text (116 chars):**
> New financial year. New budget. New reason to stop putting it off. Pristine Membership from $99/mo — we come to you.

**Headline (22 chars):**
> New Year. Fresh Paint.

**Description (24 chars):**
> Mobile care from $99/mo.

**Why this works:** July 1 is a cultural trigger for Melbourne professionals — the same people who do their super contributions, gym memberships, and subscription reviews in the first week of July. "New financial year. New budget." is how they think about this period; the copy names the moment rather than fighting it. "New reason to stop putting it off" is honest: there's no urgency countdown, no scarcity, just an acknowledgement that they've already decided to do this and July 1 is as good a reason as any to act. The CTA is embedded in the text ("from $99/mo — we come to you") so the headline can close with the emotion rather than repeat the product name.

**Note on character count:** Primary text is 116 chars. Full hook reads within the 125-char feed threshold — no "See more" truncation before the CTA.

### Variant 2 — Deferred Decision / Year-in-Review (Angle: Last year you meant to. This year it's done.)

**Primary text (124 chars):**
> You spent last year meaning to sort the car out. This year, it's sorted from July 1. Membership from $99/mo — we come to you.

**Headline (26 chars):**
> Membership Sorted. $99/mo.

**Description (27 chars):**
> We come to you. No dropoff.

**Why this works:** "You spent last year meaning to sort the car out" is the most common statement a Pristine prospect would make about their maintenance history — not that they don't care, they just kept delaying. The copy validates that pattern without judgment. "This year, it's sorted from July 1" reframes the membership as a resolved decision, not a commitment to be made. The past tense of "sorted" is deliberate — it implies the action is already complete once they click. "From $99/mo — we come to you" closes with the two facts that answer every remaining objection (price and logistics).

**Vehicle rotation plan (same structure, car-specific first line):**
- V2a: "You spent last year meaning to sort the BMW out. This year, it's sorted from July 1." — BMW interest targeting
- V2b: "You spent last year meaning to sort the Range Rover out. This year, it's sorted from July 1." — Land Rover/Range Rover interest
- V2c: "You spent last year meaning to sort the Porsche out. This year, it's sorted from July 1." — Porsche interest + Hawthorn/Kew postcodes
- V2d: "You spent last year meaning to sort the Tesla out. This year, it's sorted from July 1." — EV/Tesla interest + Richmond/Malvern

Each variant drops the specific make into the first sentence. Same structure, immediate relevance to the interest group being targeted.

### Variant 3 — Winter + Protection (Angle: July is the worst month for Melbourne paint.)

**Primary text (112 chars):**
> Winter in Melbourne is hard on paint. Bird droppings, grime, morning dew. Membership means it never accumulates.

**Headline (23 chars):**
> Winter Paint Protection

**Description (25 chars):**
> Monthly care from $99/mo.

**Why this works:** July 1 is also mid-Melbourne-winter — cold mornings, morning dew sitting on paint, bird activity in inner suburbs (particularly Toorak's tree-lined streets), and heavier road grime from wet weather. This variant doesn't mention the new financial year at all; it speaks to the seasonal, environmental condition the car is literally in right now. "Bird droppings, grime, morning dew" is a specific list — not abstract paint damage but the three concrete things sitting on the car this morning. "Membership means it never accumulates" closes the loop: the maintenance plan is the environmental countermeasure. Run this variant against Variant 1 to test whether the financial year trigger or the seasonal trigger drives higher CTR.

## Creative Direction Notes

**Image/video guidance:**
- **V1 and V2 (FY variants):** Clean, calm visual — a Toorak or South Yarra residential driveway, early morning, technician van pulling in. The "new start" feeling is environmental: still, quiet, professional. No bold text overlays needed on the hero image — the copy does the work.
- **V3 (winter variant):** Close-up of water beading on a dark-coloured bonnet (black or navy Range Rover or BMW) — the hydrophobic effect is instantly legible as "protected." Alternatively: a close-up of a leaf or water drop on an uncovered car bonnet with the caption "This is July in Melbourne." Short video loop works well here.
- **Vehicle rotation variants (V2a–V2d):** Where possible, match the hero image vehicle to the interest group — BMW image in the BMW ad set, Range Rover in the Land Rover set. Consistent ICP signal between visual and copy.
- **Avoid:** Summertime/sunshine imagery (it's July in Melbourne — this will look out of season). Generic "shiny car" stock photos without location context.

**Format priority for July 1 launch:**
1. Instagram Feed (V1, V2) — professional audience scrolling morning feed; FY/financial year language hits in this context
2. Facebook Feed (V3, retargeting) — broader reach for the winter/seasonal angle and older ICP segment
3. Instagram Reels (V3 time-lapse concept: 30-second winter grime building up vs. monthly Pristine visit keeping the car clean)
4. Stories (retargeting only) — FY nudge variant; swipe-up friction matches "takes 2 mins" messaging

**Copy test plan (first 7 days of July):**
- V1 (FY trigger) vs V3 (winter/seasonal) as cold audience in both Inner East and Bayside — primary test question: does the financial year frame or the seasonal frame drive higher CTR for membership acquisition?
- V2 vehicle variants run per interest group — consistent with June 28 approach; July 1 copy shifts the first line but the structure is proven
- Retargeting budget separate; FY-specific 48-hour variant is a one-off; revert to standard retargeting copy from July 3

**Expected vs June 28 creative:** V1 and V2 here will likely outperform the June 28 "maintenance-slipping" angle in the first week of July because the FY trigger is an external calendar prompt (not requiring the prospect to feel internal pain first). Test both; retire the weaker by July 8 based on CTR.
