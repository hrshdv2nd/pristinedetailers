| # | Description | Chars |

| D1 | We come to you. Certified coating for Porsche, Tesla, BMW. From $750. Book online. | 82 |
| D2 | 4.9 stars · 39 reviews. 6 years protecting Melbourne cars from birds, UV, stone chips. | 85 |

**Why D1 works:** Opens with the service model, not the product — "We come to you" is the first signal that separates Pristine from every fixed studio in the search results. Names the actual vehicles served (Porsche, Tesla, BMW) which does two things at once: validates expertise with expensive cars and acts as a self-selecting filter — an owner of a $30K Corolla self-selects out; a Porsche owner leans in. "Birds, UV, stone chips" names the exact threats a Melbourne car owner in Toorak or Bayside faces daily — specific, credible, not generic. The description doesn't explain ceramic coating; the buyer already knows what it is. It explains *why they should trust this provider* with their $100K car.

## Keyword Recommendations

### Exact Match — 5 Keywords

```
[ceramic coating Melbourne]
[mobile ceramic coating Melbourne]
[ceramic coating Toorak]
[ceramic car coating Melbourne]
[ceramic coating Bayside Melbourne]
```

**Rationale:**
- `[ceramic coating Melbourne]` — highest-volume primary term; buyer is solution-aware, actively comparing providers; must own this term to compete in the category
- `[mobile ceramic coating Melbourne]` — explicitly filters for Pristine's model; buyer has already decided they want mobile; this is the highest-intent subset of the category and should convert at a lower CPA than the broad term
- `[ceramic coating Toorak]` — suburb-specific queries indicate a buyer who is localised and ready to book; conversion rate typically higher than city-level terms; Toorak is Pristine's anchor suburb for premium work
- `[ceramic car coating Melbourne]` — captures buyers who use "car" as a modifier (slightly different search language); same intent, worth having in its own ad group with D1 matched against it
- `[ceramic coating Bayside Melbourne]` — second-priority geography after Inner East; Brighton, Hampton, and Sandringham searchers use this term; high-income suburb cluster with strong ICP density

---

### Negative Keywords — 3 to Exclude

```
[DIY ceramic coating]
[ceramic coating kit]
[ceramic coating spray]
```

**Rationale:**
- `[DIY ceramic coating]` — buyer is looking to self-apply; no service intent; will click out of curiosity, not to book; $0 revenue potential, pure wasted spend
- `[ceramic coating kit]` — searcher is buying a product, not a service; typically Meguiar's or Gyeon consumer-grade products; opposite of the certified professional application Pristine sells
- `[ceramic coating spray]` — spray-on consumer ceramic (marketed as "nano coating" in $40 bottles); completely different category; a buyer searching this is not mentally ready to pay $750 for professional application; will drag down quality score if they click and bounce immediately

## Context Notes

- **Melbourne-specific hooks used:** Toorak and Bayside named in keywords and descriptions; vehicles (Porsche, Tesla, BMW) signal Inner East and Bayside ICP; "birds, UV, stone chips" are real Melbourne-specific paint threats (bird droppings etch clear coat fast in summer, UV is severe near the bay, stone chips are common on the Monash and Eastern)
- **Tone check:** Technical and direct — "certified", "manufacturer warranty", "nano-ceramic" used accurately and only where they add meaning; no claim that ceramic coating stops stone chips (it doesn't — PPF does); no vague superlatives
- **Ceramic vs PPF:** This ad set does not mention PPF. The ceramic buyer is at a different price point and decision stage than the PPF buyer. Cross-selling belongs on the landing page, not in the ad — mixing the two products in a search ad confuses the intent match and drops quality score
- **Post-correction upsell path:** Paint Correction (week 28) was the upstream service. Buyers who booked paint correction in the last 30 days are the warmest ceramic coating prospects — they've already been told the surface is prepared and ready. A separate email sequence to this cohort (via Casey) would outperform any paid channel for this segment; flag to Jordan as a quick revenue lever
- **Seasonal relevance:** July is mid-winter in Melbourne — a strong ceramic coating window. Cars that have accumulated six months of UV, road grime, and bird damage since the last warm season have owners who are ready to reset. The before/after contrast is at its most visible in winter light. Early July bookings also beat the spring rush when ceramic coating demand peaks in September–October
