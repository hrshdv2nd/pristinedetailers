## Why Winter Is Harder on Your Paint Than You Think

The popular assumption is that summer UV is the biggest enemy of car paint. It is a real threat — but winter in Melbourne attacks paint differently, and often more aggressively.

Here's what's working against you between June and August:

| Threat | Why It's Worse in Winter | Urgency |
|---|---|---|
| Road grime and mineral deposits | Wet roads suspend grit and minerals; these bond to clear coat quickly on cold, damp surfaces | High — 48-hour bonding window |
| Bird droppings | Cold weather slows digestion, making droppings more acidic; they also sit longer because surfaces dry slower | Very high — etch marks can form in 24 hours |
| Bayside salt air | Winter winds off Port Phillip Bay carry fine salt particles inland; salt accelerates oxidation on unprotected clear coat | Moderate — cumulative over weeks |
| Reduced wash frequency | Most people wash less in winter; contamination sits longer without intervention | High — compounding damage |
| Overnight condensation | Morning dew and moisture trap airborne particles against the paint surface | Moderate — worsens bonding of other contaminants |

## The Bird Dropping Problem Is Worse Than You Think

Bird droppings are corrosive year-round, but in winter they become a more serious threat. In Melbourne's inner east — Hawthorn, Richmond, Toorak — large trees stay active with birds through winter. Droppings land on cold, slow-drying paint and sit undisturbed for hours or days.

The uric acid in fresh droppings has a pH between 3.5 and 4.5. Clear coat is rated for a pH of around 5 and above. Below that threshold, the acid starts dissolving the surface. In summer, dry air can actually slow this process by drying the dropping before it penetrates. In winter, with slow-drying cold-damp conditions, you lose that buffer.

The rule is simple: remove bird droppings within 10 minutes if you can. In winter, assume every dropping left overnight has etched.

## What Bayside Drivers Need to Know

If you park outdoors in Brighton, Sandringham, Beaumaris, or anywhere near the bay, you're dealing with an additional winter threat: salt air.

Winter westerlies push fine salt particles across the foreshore and into inner suburbs. These particles are invisible, but they settle on paint and begin accelerating surface oxidation over weeks. Unprotected clear coat in Bayside suburbs can show early oxidation signs within a single winter season.

A ceramic coating changes this significantly — the hydrophobic surface means salt particles don't bond the same way — but even coated cars need regular washing to clear accumulated deposits.

## What to Do Right Now

You don't need an expensive treatment to keep your paint in good shape through winter. You need a consistent cadence.

**Wash every 2–3 weeks minimum.** In winter, that's the baseline for Melbourne conditions. If you're in Bayside, or if your car parks outdoors overnight, every 2 weeks.

**Use a pH-neutral car shampoo.** Dish soap and generic cleaners strip your sealant. Every wash with the wrong product undoes the protection you've already paid for.

**Apply a spray sealant after each wash.** If your car doesn't have a ceramic coating, a quality spray sealant gives you a meaningful barrier against mineral bonding and acid damage.

**Remove bird droppings immediately.** Keep a detail spray and microfibre in the car. This is the single highest-value habit you can build.

**Book a decontamination detail before August.** By the end of winter, iron fallout, road tar, and mineral deposits have built up. A proper decontamination removes what regular washing can't reach.

## FAQ

**Should I get ceramic coating in winter?**
Yes. Melbourne winters are well-suited to ceramic coating application — ambient temperatures are stable, and moderate humidity helps the coating cure evenly. We coat cars year-round without issue. The only adjustment: confirm your car is stored undercover for the 7-day cure period after application.

**Does rain damage car paint?**
By itself, no — clean water doesn't harm paint. The problem is what rain carries and what it leaves behind. Melbourne rainwater picks up airborne pollutants and deposits them as water evaporates, leaving mineral residue on the surface. A protected car sheds water cleanly; an unprotected car accumulates these deposits with every shower.

**How often should I wash my car in Melbourne winter?**
Every 2–4 weeks, depending on your situation. Inner-city drivers parking under trees: every 2 weeks. Bayside drivers parking outdoors: every 2 weeks. Garaged cars with minimal freeway use: every 4 weeks. If water is beading and sheeting off after rain, your protection is still active. If it's sheeting flat, wash and reseal.
