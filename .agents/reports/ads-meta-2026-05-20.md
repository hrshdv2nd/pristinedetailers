# Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) — Ceramic Coating Focus
**Date:** 2026-05-20 (Wednesday — Week 21)
**Week:** 21 | **Service Focus:** Ceramic Coating (Week 5 rotation)
**Platform:** Meta — Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed + Stories
**Campaign:** META_Conv_Feed_CeramicCoating_MelbInnerEast-Bayside_2026W21
**Landing Page:** /booking

**Context:** First Meta ceramic coating set for Week 21. May in Melbourne is peak ceramic coating season: deciduous trees in Toorak and Hawthorn are dropping leaves and sap, bird activity spikes, and autumn rain returns after summer. The hydrophobic sell is both visually demonstrable and seasonally urgent. Three angles: seasonal/environmental (autumn threat to paint), technical credibility (not a wax — it's chemistry), and mobile convenience (no workshop, no drive across town). The Google set from Monday already runs search intent — Meta's job here is building brand familiarity and demand with the right postcodes before they search.

---

## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Angle: Seasonal / Autumn Melbourne Threat

**Primary Text (121 chars):**
> Autumn in Toorak: bird droppings, tree sap, rain. Ceramic coating bonds to your clear coat. We apply it in your driveway.

**Headline (21 chars):**
> Protect Before Winter

**Description (25 chars):**
> Applied in your driveway.

**Char counts:** Primary 121 ✓ | Headline 21 ✓ | Description 25 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- Opens with a hyper-local line — "Autumn in Toorak" stops the scroll for anyone in that postcode cluster; it's their street, their trees, their paint being described
- Three threats separated by commas land fast and are visually scannable; bird droppings + tree sap are the highest-urgency seasonal threats right now (acid etching risk within hours on unprotected clear coat)
- "Bonds to your clear coat" is the technical truth that separates ceramic from a spray wax — earns credibility without claiming "best" or "premium"
- "We apply it in your driveway" is the USP close — no drop-off, no waiting room, no driving
- Best format: Facebook Feed with a square (1:1) close-up shot of a protected surface with water beading on a rainy Toorak street — real Melbourne setting, not studio
- Run May through July while the seasonal context holds; swap "Autumn" to "Winter" from mid-June

### Variant 2 — Angle: Technical Credibility / Not A Wax

**Primary Text (123 chars):**
> A ceramic coating isn't a wax. It's a chemical bond with your paint — 3 to 8 years. Mobile, certified, Melbourne. Book now.

**Headline (27 chars):**
> Not A Wax. A Chemical Bond.

**Description (26 chars):**
> 3–8 year protection. Book.

**Char counts:** Primary 123 ✓ | Headline 27 ✓ | Description 26 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- This ad leads with the single most common misconception ("it's just a spray wax") and corrects it in four words — that tension earns the click from an informed buyer
- "Chemical bond with your paint" is technically precise: ceramic forms a Si-O-C covalent bond with the clear coat; this language resonates with the 35–55 ICP who has already done some research and wants a detailer who speaks like a professional
- "Mobile, certified, Melbourne" packs three key qualifiers into four words at the end — doesn't need expansion, just confirmation
- Best format: Instagram Feed with a split image — a shelf of DIY spray cans on the left, a technician's kit and GYEON/Gtechniq coating bottle on the right, no text overlay
- Target this variant to anyone who has visited /journal posts on ceramic coating — they're already educated, this ad speaks their language
- Headline "Not A Wax. A Chemical Bond." can run as a hook in a 15-second Reel with a paint-thickness gauge visual

### Variant 3 — Angle: Mobile Convenience / No Workshop

**Primary Text (113 chars):**
> Your car doesn't drive to us. We bring the ceramic coating to your driveway. No workshop. Just booked, then done.

**Headline (27 chars):**
> No Workshop. We Come to You

**Description (27 chars):**
> Inner East & Bayside. Book.

**Char counts:** Primary 113 ✓ | Headline 27 ✓ | Description 27 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- Directly contrasts fixed-location detailing studios without naming them — "your car doesn't drive to us" inverts the expectation and surfaces the competitor model as the friction
- Time-poor ICP (professionals, 28–55, South Yarra and Brighton) respond to convenience copy; this is the most frictionless message in the set
- "No workshop. Just booked, then done." — the double period cadence creates the "before/after" feeling inside a single sentence
- Best format: Short video (15 sec) showing a Pristine van arriving at a Bayside driveway, technician setting up, car emerging protected — no voiceover needed, just the sequence with captions matching the primary text
- Instagram Stories and Reels: crop primary text to the first sentence as the opening caption; let the visual carry the rest
- This variant wins on cold audiences who don't yet know Pristine; test against Variant 1 for CPM efficiency

### Lookalike Audience

- **Seed:** Export Pristine customer list filtered to ceramic coating + PPF purchasers (highest LTV jobs); upload as custom audience
- **LAL size:** Start at 1% in VIC for prospecting (tightest match to ICP); expand to 2% if CPM exceeds $12 AUD at 1%
- **Exclude:** Active Pristine members, anyone who converted in the last 30 days, visitors who bounced within 10 seconds

### Budget Allocation (Suggested — Prospecting)

- Split initial budget equally across three variants × two interest stacks (Stacks A and B) = 6 combinations
- After 500 impressions per combination, pause the bottom 2 on CTR
- Allocate 20% of total spend to retargeting (audience is warm and will convert at lower CPA)

## Retargeting — /booking Non-Converters

**Audience:** Visited /booking but did not complete a booking (7–14 day window)
**Frequency cap:** 4x per week maximum
**Window:** 7–14 days post-visit
**Exclusion:** Anyone who completed a booking in the same window

**Primary Text (105 chars):**
> You checked our booking page. Ceramic coating — chemical bond, 3–8 years. We come to you. One more click.

**Headline (27 chars):**
> Your Booking's Still There.

**Description (21 chars):**
> Ceramic at your door.

**Char counts:** Primary 105 ✓ | Headline 27 ✓ | Description 21 ✓

**Notes:**
- "You checked our booking page" mirrors their exact behaviour — the moment of recognition creates a mental connection; this is honest and direct, not creepy
- "One more click" is the softest possible CTA — no urgency fabrication, no false scarcity; it respects that they were interested and just didn't complete
- Pair with a static image: Pristine-branded van parked on a recognisable inner Melbourne street (Orrong Rd, Church St, Bay St Brighton) — the location makes it feel local, not generic
- If Pristine is running a first-booking incentive or seasonal promotion, add it after "3–8 years": "...3–8 years. First booking 10% off this week. We come to you."
- Do not show this to anyone who viewed the retargeting ad 3+ times without clicking — move them to a longer-window cold re-engagement segment instead
