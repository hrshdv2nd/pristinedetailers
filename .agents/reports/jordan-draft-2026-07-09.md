# Winter Car Care Melbourne: Why June and July Are the Worst Months for Your Paint

In Melbourne, winter road grime accumulates 3–4× faster than summer because wet roads kick up mineral deposits that bond to clear coat within 48 hours.

That's not a scare tactic — it's chemistry. Dry roads allow debris to bounce off your car. Wet roads don't. Mineral-rich water from Melbourne's road surfaces dries into a thin, abrasive film on your clear coat that most people mistake for ordinary dust. Leave it long enough and it etches in permanently.

June and July are Melbourne's worst months for paint care — not because of the rain itself, but because of what the rain carries and leaves behind.

## Melbourne-Specific Winter Hazards

**Inner-city road grime.** The arterials that Melbourne professionals drive every day — Punt Road, Swan Street, Toorak Road, Alexandra Avenue — carry dense traffic that churns mineral-laden surface water. A wet commute through Richmond or South Yarra deposits more contamination than a week of dry summer driving. If you park on the street overnight, that layer starts bonding by the time you wake up.

**Bayside salt air.** Brighton, Hampton, and Sandringham residents deal with salt air year-round. But Melbourne's winter humidity holds sodium chloride on your paint surface longer and carries it further inland. Salt doesn't just attack metal — it degrades clear coat by cycling moisture in and out of microscopic surface pores over time. Regular rinsing is the only defence.

**Bird droppings in cold weather.** You've probably heard "remove bird droppings immediately." In winter, the urgency is different — not less. Cold air slows evaporation, which means droppings stay wet and chemically active on the paint surface longer. In Toorak, Hawthorn, and Armadale, native birds are constant. Uric acid at pH 3–4.5 starts etching clear coat at room temperature within minutes on warm paint. On a 12°C Melbourne morning, it may feel less urgent. It's not.

## What to Do Right Now

**Wash every 2–3 weeks.** In summer, most Melbourne cars need washing every 4–6 weeks. In winter — especially if you park outdoors, drive inner-city routes, or live near the bay — pull that forward to every 2–3 weeks. The goal is removing mineral deposits before they bond to the clear coat, not after.

**Don't use a drive-through car wash.** Most use alkaline cleaners that strip your existing wax or sealant protection with each visit. A pH-neutral hand wash or maintenance detail preserves that barrier. Without a sealant layer, you're washing road grime off bare clear coat — better than not washing, but worse than it needs to be.

**Book a paint decontamination.** If your car hasn't had an iron fallout and clay bar treatment in the past 12 months, winter is the right time. Iron particles from brake dust and road fallout embed in your clear coat and oxidise. A decontamination service removes them before another four months of Melbourne winter drives them deeper.

**Treat bird droppings as an emergency.** Keep a quick-detailer spray and a clean microfibre in your car. If you see droppings, blot — don't wipe. Wiping pushes particulates across the surface. Blotting lifts them.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I get a ceramic coating in winter?**
Yes — with one condition. Ceramic coating requires a 7-day cure period away from moisture after application. If your car is garaged or kept undercover, winter is a perfectly viable time to apply. Once cured, winter maintenance becomes significantly easier: water, bird droppings, and mineral deposits bead off rather than bonding to the surface.

**Does rain damage car paint?**
Rain alone doesn't damage clear coat. Rain combined with road grime, industrial fallout, and bird droppings does. The risk is what the rain carries and leaves behind when it dries — not the water itself. Wash after rain, not before it.

**How often should I wash my car in Melbourne winter?**
Every 2–3 weeks if you park outdoors or drive inner-city routes in the Inner East or Bayside suburbs. Every 3–4 weeks for garaged vehicles. If you park under trees in Hawthorn or Toorak, or near the bay in Brighton or Sandringham, wash closer to weekly and treat any bird droppings the moment you see them.
