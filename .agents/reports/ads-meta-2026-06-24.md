## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Problem-Agitate-Solve (Angle: The M1 is chipping your paint every single commute)

**Primary text (104 chars):**
> Every trip on the M1 is a chip lottery. PPF ends that game. We install at your home — Toorak to Bayside.

**Headline (27 chars):**
> Invisible Film. Real Shield

**Description (22 chars):**
> Book mobile PPF today.

**Why this works:** "Chip lottery" reframes the daily commute from accepted risk to a winnable game with a specific solution. The M1 is the Eastlink/Eastern Freeway corridor — Toorak, Hawthorn, Richmond, and South Yarra residents hit it every day. This isn't abstract fear; it's a named, lived experience. "PPF ends that game" delivers the solution in four words, with no preamble. "We install at your home" removes the only remaining objection: the inconvenience of a workshop appointment. The suburb range (Toorak to Bayside) confirms geographic relevance for inner-east and Bayside audiences simultaneously — use postcode-level targeting to serve this variant to the right postcodes, not both at once.

**Vehicle test variants:** Run "your BMW" / "your Porsche" / "your Audi" / "your Tesla" in the primary text to match against interest-segmented audiences. Example:
- V1a: "Every M1 trip chips a bit of your Porsche. PPF ends that. We install at your place."
- V1b: "Your Tesla's paint isn't immune to the M1. PPF films every panel. We come to Toorak."

### Variant 2 — Resale Value / Investment (Angle: The first chip costs you at trade-in)

**Primary text (110 chars):**
> Your Porsche wears every chip from the Monash. PPF stops that before it reaches paint. We come to South Yarra.

**Headline (25 chars):**
> Protect What You Paid For

**Description (22 chars):**
> Certified PPF. Mobile.

**Why this works:** "Wears every chip" positions the car as accumulating damage — not dramatically, but steadily, the way it actually happens. "The Monash" is exact: South Yarra, Malvern, and Hawthorn residents commute the Monash daily; Porsche GTS and Cayman owners in those suburbs know the road. "Before it reaches paint" is the technical precision that earns credibility — PPF is a sacrificial layer above the clear coat, not a coating on the paint itself. This accuracy signals expertise and filters out casual readers. "South Yarra" as the drop-in suburb ties the mobile service to a real postcode — swap to "Brighton" or "Hawthorn" for the respective ad sets.

**Vehicle rotation plan:**
- V2a: "Your Porsche wears every chip from the Monash…" — Porsche interest group + Toorak/South Yarra postcodes
- V2b: "Your BMW wears every chip from the Eastern…" — BMW interest group + Hawthorn/Kew postcodes
- V2c: "Your Audi wears every chip from the Nepean…" — Audi interest group + Mornington Peninsula postcodes
- V2d: "Your Tesla wears every chip from the M1…" — Tesla/EV interest group + Richmond/South Yarra postcodes

Rotate the freeway name to match the suburb: Eastern for inner east, Monash for southeast, Nepean for Mornington Peninsula.

### Variant 3 — Identity (Angle: Premium Melbourne suburbs deserve protection installed at home)

**Primary text (105 chars):**
> Brighton driveways. Toorak car parks. Mornington weekenders. PPF installed where your car actually lives.

**Headline (23 chars):**
> No Studio. No Drop-Off.

**Description (27 chars):**
> Melbourne's Mobile PPF Pros

**Why this works:** Three location-context pairs paint a specific picture without saying "we're mobile." The reader sees their own situation — their Brighton driveway, their Toorak car park, their Mornington weekender — before any feature is mentioned. "Where your car actually lives" closes with a rhythm that makes the mobile service feel intuitive, not like a compromise. "No Studio. No Drop-Off." — the headline removes the two biggest mental friction points in a single line. It's the objection-kill, not the pitch.

**Mornington Peninsula note:** The "weekender" reference is intentional — Peninsula residents have vehicles that do the Nepean Hwy run; these are often higher-value cars (convertibles, weekend sports cars) that are kept in secondary garages and not typically serviced locally. PPF is a strong angle for this audience because the Peninsula road surface and salt air are active threats. Run a standalone Peninsula ad set replacing "Toorak car parks" with "Portsea garages."

## Retargeting — /booking Page Abandoners

For users who visited `/booking` but did not complete (window: 7 days):

**Primary text (111 chars):**
> Your paint's still unprotected. You got to the booking page — the hard part's done. Takes 2 minutes to confirm.

**Headline (20 chars):**
> Spots Are Going Fast

**Description (27 chars):**
> Finish your PPF booking now

**Targeting window:** 7 days post-visit (hot intent)
**Frequency cap:** 3× per week maximum — these are warm leads; over-serving is counterproductive
**Exclusions:** Suppress anyone who completed a booking after the page visit — immediate exclusion on conversion event

**Why this works:** "Your paint's still unprotected" ties the retargeting message directly back to the PPF intent signal — they visited a PPF booking page, so unprotected paint is the specific concern. "You got to the booking page — the hard part's done" validates that they were serious, removes the mental friction of starting again, and reframes 2 minutes as the only remaining investment. "Takes 2 minutes to confirm" directly addresses the most common abandonment driver: the form felt like it required decisions and time. Specificity about time removes that uncertainty. No discount, no countdown timer — this audience already has high intent; urgency comes from scarcity ("Spots Are Going Fast"), not manufactured pressure.

## Creative Direction Notes

**Image/video guidance:**
- Hero visual: certified technician in gloves applying PPF film to the hood of a European vehicle in a Toorak or Brighton residential setting — morning light, leafy street, driveway context unmistakably Melbourne inner suburbs
- PPF application process video: the squeegee pass removing the air bubble from the film, the edge wrap around a door panel — process credibility signals expertise before the CTA; no studio required in the shot
- Before/after hood: one half shows light surface chip damage visible in angled direct light (the kind you only notice at the petrol station); the other half shows the same hood under PPF — immaculate, glass-like. Caption: "Both sides. Same commute."
- Water bead test (PPF version): demonstrate hydrophobic top-coating on PPF surface — water sheets off identically to ceramic, because premium PPF has a built-in top coat. This comparison visual works strongly for Instagram Reels.
- Avoid: generic stock car-detailing images without the mobile, residential Melbourne context. This audience can tell stock from real.

**Format priority for PPF:**
1. Instagram Feed (static or carousel) — primary; before/after carousel is high-performing for PPF because the difference is visible even at thumbnail size
2. Facebook Feed — secondary; broader prospecting reach, pairs well with V3 identity copy
3. Instagram Reels — PPF application process video; native format for a visually satisfying process
4. Stories — retargeting only; single-image with the booking abandoner copy; low-friction format for a high-intent warm audience

**Copy test plan (weeks 1–2):**
- Run V1 (chip lottery/PAS) against V3 (identity/suburb) as cold audience — both are awareness-to-consideration plays for different segments of the same ICP
- V2 (vehicle/Monash) as a challenger per vehicle interest group — tests whether naming the specific vehicle and specific road converts better than the generic angle
- Retargeting runs independently; exclude from cold test budget and measurement
- Kill lowest-CTR cold variant at 1,000 impressions per ad set; scale winner with fresh visual creative on the same copy angle
- If V2 vehicle variants show >25% CTR spread between Porsche and Tesla, redistribute budget toward the strongest vehicle angle and expand reach within that interest group

**Next creative test (if budget allows in week 3):**

Financial comparison angle. Positions PPF as the rational cost-avoidance decision rather than a luxury purchase. Relevant for buyers who are PPF-aware but haven't made the value calculation.
