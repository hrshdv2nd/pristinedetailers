## Campaign Notes

**Bid strategy:** Maximise Conversions (learning phase). After 50+ conversions, shift to Target CPA — expected range $35–$65 for a high-ticket service.

**Ad group structure:** Run Variant A (June 10) and Variant B (this file) as two RSAs within the same ad group. Google will prioritise the higher-performing combination over time. Do not manually split budget — let the algorithm find winners.

**Location bid adjustments:** +25% on postcodes 3142 (Toorak), 3186 (Brighton), 3141 (South Yarra), 3122 (Hawthorn). These postcodes consistently index highest for our ICP.

**Ad schedule:** Weight towards weekday evenings (6–9pm) and Saturday mornings — when car owners are at home, notice their paint, and have time to book. Reduce bids during business hours Monday–Friday.

**Device:** Mobile-first — 65%+ of local service searches happen on mobile. Confirm `/booking` loads under 2 seconds on 4G before launching.

**Creative refresh trigger:** If Variant B CTR trails Variant A by >20% after 1,000 impressions, retire H2 ("Bird Drop Etching? We Fix It") first — test replacement: "Restore Your Clear Coat" (24 chars) for buyers at a less incident-specific stage.

## Melbourne Context Notes

**New angle tested this set:** Bird drop etching is a specific, Melbourne-summer problem — acidic droppings from mynas and rosellas near plane trees etch clear coat within hours on a hot day. Toorak Rd, St Kilda Rd, and the inner east are high-exposure corridors. This is a painful, specific problem most premium car owners have experienced — naming it directly in H2 should improve resonance with this exact audience.

**The "one session" framing in H4** addresses a common objection for paint correction buyers: "will I need to come back multiple times?" One visit at your driveway removes the two biggest friction points (time, inconvenience) simultaneously.
