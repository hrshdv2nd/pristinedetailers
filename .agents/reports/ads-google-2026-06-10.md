## Campaign Notes

**Bid strategy:** Maximise Conversions to start (gather data). Switch to Target CPA after 50+ conversions — expected CPA range $35–$65 given high-ticket service value.

**Budget signal:** Paint correction is a considered purchase — allow 7–14 day conversion windows. Don't optimise for same-day conversions only.

**Location bid adjustments:** +25% on Toorak (3142), Brighton (3186), South Yarra (3141), Hawthorn (3122). These postcodes index highest for our ICP (professional, 30K+ vehicle, time-poor).

**Ad schedule:** Weight towards weekday evenings (6–9pm) and Saturday mornings — when car owners notice their paint and have headspace to book. Reduce bids during business hours Monday–Friday.

**Device:** Mobile-first — 65%+ of premium local service searches happen on mobile. Ensure `/booking` landing page loads under 2 seconds on 4G.

**Match type discipline:** Exact match only for first 30 days. Review search term report weekly. Open to phrase match on [mobile paint correction] and [swirl removal] once search terms confirm clean intent.

**Creative refresh trigger:** If CTR drops below 5% or impression share falls, rotate H4 ("Car Park Scratches Removed") to H4 alt: "Freeway Swirls Erased" (24 chars) — captures M1/Nepean Hwy damage angle for the same suburban buyer.

## Melbourne Context Notes

Paint correction in this market is driven by three specific damage sources worth referencing in future creative iterations:
- **Car park scratches** — underground car parks in South Yarra, Toorak Village, Brighton shops
- **Bird drop etching** — plane trees along St Kilda Rd and Toorak Rd leave acidic deposits that etch clear coat within hours on Melbourne summer days
- **Freeway swirl marks** — stone chips and road grit from M1, Monash, and Nepean Hwy surface at 100km/h

These are all "invisible until they're not" problems — they matter most to buyers who have already noticed the damage and are now motivated to fix it. Search intent here is high and specific.
