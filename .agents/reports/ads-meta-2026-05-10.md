# Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) — Membership Focus (Fresh Variants)
**Date:** 2026-05-10 (Sunday — week-end prep)
**Week:** 19 | **Service Focus:** Membership (Week 3 rotation)
**Platform:** Meta — Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed + Stories
**Campaign:** META_Conv_Membership_MelbBayside-InnerEast_2026W19
**Landing Page:** /membership

**Context:** Fresh creative variants to A/B test alongside the 2026-05-06 set. New angles: financial logic (cost vs. value), Melbourne road environment (Monash/Port Phillip), and ceramic coating maintenance. Retargeting message is also fresh — targets hesitation/delay rather than bare re-engagement.

Visual recommendation across all variants: a clean European or luxury SUV photographed in a real Melbourne setting — Toorak driveway, Brighton foreshore car park, or Hawthorn side street. Natural midday light. No studio. Technician visible but not posed. The car is the subject.

## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Angle: Financial Logic (Prevention vs. Cure)

**Primary Text (117 chars):**
> One swirl removal costs more than 3 months of membership. Pristine members stay ahead — monthly care, we come to you.

**Headline (24 chars):**
> Stop Paying More, Later.

**Description (27 chars):**
> Monthly plan, no surprises.

**Char counts:** Primary 117 ✓ | Headline 24 ✓ | Description 27 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- Lead with the number contrast — "3 months of membership" is a concrete comparison that stops the scroll
- Best in Facebook Feed where users slow down to read; pair with a before/after image showing swirl damage vs. corrected paint
- The period after "Later" creates a firm, confident pause — don't soften it

### Variant 2 — Angle: Melbourne Environment (Roads as the Antagonist)

**Primary Text (108 chars):**
> The Monash takes paint. Port Phillip takes polish. Melbourne's roads are relentless — your membership isn't.

**Headline (24 chars):**
> Melbourne Roads Hit Hard.

**Description (25 chars):**
> Come to you. Every month.

**Char counts:** Primary 108 ✓ | Headline 24 ✓ | Description 25 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- Opening two sentences are punchy parallel structure — strong hook for Stories and Reels
- The Monash and Port Phillip are immediately recognisable to every Melbourne driver — no explanation needed
- Pair with a close-up shot of paint damage (stone chips, water spots from bay mist) transitioning to a restored, protected surface
- Test with and without the period after "Hard" — period lands harder on desktop, comma may perform better on mobile

### Variant 3 — Angle: Coating Maintenance (Service Upsell / Informed Buyer)

**Primary Text (107 chars):**
> Ceramic coating on your car? Monthly maintenance is non-negotiable. Pristine Members Club — we come to you.

**Headline (25 chars):**
> Keep Your Ceramic Coating.

**Description (25 chars):**
> Members get monthly care.

**Char counts:** Primary 107 ✓ | Headline 25 ✓ | Description 25 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- Targets car owners who already have ceramic coating — they understand the investment and know maintenance matters

- Pair with an image of a hydrophobic water bead shot on a coated bonnet — the "proof of care" visual that coating owners recognise immediately
- This variant works well in a Custom Audience retargeting any visitor to `/journal` posts on ceramic coating

- **Exclude:** Current active members, anyone who converted in the last 30 days, bounced visitors (<10 sec session)

## Retargeting — /booking Non-Converters

**Audience:** Visited /booking but did not complete (7-day window — warm, high intent)
**Frequency cap:** 4x per week
**Window:** 7–14 days post-visit

**Ad Copy:**

**Primary Text (109 chars):**
> Your car won't wait. That booking page you left open — your neighbours in Toorak won't wait either. Join now.

**Headline (21 chars):**
> Your Spot Won't Last.

**Description (23 chars):**
> Lock in membership now.

**Char counts:** Primary 109 ✓ | Headline 21 ✓ | Description 23 ✓

**Notes:**
- This message introduces social proof/FOMO with "your neighbours in Toorak" — different angle from the 05-06 retargeting message which was a straight re-engagement nudge
- "Booking page you left open" mirrors their exact behaviour back at them — creates a moment of recognition without being creepy
- If Pristine offers a first-month incentive, add it here: "...won't wait either. First month half-price. Join now." — confirm with business before activating
- Match this to a static image: a Pristine-branded vehicle parked in a recognisable inner Melbourne street, no tech visible — the membership is the focus

## Budget Recommendation

- **Testing (first 7 days):** Equal split across 3 variants × 3 interest stacks (9 combinations)
- **Scaling:** After 500+ impressions per combination, pause bottom 3 on CTR; double spend on top 2
- **Retargeting budget:** 20% of total campaign spend — warm audience, CPM will be lower, conversion rate higher
- **Variant 3 note:** Run against Custom Audience (/journal ceramic readers) with a separate micro-budget before scaling to cold audience

---

## A/B Test Note vs. 05-06 Creative

The May 6 set used: pain point (neglect), identity (suburb pride), and simplicity/value-stacking. This set tests: financial logic, environmental threat, and product-sophistication (coating maintenance). Run both sets simultaneously at equal budget and identify which angle theme drives lower CPA after 1,000+ impressions each.
