## Seasonal Context (May — Melbourne Autumn / Early Winter)

- Bird droppings increase in autumn as birds settle into suburban street trees. Droppings are acidic and etch clear coats in as little as 48 hours; on a ceramic coating, the coating takes the damage instead of the paint — but not indefinitely.
- Wet fallen leaves deposit tannins onto bonnets and roofs overnight. Tannin staining is underrated by most car owners and accelerates coating degradation.
- Autumn rain doesn't clean — it redistributes brake dust, industrial fallout, and grime across the paint surface.
- Bayside and Mornington Peninsula customers: salty air from Port Phillip Bay accelerates contamination bonding.
- Cars that haven't had a pH-neutral wash + decontamination since a ceramic coating appointment are likely losing hydrophobic performance.

Hi [First Name],

It's been roughly three months since we came out to [suburb / their address if available] — which means your ceramic coating has been working on its own since then.

The good news: it's probably still intact.

The less good news: May is one of the harder months on coated cars in Melbourne, and most people don't realise it until the damage is already done.

Here's what we're seeing on cars that haven't had a maintenance wash since their coating appointment:

**Bird droppings.** Autumn means more birds in suburban trees, and their droppings are acidic enough to etch a clear coat in under 48 hours. On a ceramic coating, the coating absorbs the damage instead of your paint — but leave deposits long enough and they bond into the surface permanently. The coating starts failing from that point, not protecting.

**Tannins from wet leaves.** A wet maple leaf sitting on your bonnet overnight is more corrosive than most car owners expect. The tannins leach into whatever's underneath. Over time, this is one of the main things that ages a coating prematurely.

**Contamination the rain won't shift.** Autumn rain feels like it cleans your car. It doesn't. Brake dust, industrial fallout, and road grime don't wash off with water — they embed into the coating surface and reduce its hydrophobic properties. That's why your car probably isn't shedding water the way it did the day we finished it.

None of this means your coating has failed. It means it needs attention before the damage becomes harder to reverse.

A maintenance wash and decontamination takes about 90 minutes at your place. We come to you, same as before. If the coating needs a top-up, we'll let you know.

— The Pristine Detailers team
