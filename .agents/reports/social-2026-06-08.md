**Caption:**

Most ceramic coating failures aren't coating failures. They're maintenance failures.

After working on cars across Melbourne — from Toorak BMWs to business fleet Teslas in the CBD — the most common reason a coating underperforms its rated lifespan is what happens after the application. Specifically: the wash routine. Alkaline-heavy shampoos degrade SiO2 bonds over time. A coating rated for 5 years can be functionally compromised in under 18 months if the owner is using a generic degreaser-style product. The chemistry isn't complicated — pH matters — but it's rarely explained at point of sale.

For business owners and fleet managers investing in ceramic coating as asset protection (which it is — coating a $120K vehicle is long-term insurance, not a luxury), the maintenance protocol is as critical as the application itself. A degraded coating means reapplication costs every 12–18 months instead of every 5–8 years. The numbers on doing it right are straightforward.
