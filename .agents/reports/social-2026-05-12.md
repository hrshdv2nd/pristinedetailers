## Platform: LinkedIn

**Caption:**

Most Melbourne car owners assume their car has "faded with age." In almost every case, that's not what's happening.

The clear coat on a modern vehicle is designed to protect the colour underneath — but it's not immune to damage. Three to five years of Eastlink and Peninsula Link gravel, servo brush washes, tight underground car park turns, and Melbourne's summer UV leaves the clear coat covered in micro-scratches and swirl marks. These scatter light instead of reflecting it, which is why a five-year-old car looks dull next to a new one — even if the paint itself is untouched.

Paint correction is the process of machine polishing that clear coat back to a flat, reflective surface. It's not a wax or a one-pass polish. Done properly by a trained technician, it removes the actual damage rather than masking it. The result is paint that looks as it did from the factory — often better, once contamination is stripped back.

For business owners with company vehicles or executives running European and luxury cars, paint correction before a ceramic coating is the difference between a protection layer that lasts three years and one that lasts eight. We come to your office or building car park across Melbourne's inner east, Bayside, and the Mornington Peninsula. No downtime. No dropping keys. Just a measurable improvement in finish and long-term resale value.
