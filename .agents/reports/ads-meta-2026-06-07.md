# Meta Ads — Membership
**Date:** 2026-06-07 (launch Thursday 2026-06-12)
**Week:** 23 — Rotation: Membership (weeks 3 & 7)
**Platforms:** Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed
**Campaign objective:** Lead generation / landing page conversions
**Landing page:** `/membership`
**Target location:** Melbourne, VIC — inner east, Bayside, Mornington Peninsula postcodes

---

## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Problem-Agitate-Solve (Angle: Melbourne destroys car paint)

**Primary text (119 chars):**
> Your car lives outside in Melbourne. Bird drops, UV, Eastlink gravel. A monthly membership handles it — we come to you.

**Headline (24 chars):**
> Car Care On Subscription

**Description (25 chars):**
> We come to you. Book now.

**Why this works:** Opens with a Melbourne-specific observation — Eastlink gravel, UV, bird drops hit Toorak and Bayside cars every day. Doesn't pitch, just frames the reality. The membership is presented as the obvious resolution, not a sales push.

---

### Variant 2 — Identity / Outcome (Angle: Members don't chase bookings)

**Primary text (107 chars):**
> Members get priority. Monthly detail, member pricing, tech comes to you. Serving Toorak, Brighton, Bayside.

**Headline (22 chars):**
> Priority. Every Month.

**Description (27 chars):**
> Book your membership today.

**Why this works:** Appeals to the time-poor professional who hates admin. Drops three key Melbourne suburb names — that specificity signals to the right buyer that this is meant for them, not everyone.

---

### Variant 3 — Local Expertise / Environmental Threat (Angle: Melbourne's inner east is hard on paint)

**Primary text (102 chars):**
> Melbourne's inner east is hard on paint — UV, gravel, café parks. Membership keeps your car protected.

**Headline (21 chars):**
> Covered. Every Month.

**Description (21 chars):**
> See membership plans.

**Why this works:** Hyper-local. "Café parks" is a real inner-east problem — South Yarra, Richmond, Hawthorn. Car owners who parallel park outside Lune or Tipo 00 know exactly what this means. Contrarian tone (acknowledges risk rather than claiming immunity) aligns with the brand voice.

### Interests & Behaviours
- Luxury vehicle ownership: BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi, Porsche, Tesla, Toyota Land Cruiser
- Engaged shoppers — home services / subscription services
- Interests: car detailing, car care, automotive accessories
- Behaviours: frequent travellers (proxy for high income), affluent postcode clustering

### Lookalike Audiences
- **Seed:** Upload current membership email list + `/membership` page converters
- **Minimum seed:** 500 real customers for a quality 1–3% lookalike
- **Stack:** Run 1% lookalike (quality) and 3% lookalike (scale) as separate ad sets with split budget
- **Exclude:** Current members (suppress from acquisition campaigns; retarget separately for upgrades)

## Retargeting — /booking Page Abandoners

For users who visited `/booking` but did not convert (window: 7 days):

**Primary text (113 chars):**
> Still deciding? Members get priority booking and discounted service — no lock-in contract. Start this month.

**Headline (20 chars):**
> Your Spot Won't Wait

**Description (21 chars):**
> Join. Cancel anytime.

**Targeting window:** 7 days post-visit (hot intent)
**Frequency cap:** 3× per week — these are warm leads; don't burn them
**Angle:** Objection handling — "no lock-in" directly addresses commitment hesitation. "Your spot won't wait" adds soft urgency without countdown tactics.

## Creative Direction Notes

**Image/video guidance:**
- Show a technician arriving at a Toorak or Brighton driveway — context matters (European car, leafy street)
- Before/after paint comparison performs well on feed (swipe format on Instagram Carousel)
- Avoid studio/white background — mobile, outdoor, lifestyle context converts better for this audience
- Member card or app mockup can work for the subscription angle (visualises the product)

**Format priority:** Instagram Feed > Facebook Feed > Instagram Stories (test in that order)

**Copy test plan (weeks 1–2):**
- Run V1 and V3 head-to-head (both local/environmental angle, different hooks)
- V2 as challenger (identity angle)
- Kill the lowest CTR at 1,000+ impressions; scale the winner with fresh creative on the same angle
