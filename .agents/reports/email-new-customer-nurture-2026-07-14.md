**Keep it dry if you can.** Not "dry" as in you can't drive it — you can. Just avoid putting it through a car wash or hosing it down until day 7. A light Melbourne shower is fine.

## Email 2 of 4 — Day 7

```
Send:    7 days after service completion
Subject: Your coating just survived its first Melbourne week
Preview: July is one of the harder months for paint. Here's what to watch now.
```

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Seven days in. If you've had any rain on the car, you've probably already seen what a cured coating does: water beads up and rolls straight off the paint instead of sitting.

That's not just visual. Water sitting on paint in winter carries dissolved minerals and pollution from Melbourne air that builds up in layers — you don't see it until you're looking at haze on a dark car in direct light. The coating deflects most of that.

**What July specifically does to paint**

Melbourne's coldest month comes with a few things worth knowing about:

*Cold mornings and condensation.* Cars parked undercover — garage, carport, apartment basement — pick up moisture from the overnight temperature drop. On unprotected paint, that moisture sits in micro-imperfections in the clear coat. On your coating, it runs off. Small difference daily, significant difference over a winter.

*Road contamination.* The Eastern Freeway and Monash pick up oil, rubber, and road spray that becomes a fine mist behind trucks in the rain. A rinse after a long highway run is worth 5 minutes of your time.

*Bare winter trees.* The tree above your usual parking spot looks clean in July. It isn't. Sap, bird droppings, and debris are still dropping — no leaves to intercept them. Birds that shelter in canopy trees are more concentrated in winter, and their droppings are acidic enough to start etching unprotected paint within hours. Your coating gives you a meaningful buffer, but don't leave it sitting overnight if you can help it.

**The one maintenance habit that matters most right now**

A hand wash every 2–3 weeks keeps the coating performing properly. Use a pH-neutral car shampoo — standard dish soap strips the hydrophobic layer over time, and most people don't notice until the water stops beading. Touchless auto washes are acceptable. Brush washes are not, ever.

We'll check in again at the three-week mark.

— Tom

**Personalise the car make.** If the CRM captures vehicle make/model, use it in Email 1 and Email 4.
"Your 3 Series" lands differently than "your car." This is the single highest-leverage personalisation in the sequence.

**Sender name.** Send from a person — ideally the technician who did the job if that's trackable in your system.
"Tom from Pristine Detailers" gets meaningfully better open rates than "Pristine Detailers".

**July vs. June distinctions used in this version:**
- June version led on rain and early-winter condensation. July version leads on dry cold, road contamination,
  winter tree coverage, and overnight frost in outer suburbs (Doncaster, Ringwood, Berwick).
- Membership pitch in this version uses a real-scenario hook (the M3 story) rather than a feature list,
  because by July the feature list approach has already been in market for two months.
