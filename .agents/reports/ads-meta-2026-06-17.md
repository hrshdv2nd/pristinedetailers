# Meta Ads — Ceramic Coating
**Date:** 2026-06-17 (Wednesday mid-week run — launch Thursday 2026-06-18)
**Week:** 25 — Rotation: Ceramic Coating (weeks 1 & 5 in the 8-week cycle; week 25 mod 4 = 1)
**Platforms:** Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed
**Campaign objective:** Lead generation / landing page conversions
**Landing page:** `/booking` (service pre-selected: Ceramic Coating)
**Target location:** Melbourne, VIC — Toorak, South Yarra, Brighton, Bayside, Hawthorn, Mornington Peninsula

## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Problem-Agitate-Solve (Angle: Melbourne's environment is actively working against your paint)

**Primary text (116 chars):**
> Melbourne UV. Toorak bird drops. Eastlink chips. Ceramic bonds to your clear coat — stops all three. We come to you.

**Headline (26 chars):**
> Shield Your Paint. Mobile.

**Description (25 chars):**
> We come to you. Book now.

**Why this works:** Opens with three specific, recognisable Melbourne paint threats — no preamble. The Toorak or Bayside car owner knows exactly what "bird drops" and "Eastlink chips" mean for their paint. "Ceramic bonds to your clear coat" is technical enough to signal expertise without requiring explanation. "Stops all three" creates a satisfying resolution rhythm. "We come to you" delivers the differentiator at the end, where it lands with contrast — by the time you reach it, the reader understands what they'd normally have to do. No exclamation marks. No superlatives. Reads like a technician, not a salesperson.

**Seasonal note:** The three threats named are all present in June — Melbourne winter UV is still moderate (UVI 2–3), inner-east bird activity peaks in cooler months, and Eastlink/M1 stone chip risk is year-round. This ad doesn't need seasonal modification. Reassess heading into August if UV angle needs updating.

### Variant 2 — Before/After (Angle: Ceramic coating protects your car's resale value)

**Primary text (111 chars):**
> Your BMW's resale starts with the paint. Ceramic coating protects it for 8 years. Mobile — we come to Brighton.

**Headline (25 chars):**
> Protect Resale Value Now.

**Description (26 chars):**
> Certified techs. Book now.

**Why this works:** "Your BMW's resale starts with the paint" is a financial reframe — this isn't cosmetic vanity, it's asset protection. The ICP in Brighton and Toorak is upgrading vehicles every 2–4 years; ceramic coating is a rational investment in that cycle, not a luxury add-on. "8 years" anchors the value proposition: a single coating could outlast their current ownership period. "Mobile — we come to Brighton" names a specific suburb after the benefit has been established — the reader has already accepted the value argument before the convenience point lands. Run with BMW as the named vehicle, then test Audi, Porsche, and Tesla as vehicle-specific variants against the corresponding interest audience. Don't use "luxury car" — name the specific model the audience drives.

**Vehicle test plan:** Create four versions of V2:
- V2a: "Your BMW's resale…" — target BMW interest group
- V2b: "Your Audi's resale…" — target Audi interest group  
- V2c: "Your Porsche's resale…" — target Porsche interest group
- V2d: "Your Tesla's resale…" — target Tesla interest group

The suburb reference ("we come to Brighton") should also be rotated: Brighton for Bayside ad set, Toorak for inner-east ad set, Portsea for Mornington Peninsula ad set.

### Variant 3 — Identity (Angle: Premium Melbourne suburbs deserve a paint-protection service that comes to them)

**Primary text (112 chars):**
> Toorak driveways. Brighton garages. South Yarra car parks. Pristine's ceramic coating goes where your car lives.

**Headline (26 chars):**
> Melbourne's Detail Experts

**Description (27 chars):**
> Mobile. No workshop needed.

**Why this works:** Lists three real Melbourne residential contexts — driveway, garage, car park. The reader sees their own situation reflected back at them without being told what to do. "Goes where your car lives" closes with a rhythm that makes the mobile model feel like a feature designed for them, not a compromise. No discount mention, no urgency tactics — this is identity advertising. The Toorak/Brighton buyer doesn't respond to countdown timers. They respond to recognition that their time, their car, and their suburb have been considered. "Melbourne's Detail Experts" in the headline asserts authority without explaining it — appropriate for an audience that researches before they book.

## Retargeting — /booking Page Abandoners

For users who visited `/booking` but did not complete a booking (window: 7 days):

**Primary text (109 chars):**
> You looked at ceramic coating. Your driveway is still a perfectly good studio. We're taking bookings this week.

**Headline (22 chars):**
> Still Taking Bookings.

**Description (24 chars):**
> Takes 2 minutes to book.

**Targeting window:** 7 days post-visit (hot, high-intent)
**Frequency cap:** 3× per week maximum — these are warm leads, not cold audience
**Exclusions:** Anyone who completed a booking after the visit (suppress converters immediately)

**Why this works:** "You looked at ceramic coating. Good call." validates their interest — the original version — but this iteration ("Your driveway is still a perfectly good studio") does something more specific: it removes the imaginary friction of needing a workshop or studio space, which is one of the most common mental barriers for buyers who haven't used a mobile service before. "We're taking bookings this week" introduces mild temporal availability without manufactured urgency. "Takes 2 minutes to book" directly addresses the friction that most likely caused the abandonment — they got to the booking form, saw it required time or decisions, and left. Two minutes is the antidote to that hesitation.

## Creative Direction Notes

**Image/video guidance:**
- Hero visual: certified technician applying ceramic at a leafy Toorak or Brighton driveway — European vehicle in the foreground, morning light, residential context unmistakably Melbourne inner suburbs
- The application process itself is high-performing creative: gloved hand buffing ceramic into clear coat, the gloss reveal as the product cures — process credibility builds trust before the CTA
- Water bead demonstration: a slow-motion shot of water sheeting off a freshly-coated bonnet is the most shareable, platform-native ceramic coating visual — works strongly for Instagram Reels
- Before/after carousel: half panel shows orange peel/light swirling visible in angled light; half panel shows deep gloss post-ceramic — contrast is the hook, caption does the rest
- Avoid stock images of anonymous cars in sterile studio environments — mobile, residential, Melbourne-specific context converts better for this audience

**Format priority for ceramic coating:**
1. Instagram Feed (static or carousel) — primary; highest CPM but best targeting precision for this audience
2. Facebook Feed — secondary; broader reach, useful for prospecting lookalike audiences
3. Instagram Reels — water bead video only; native format for a visually dramatic process
4. Stories — retargeting only; lower production threshold, good for the /booking abandoner message

**Copy test plan (weeks 1–2):**
- Run V1 (environment/PAS) and V3 (identity/suburb-specific) head-to-head as cold audience — both are awareness-to-consideration plays
- V2 (resale/BAB) with vehicle-specific variants as a challenger — tests whether financial framing converts cold ceramic buyers
- Retargeting runs independently; don't include it in the cold audience test budget
- Kill the lowest-CTR cold variant at 1,000 impressions; scale the winner with fresh visual creative on the same copy angle
- If V2 vehicle variants show >20% CTR difference between BMW and Tesla, split the Bayside and Toorak ad sets to serve vehicle-matched creative by postcode income proxy

**Copy angle to test in week 3 (if budget allows):**
> "Washed and waxed every weekend. Still fading. Ceramic coating is a different category — it bonds to the paint, not just sits on it. We apply it at your driveway."

This angle targets the informed buyer who has tried premium wax products and is ready for the next level. Length exceeds feed preview but is suitable for a longer-form placement test (Facebook right-column or article placement).
