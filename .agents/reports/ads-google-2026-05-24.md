Three angles: (1) physics/inevitability of chips on Melbourne freeways, (2) mobile application — no workshop drop-off, (3) certified vs. dealer package trust gap.

## Exact-Match Keywords (5)

```
[paint protection film Melbourne]
[PPF Melbourne]
[mobile PPF installation Melbourne]
[paint protection film Toorak]
[car paint protection Melbourne]
```

**Rationale:**
- `[paint protection film Melbourne]` — primary volume term; this searcher knows what PPF is and is looking for a Melbourne provider; highest commercial intent in the PPF category
- `[PPF Melbourne]` — shorter abbreviation used by buyers who have already researched; if they're using the abbreviation, they're past the education phase and in the decision phase; lower CPCs than the full-phrase equivalent with comparable conversion intent
- `[mobile PPF installation Melbourne]` — captures the subset of searchers who have already decided they want mobile application, not a studio; these buyers are pre-qualified on the key differentiator; likely to convert faster and at lower CPA
- `[paint protection film Toorak]` — geo-intent modifier; Toorak (3142) sits on the Monash Freeway route and has high concentration of European prestige vehicles; searchers adding a suburb modifier are demonstrating serious purchase intent — they're not researching broadly, they're choosing a provider in their area
- `[car paint protection Melbourne]` — broader category term that captures buyers comparing PPF vs. ceramic coating vs. other protection options; serve these searchers H2 ("Stop Stone Chips at Source") and D2 (physics description) to move them from category-aware to PPF-specific intent

**Note on overlap with Week 21 set:** The Week 21 ceramic coating campaign runs `[car paint protection melbourne]` as a broad match modified term — pause or exclude that overlap in the PPF campaign by adding [ceramic coating] as a negative, or separate the PPF and ceramic campaigns into distinct ad groups with mutual negatives.

## Negative Keywords (3)

```
-PPF DIY kit
-vinyl wrap
-window tint
```

**Rationale:**
- `-PPF DIY kit` — DIY PPF kits (XPEL, SunTek, Llumar consumer versions) are a distinct product category; searchers buying DIY kits are not buying professional installation; excluding prevents budget waste on non-converting clicks and protects Quality Score
- `-vinyl wrap` — vinyl wrap is a cosmetic product (colour change, aesthetics) rather than a protection product; buyers searching for vinyl wraps are optimising for appearance, not stone chip protection; completely different purchase intent and LTV profile — a vinyl wrap buyer is unlikely to convert to a $2,000–$5,000 PPF installation
- `-window tint` — window tint shares some keywords ("car film", "paint film", "protective film") and is a common search adjacent to PPF; a tint buyer is looking for a $200–$400 service, not a $2,000+ full-body wrap; excluding keeps the campaign audience qualified and CPA efficient

### Device & Schedule
- Run Monday–Saturday; suppress Sunday (low booking conversion, budget recharges overnight)
- +15% bid adjustment for mobile devices on Saturday 9am–1pm — car owners are home, in the driveway, and often notice paint damage on weekend mornings
- +10% bid adjustment for weekday evenings 6–9pm — after-work search window, same behaviour as the ceramic coating set

### Bid Strategy
- Start on Maximise Conversions (no target CPA) for the first two weeks to build data
- Switch to Target CPA after 30+ conversions have been recorded; set initial target at 1.3× the actual CPA from the Maximise Conversions phase
- Do not set a Target ROAS — PPF jobs vary significantly in ticket size (partial vs. full body); CPA targeting against a booking conversion is more stable
