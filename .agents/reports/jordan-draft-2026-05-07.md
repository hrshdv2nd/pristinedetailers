## Article Body

## The 10-minute rule isn't a myth

You park under a tree on Chapel Street, grab your coffee, come back 15 minutes later to a fresh deposit on your bonnet. You think: *I'll wash it at the weekend.* That's the mistake.

Bird droppings begin damaging your paint within minutes of contact — and by the time they've dried hard in Melbourne's summer heat, the damage is already done. Here's why, and what to do instead.

![Car being detailed — act fast before droppings harden into your clear coat](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1617469767824-e76ad64e9f86?w=1200&q=80)

## What's actually in bird droppings

Bird droppings aren't just unpleasant — they're chemically aggressive. Unlike mammals, birds excrete uric acid as their primary waste product. Uric acid sits at a pH of around 3.5 to 4.5. Your car's clear coat is designed to handle mild environmental exposure, but uric acid at that concentration is in the same territory as vinegar and citrus juice — corrosive enough to begin breaking down the clear coat's surface on contact.

There's a second factor most people don't know about: as the dropping dries, the uric acid crystals solidify and physically bond to the clear coat. When the sun heats the panel, the deposit contracts as it dries — and it pulls the softened clear coat with it. The result is a textured etch mark: the precise outline of the dropping, pressed permanently into your paint. Detailers call this sun printing.

On a 30-degree Melbourne summer day — which you'll see in Toorak from November through March — this process takes less than 10 minutes. In direct sun on a black bonnet, less than five.

## Why location makes it worse in Melbourne

Melbourne's bird population is not evenly distributed. If you park regularly under the Moreton Bay figs on St Kilda Road, near the waterfront at Port Melbourne, or in any tree-lined street in Fitzroy, Hawthorn, or Carlton, you're in high-risk territory. Sulphur-crested cockatoos, ibis, and silver gulls are particularly prolific — and seagull droppings from the bay tend to be more liquid, which means they spread further across the panel and penetrate faster.

Compound that with Melbourne's UV intensity. Victoria sits in a zone where UV radiation is disproportionately high relative to its latitude, partly due to ozone depletion over the Southern Hemisphere. UV softens clear coat, making it more susceptible to acid attack. A dropping that causes minimal damage on a cloudy winter morning can etch deeply on a clear February afternoon.

![Paint protection close-up — clear coat under UV and acid attack is more vulnerable than you think](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1604754742629-3e5728249d73?w=1200&q=80)

## What to do when you find one

The worst thing you can do is wipe it off dry. A dried dropping contains hardened uric acid crystals and grit — dragging that across your clear coat leaves fine scratches visible in direct light. If you've ever noticed a circular scratch pattern around a cleaned-off dropping, that's the cause.

Here's the correct process:

**1. Soak it first.** Pour water directly onto the dropping — or use a detail spray if you keep one in the car. Let it sit for 30–60 seconds to rehydrate the material.

**2. Blot, don't wipe.** Use a clean microfibre cloth and press gently to lift the deposit rather than drag it.

**3. Rinse the area.** A splash of clean water to remove any remaining residue.

**4. Check for etching in direct sunlight.** Look at the panel from a low angle. A dull or textured patch where the dropping sat means the clear coat has been etched.

If there's etching, don't wait. A light etch caught early can often be corrected with a machine polish before it deepens. Left alone — or repeatedly baked in the sun — it becomes a permanent mark in your paint.

## How ceramic coatings change the equation

A ceramic coating doesn't make your car immune to bird droppings. Nothing does. But it changes the timeline significantly. The coating acts as a sacrificial chemical layer between the dropping and your clear coat. The uric acid attacks the coating first, which buys you substantially more time before any real damage occurs.

Clients with a properly applied ceramic coating typically have a window of 30–60 minutes rather than 5–10, even in direct sun. The hydrophobic surface also means droppings don't bond as readily — they're easier to remove cleanly without dragging.

![Ceramic coating application — building a sacrificial layer over your clear coat](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1619767886558-efdc259cde1a?w=1200&q=80)

## If the damage is already done

Etch marks from old droppings aren't necessarily permanent. Depending on depth, a single or two-stage paint correction can restore the surface. But the window for easy correction closes fast — once the etch reaches the base coat, the panel needs a respray.

The practical takeaway: carry a small detail spray and a microfibre cloth in your car. Dealing with a fresh dropping correctly takes about 60 seconds. Correcting the damage from one you left over the weekend takes a half-day appointment and several hundred dollars. The maths on that one is easy.
