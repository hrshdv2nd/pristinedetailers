**Pin guidance:**
- Pin H1 ("Ceramic Coating Melbourne") to Position 1 — exact-match keyword; captures the buyer who has researched ceramic coating and is now selecting a provider
- H2 and H3 rotate in Position 2 — H2 ("Mobile Ceramic. We Come To You") removes the workshop drop-off objection upfront; H3 ("8-Year Ceramic Warranty") speaks to the buyer evaluating quality and longevity, who has likely already read that warranties vary from brand to brand
- H4 and H5 rotate in Position 3 — H4 ("Melbourne Roads Destroy Paint") is a provocative pain-point opener for searchers arriving via broader terms like "car paint protection Melbourne"; H5 closes on a low-friction action prompt

**Why H4 matters:** "Melbourne Roads Destroy Paint" is geographically specific and confrontational — the Eastern Freeway stone chip problem, the grit from the Mornington Peninsula, the salt-spray near the bay. A car owner in Toorak who commutes to the CBD and parks in an open-air car park on Collins Street knows exactly what this sentence means. It validates the anxiety that brings them to search, before they've read a word of ad copy.

**Why H2 works for this service in particular:** Ceramic coating is a professional application requiring prep, decontamination, and a controlled environment. The instinct for buyers researching this service is to assume it requires a workshop. "We Come To You" directly corrects that assumption in the headline slot — before they read any description copy.

**Rationale:**

- `[ceramic car protection Melbourne]` — problem-solution searchers who may not know the exact product name; captures buyers who've read about paint protection and are still deciding between PPF and ceramic; landing page should address the choice
- `[nano ceramic coating Melbourne]` — technical-aware buyer; has researched product chemistry; knows what they're asking for; strong signal of an informed, high-intent prospect
- `[ceramic coating Toorak]` — hyper-local, low volume but extremely high conversion intent; a Toorak resident searching for a suburb-specific coating service is as warm as the ICP gets; bid competitively

---

### Negative Keywords — 3 to Exclude

```
[ceramic tinting Melbourne]
[DIY ceramic coating kit]
[scratch repair Melbourne]
```

**Rationale:**
- `[ceramic tinting Melbourne]` — window tinting buyers; "ceramic" tint is a high-performance window film, not paint coating; completely different product, different tradesperson category; will surface in broad queries if not excluded
- `[DIY ceramic coating kit]` — product buyer, not service buyer; price sensitivity signal; wrong channel for Pristine's $750+ professional application; also flags buyers who may have applied a consumer-grade coating and are searching for a top-up, which creates a pre-existing product conflict on the car
- `[scratch repair Melbourne]` — buyer has structural paint damage (deep scratch, rock chip) requiring a panel beater or touch-up specialist, not coating prep; coating over unrepaired scratches seals in the damage; this query population will create poor post-service outcomes and review risk

Buyers who notice the degradation in winter light — particularly in the low-angle morning sun that shows swirl marks clearly — are motivated to act before summer UV arrives. - **Upsell narrative from week 28:** A meaningful share of this week's impressions will come from buyers who saw last week's paint correction ads.
