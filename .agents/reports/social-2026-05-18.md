**Caption:**

There's a widespread misconception that a ceramic coating means you stop maintaining your car. It doesn't — it means your maintenance is more effective, and less frequent. That's a meaningful difference worth understanding.

A coating chemically bonds to your clear coat and creates a hydrophobic barrier against UV radiation, bird droppings, water spots, and road contamination. But contamination still accumulates. Iron fallout from brake dust, acidic organic matter, tree sap — these degrade the coating's surface layer if left unattended. A professional maintenance wash every 4–6 weeks removes them before they penetrate and compromise the bond.

For business owners and fleet managers with coated vehicles, the timeline matters. A neglected ceramic coating starts losing performance at 18–24 months. A properly maintained one holds its properties for 5–8 years. The difference isn't the product — it's the follow-through.
