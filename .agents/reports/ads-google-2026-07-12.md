## Responsive Search Ad — Ceramic Coating Melbourne

### Headlines (30 chars max)

| # | Headline | Chars | Angle |
|---|----------|-------|-------|
| H1 | Ceramic Coating Melbourne | 25 | Primary keyword match |
| H2 | 8-Year Warranty. Mobile. | 24 | Differentiator — warranty length + no drop-off |
| H3 | Nano Ceramic — No Drop-Off | 26 | Product mechanism + logistical hook |
| H4 | Ceramic Coating From $750 | 25 | Price anchor — qualifies the right buyer |
| H5 | Book Ceramic Coating Online | 27 | CTA headline — direct conversion intent |

**Pin guidance:**
- Pin H1 ("Ceramic Coating Melbourne") to Position 1 — exact keyword match; identifies Pristine immediately for solution-aware searchers comparing providers
- H2 and H3 rotate in Position 2 — H2 leads with the 8-year warranty, which signals manufacturer-backed product rather than trade-fair DIY coating; H3 speaks to the buyer who's researched the product and knows what nano-ceramic means, but hasn't yet committed to a provider because they're dreading the workshop logistics
- H4 and H5 rotate in Position 3 — H4 filters for the right buyer (someone who sees $750 and doesn't leave is exactly the ICP); H5 drives the click-to-book action and works in any combination since it stands alone as a clear CTA

**Why H2 matters:** The 8-year warranty claim is a genuine differentiator and a rare one in search results — most mobile operators either don't carry manufacturer-backed product lines or don't advertise the warranty term. It does two things: signals credibility and sets Pristine apart from generic "ceramic coating Melbourne" results. It also does quiet objection handling — "is this as good as a studio?" becomes "no studio can give me an 8-year guarantee on a mobile service; they must know what they're doing."

**Why H3 matters:** "No Drop-Off" in a search context directly addresses the primary friction point for the ICP — time-poor professionals who want studio quality without the logistics of leaving a Porsche or Range Rover at a workshop in a Dandenong industrial estate. This headline intercepts searchers who are evaluating fixed studios vs. mobile alternatives.

## Keyword Recommendations

### Exact Match — 5 Keywords

```
[ceramic coating Melbourne]
[mobile ceramic coating Melbourne]
[ceramic coating Toorak]
[nano ceramic coating Melbourne]
[ceramic coating before paint protection film Melbourne]
```

**Rationale:**
- `[ceramic coating Melbourne]` — primary high-intent term; buyer is solution-aware and comparing providers; highest volume and priority bid
- `[mobile ceramic coating Melbourne]` — explicitly mobile-intent; this buyer has already decided they don't want to drop off the car; conversion intent is high and the message-to-market match is exact
- `[ceramic coating Toorak]` — hyper-local; a Toorak or South Yarra owner searching with their suburb name is confident in the service and filtering for someone who knows their area; low volume, very high conversion rate
- `[nano ceramic coating Melbourne]` — product-literate searcher; already understands the technology; will be harder to convert with generic copy, very easy to convert with technical proof and warranty language; low waste
- `[ceramic coating before paint protection film Melbourne]` — long-tail, high intent; buyer is sequencing a ceramic + PPF treatment and needs expert guidance on which order; sets up a natural upsell into the Revitalise package or a PPF-first consultation

---

### Negative Keywords — 3 to Exclude

```
[ceramic coating kit]
[how to apply ceramic coating]
[ceramic tint Melbourne]
```

**Rationale:**
- `[ceramic coating kit]` — DIY buyer searching for a product to apply themselves; zero service intent; will arrive on the landing page, see service pricing, and exit immediately; burns budget with no conversion path
- `[how to apply ceramic coating]` — informational/tutorial intent; this person is learning the technique, not booking a service; likely a detailing hobbyist or small competitor; exclude from all campaigns
- `[ceramic tint Melbourne]` — window tinting is a completely different product category; the word "ceramic" appears but the buyer wants glass tint, not paint coating; will generate clicks, zero relevance, guaranteed zero conversions

## Context Notes

- **Melbourne-specific hooks used:** Toorak named in D1 (suburb anchor); Bayside in D1 (geographic range signal); "bird damage" references the real Melbourne problem of sulphur-crested cockatoos and Indian mynas — beak gouges and droppings that etch in 24 hours in direct sun; "UV" references Port Phillip Bay UV levels that degrade clear coat faster than Melbourne's temperate climate suggests
- **Tone check:** Technical without being inaccessible — "chemical bond with your clear coat", "manufacturer-backed", "nano ceramic" used accurately; "From $750" is direct and filters correctly; no vague superlatives; no exclamation marks
- **Seasonal relevance:** Mid-winter in Melbourne means condensation, road salt, and spray debris from wet-weather freeway driving, but it is also the quietest period for paint correction bookings — which means Pristine can offer faster turnaround windows and should lean into this in the landing page copy ("book now, faster install slots available in July–August"). Winter UV is still present in Melbourne; UV protection messaging remains valid year-round
- **Upsell path:** Ceramic coating is the natural follow-on to paint correction (week 28) — buyers who saw those ads and didn't convert are now your warmest ceramic prospects. Ensure the ceramic landing page cross-references the Revitalise + Ceramic Coating combined package. Average order value lift is significant when both are booked together
- **Price positioning:** $750 for a ceramic coating that protects a $100K–$200K car for up to 8 years is a straightforward value equation. The objection is not price; it's trust (mobile vs. fixed studio) and awareness (not all buyers know what ceramic coating actually does). Address both on the landing page — mechanism explanation + testimonials + warranty statement
