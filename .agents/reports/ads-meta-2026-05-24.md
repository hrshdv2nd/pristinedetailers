# Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) — Paint Protection Film Focus (Week 22 Prep)
**Date Produced:** 2026-05-24 (Sunday — prepared for Thursday 28 May activation)
**Week:** 22 | **Service Focus:** Paint Protection Film (Week 22 rotation — mod 4 = 2)
**Platform:** Meta — Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed + Stories
**Campaign:** META_Conv_Feed_PPF_MelbInnerEast-Bayside_2026W22
**Landing Page:** /booking

**Context:** PPF is the most visually demonstrable service in the Pristine portfolio — a stone chip landing on unprotected paint versus film absorbing the impact is a story you can show, not just tell. Where the Week 21 ceramic coating Meta set leaned on seasonal threats (autumn sap, bird droppings) and chemistry credibility, PPF creative should lean on the physics of impact and the emotional weight of a fresh car being damaged. Three angles: (1) the inevitability angle — stone chips are physics on Melbourne freeways, not bad luck; (2) the new-car buyer angle — the window to protect a car is day one, not after the first chip; (3) the dealer-alternative angle — dealership protection packages are a margin play, Pristine is the expert. No prior Meta PPF creative this cycle — zero frequency fatigue.

## Ad Variants

### Variant 1 — Angle: Physics / Inevitability (Freeway)

**Primary Text (105 chars):**
> Stone chips aren't bad luck — they're physics. PPF stops them at the surface. We install it at your home.

**Headline (25 chars):**
> Stop Stone Chips With PPF

**Description (27 chars):**
> We install it at your home.

**Char counts:** Primary 105 ✓ | Headline 25 ✓ | Description 27 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- Opens with the brand voice line from the product context ("stone chips aren't bad luck — they're physics") applied directly to PPF; this is a verbatim credibility signal — Pristine talks like a technician, not a marketer
- "At the surface" is technically precise: PPF absorbs kinetic energy before it reaches the clear coat; buyers who have researched PPF recognise this as accurate; it earns trust without a badge or testimonial
- "We install it at your home" is the close — it converts a problem statement into a service offer in six words
- Best format: 15-second Reel showing a high-speed camera clip of a stone striking film (water bead impact simulation is widely used) followed by a cut to a Pristine technician applying film at a Toorak driveway; caption the first two lines of primary text; no voiceover required
- Also works as a Facebook Feed static with a close-up of PPF film on a front bumper and the headline as a text overlay on the lower third
- Run on cold audiences in the Inner East and Bayside postcode clusters; highest potential for reach efficiency because the physics angle is universally understood by the ICP — no prior knowledge of PPF required

### Variant 2 — Angle: New Car Buyer / Day One Protection

**Primary Text (107 chars):**
> Just picked up the BMW. The Mornington Freeway will chip it by next week. PPF on day one, at your driveway.

**Headline (26 chars):**
> Protect It Before It Chips

**Description (27 chars):**
> Mobile install. Book today.

**Char counts:** Primary 107 ✓ | Headline 26 ✓ | Description 27 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- "Just picked up the BMW" is the hook — it names the exact vehicle and the exact emotional state (new purchase pride); any buyer who just collected a prestige vehicle will stop scrolling at this line because it's them
- "The Mornington Freeway will chip it by next week" — Melbourne-specific, specific road, specific timeline; this is not abstract risk, this is a date on a calendar; the Mornington Peninsula Freeway is the most chip-prone route for the Bayside/Peninsula ICP (weekend drives, limestone grit on the road surface)
- "PPF on day one, at your driveway" — the call to action is embedded in the problem statement; by the time they've read to this point, the solution is already stated
- Do not replace "BMW" — it is intentional; swap to "Porsche," "Mercedes," or "Tesla" to create three micro-variants for A/B testing; one vehicle per ad; the specificity of naming a car model outperforms "your car" by a significant margin with the 35–55 prestige vehicle ICP
- Best format: Instagram Feed square with a pristine (pun intended) black BMW on a Melbourne street, no visible damage, Pristine van in the background; the contrast between the perfect car and the implied threat is the visual tension that holds attention
- Target this variant to the 28–45 age bracket with interest in BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Porsche, or Tesla owners; also effective as a LAL audience based on past PPF customers

**Headline (27 chars):**

**Description (27 chars):**

**Char counts:** Primary 119 ✓ | Headline 27 ✓ | Description 27 ✓

**Placement Notes:**
- Opens with a direct comparison that doesn't name a competitor — "dealership protection packages" is universally understood by anyone who has bought a car in the last five years; the buyer has either been offered one or knows someone who was
- "One lasts 10+ years, one earns them a margin" — the asymmetry is the point; Pristine's PPF is a 10-year product, the dealership's package is a revenue line; framing it this way respects the buyer's intelligence without making Pristine sound defensive

- This variant speaks to buyers in the consideration phase (they've been offered a dealer package, they're comparing); target it to the 35–55 bracket with recent car purchase signals and luxury vehicle interests
- Also run as a retargeting creative for /services or /journal visitors who have not yet booked — they are researching and this variant answers the "is it worth it vs. the dealer?" question directly

- **LAL size:** 1% VIC for prospecting; expand to 2% only if CPM exceeds $14 AUD at 1%
- **Exclude from all ad sets:** Active Pristine members, anyone who completed a /booking conversion in the last 30 days, bounce visitors (<10 seconds on site)

- **Mornington Peninsula test:** Allocate $20–30 AUD/day flat to Variant 2 in the Peninsula ad set; measure CPM and CTR against the Inner East/Bayside benchmarks after 5 days

**Visual Direction (all variants):**
Real Melbourne settings only — no studio, no generic backgrounds. Vehicle: European prestige (black BMW 3-Series, white Porsche Cayenne, midnight blue Mercedes E-Class) in a residential driveway or street-level car park. For PPF-specific creative, the hero shot is close-up film installation — a technician's gloved hands and a squeegee on a front bumper, film visible at the edge. Water-bead shots work for ceramic (hydrophobic finish) but are less distinctive for PPF — instead use a macro shot of the film surface showing its depth and clarity, or the installation process itself. Autumn light (overcast, mid-morning) is the Melbourne standard and avoids harsh shadows on film surfaces.
